### Q1. Short note: SGLT2 inhibitors (5m)
<!-- id: sys13-q1 -->

**Restated:** Write a short note on SGLT2 inhibitors (gliflozins).

- **Definition / mechanism (1½m):** **sodium–glucose co-transporter-2 inhibitors** (empagliflozin,
  dapagliflozin, canagliflozin) block glucose reabsorption in the **proximal renal tubule** → glycosuria →
  lower blood glucose **independent of insulin**; additional natriuretic/diuretic and cardiorenal
  haemodynamic benefits. (1½m)
- **Key indications / benefits (2m):** type 2 diabetes (HbA1c lowering, weight loss, mild BP fall);
  **proven cardiovascular & renal protection** — reduce **heart-failure** hospitalisation (benefit in HFrEF
  **and** HFpEF, even in non-diabetics) and slow **chronic kidney disease** progression. Now part of
  first-line therapy in diabetics with CV/renal disease or heart failure. (2m)
- **Adverse effects / cautions (1m):** **genital mycotic infections / UTI**, volume depletion;
  **euglycaemic diabetic ketoacidosis** (withhold during acute illness/surgery — "sick-day rule"); rare
  Fournier's gangrene; avoid starting at very low eGFR for glycaemic effect. (1m)
- **Pearl (½m):** the cardiorenal benefit, not just glucose lowering, is why these drugs have entered
  **heart-failure and CKD** guidelines independent of diabetes status. (½m)

### Q2. Short note: GLP-1 receptor agonists (5m)
<!-- id: sys13-q2 -->

**Restated:** Write a short note on GLP-1 receptor agonists.

- **Definition / mechanism (1½m):** **glucagon-like peptide-1 receptor agonists** (semaglutide, liraglutide,
  dulaglutide; **tirzepatide** = dual GIP/GLP-1 agonist) are **incretin mimetics** — they augment
  **glucose-dependent insulin secretion**, suppress glucagon, **slow gastric emptying** and promote satiety.
  (1½m)
- **Key indications / benefits (2m):** type 2 diabetes with strong **HbA1c lowering** and substantial
  **weight loss**; **cardiovascular benefit** (reduced MACE) in high-risk diabetics; semaglutide/liraglutide
  approved for **obesity**; low intrinsic hypoglycaemia risk (glucose-dependent action). Mostly weekly
  **subcutaneous** injection; oral semaglutide available. (2m)
- **Adverse effects / cautions (1m):** **GI** — nausea, vomiting, diarrhoea (dose-titrate); pancreatitis
  (rare); contraindicated with personal/family history of **medullary thyroid carcinoma / MEN-2**; caution
  in gastroparesis. (1m)
- **Pearl (½m):** combining strong weight loss with CV protection has moved these agents from "diabetes
  drug" to a mainstay of **cardiometabolic and obesity** management. (½m)

### Q3. Short note: Direct oral anticoagulants (DOACs) (5m)
<!-- id: sys13-q3 -->

**Restated:** Write a short note on the direct oral anticoagulants (DOACs / NOACs).

- **Definition / classes (1½m):** orally active anticoagulants acting on a **single specific clotting
  factor** — **direct thrombin (factor IIa) inhibitor: dabigatran**; **direct factor Xa inhibitors —
  rivaroxaban, apixaban, edoxaban**. Predictable effect, **no routine INR monitoring** (unlike warfarin).
  (1½m)
- **Key indications (1½m):** **non-valvular atrial fibrillation** (stroke prevention), treatment & secondary
  prevention of **DVT/pulmonary embolism**, VTE prophylaxis after major orthopaedic surgery. Generally
  preferred over warfarin in non-valvular AF (NICE/ESC). (1½m)
- **Adverse effects / cautions (1m):** **bleeding** (main risk); **avoid** in mechanical prosthetic valves
  and moderate–severe mitral stenosis (warfarin only there); dose-reduce in renal impairment (esp.
  dabigatran — renally cleared); caution with antiplatelets/NSAIDs. (1m)
- **Reversal pearl (½m):** **idarucizumab** reverses **dabigatran**; **andexanet alfa** reverses factor-Xa
  inhibitors; activated charcoal if recent ingestion. (½m)

### Q4. Short note: Biologics & targeted DMARDs (5m)
<!-- id: sys13-q4 -->

**Restated:** Write a short note on biologic and targeted-synthetic DMARDs in rheumatic disease.

- **Definition (1m):** **disease-modifying antirheumatic drugs** that target specific immune molecules/
  pathways, used when conventional DMARDs (e.g. methotrexate) fail; comprise **biologic DMARDs** (monoclonal
  antibodies / fusion proteins) and **targeted-synthetic DMARDs** (small-molecule JAK inhibitors). (1m)
- **Key classes & examples (2m):**
  - **TNF-α inhibitors** — infliximab, adalimumab, etanercept (RA, ankylosing spondylitis, IBD, psoriasis).
  - **IL-6 inhibitor** — tocilizumab; **IL-17** — secukinumab; **IL-23/12** — ustekinumab.
  - **B-cell depletion** — rituximab (anti-CD20); **T-cell co-stim block** — abatacept.
  - **JAK inhibitors** (oral) — tofacitinib, baricitinib. (2m)
- **Adverse effects / cautions (1½m):** **infection risk** — must **screen & treat latent TB and hepatitis B**
  before starting anti-TNF/biologics; reactivation of TB/HBV; injection/infusion reactions; JAK inhibitors
  carry **thromboembolism and MACE** warnings; keep vaccinations up to date (avoid live vaccines). (1½m)
- **Pearl (½m):** **latent TB screening before anti-TNF therapy** is a classic exam point — anti-TNF agents
  notably reactivate tuberculosis. (½m)

### Q5. Short note: Monoclonal antibodies & immunotherapy basics (5m)
<!-- id: sys13-q5 -->

**Restated:** Write a short note on monoclonal antibodies and the basics of immunotherapy.

- **Definition (1½m):** **monoclonal antibodies (mAbs)** are laboratory-produced antibodies of a single
  specificity directed at one target antigen/epitope; **immunotherapy** harnesses or modulates the immune
  system to treat disease (notably cancer and autoimmunity). Naming suffix **"-mab"**; types — murine
  (-omab), chimeric (-ximab), humanised (-zumab), fully human (-umab). (1½m)
- **Mechanisms / classes (2m):**
  - **Immune-checkpoint inhibitors** — anti-**PD-1** (nivolumab, pembrolizumab), anti-**PD-L1**, anti-**CTLA-4**
    (ipilimumab): release the brakes on cytotoxic T-cells against tumours.
  - **Targeted anti-tumour mAbs** — trastuzumab (HER2), rituximab (CD20), bevacizumab (VEGF).
  - **CAR-T cell therapy** — patient's T-cells re-engineered to express a chimeric antigen receptor.
  - **Anti-inflammatory mAbs** — see biologics (TNF/IL targets). (2m)
- **Adverse effects / cautions (1m):** **immune-related adverse events** with checkpoint inhibitors —
  colitis, hepatitis, pneumonitis, dermatitis, **endocrinopathies** (thyroiditis, hypophysitis); infusion
  reactions; **cytokine release syndrome** with CAR-T/bispecifics. (1m)
- **Pearl (½m):** checkpoint-inhibitor toxicity is *autoimmune* — managed with **corticosteroids/
  immunosuppression**, not by stopping the immune response pharmacologically the way infections are. (½m)

### Q6. Short note: Newer & MDR-TB drugs (5m)
<!-- id: sys13-q6 -->

**Restated:** Write a short note on newer anti-tubercular drugs and the management of drug-resistant TB.

- **Definition (1m):** **MDR-TB** = resistance to at least **isoniazid + rifampicin**; **XDR-TB** = MDR plus
  resistance to a fluoroquinolone and ≥1 additional Group A drug (current WHO definition). Newer drugs allow
  **shorter, all-oral, injectable-free** regimens. (1m)
- **Newer drugs / regimens (2½m):**
  - **Bedaquiline** — diarylquinoline; inhibits mycobacterial **ATP synthase**. Cornerstone of all-oral
    MDR-TB therapy. (1m)
  - **Delamanid / pretomanid** — nitroimidazoles inhibiting mycolic-acid synthesis. (½m)
  - **BPaL(M) regimen** — **B**edaquiline + **Pa**(pretomanid) + **L**inezolid (± **M**oxifloxacin),
    ~6 months, for MDR/pre-XDR TB (WHO-endorsed). (1m)
- **Adverse effects / monitoring (1m):** **bedaquiline → QT prolongation** (monitor ECG; avoid combining
  multiple QT-prolonging drugs); **linezolid → myelosuppression, peripheral & optic neuropathy** (monitor
  FBC and vision); baseline + serial ECG and bloods. (1m)
- **Pearl (½m):** modern programmatic management is **all-oral, shorter (6–9 months)** — the era of long
  injectable (aminoglycoside)-based regimens is being phased out. (½m)

### Q7. Short note: PCSK9 inhibitors (5m)
<!-- id: sys13-q7 -->

**Restated:** Write a short note on PCSK9 inhibitors.

- **Definition / mechanism (1½m):** monoclonal antibodies (**evolocumab, alirocumab**) against **proprotein
  convertase subtilisin/kexin type 9 (PCSK9)**. PCSK9 normally promotes degradation of hepatic **LDL
  receptors**; inhibiting it → **more LDL receptors recycled** → greater LDL-cholesterol clearance.
  **Inclisiran** is a small-interfering-RNA that suppresses PCSK9 synthesis (twice-yearly dosing). (1½m)
- **Key indications / benefits (2m):** **add-on** lipid lowering when **maximally-tolerated statin ± ezetimibe**
  is insufficient — esp. **familial hypercholesterolaemia** and very-high-risk **established ASCVD**; achieve
  large additional **LDL-C reductions (~50–60%)** and reduce cardiovascular events. Subcutaneous, every
  2–4 weeks (mAbs) or 6-monthly (inclisiran). (2m)
- **Adverse effects / cautions (1m):** generally well tolerated; **injection-site reactions**, occasional
  flu-like symptoms; **cost** limits access. (1m)
- **Pearl (½m):** they are an **add-on to statins, not a replacement** — reserved for high-risk patients not
  at LDL goal on standard oral therapy. (½m)

**What to draw / tabulate:**
- [ ] **Flowchart — LDL lowering ladder** (statin → +ezetimibe → +PCSK9 inhibitor) — ~1m

**Learn it:** Harrison's — *Disorders of Lipoprotein Metabolism*. · Davidson's — *Dyslipidaemia*. · API —

### Q8. Short note: mRNA vaccines & COVID-19 update (5m)
<!-- id: sys13-q8 -->

**Restated:** Write a short note on mRNA vaccines and the current status of COVID-19 management.

- **Definition / mechanism (1½m):** **mRNA vaccines** deliver **lipid-nanoparticle-encapsulated messenger
  RNA** encoding the SARS-CoV-2 **spike antigen**; host cells translate it, presenting the antigen to evoke
  humoral + cellular immunity. The mRNA is **non-integrating** and rapidly degraded — it does not enter the
  genome. A flexible platform (rapidly re-codable for variants; under study for other infections and
  cancers). (1½m)
- **COVID-19 update (2m):** SARS-CoV-2 is now **endemic** with ongoing variant evolution; management has
  shifted to **risk-stratified outpatient care** — early **oral antivirals (nirmatrelvir/ritonavir,
  molnupiravir)** for high-risk patients, **dexamethasone** + oxygen for those needing respiratory support,
  ± **IL-6 inhibitor (tocilizumab)/baricitinib** in severe disease; **updated (variant-adapted) booster**
  vaccination for at-risk groups. (2m)
- **Adverse effects / cautions (1m):** mRNA vaccines — local reactions, transient fever/myalgia; rare
  **myocarditis/pericarditis** (young males); rare anaphylaxis (observe post-dose). (1m)
- **Pearl (½m):** the **non-integrating, rapidly degraded** nature of mRNA is the key safety point — and the
  re-codable platform is why boosters can be updated quickly for new variants. (½m)

**What to draw / tabulate:**

### Q9. Short note: Recent evidence in heart-failure / diabetes therapy (5m)
<!-- id: sys13-q9 -->

**Restated:** Write a short note on recent advances/evidence in heart-failure and diabetes therapy.

- **Heart failure — the "four pillars" of HFrEF therapy (2m):** modern guideline-directed medical therapy
  combines, early and together: (1) **ARNI** (sacubitril/valsartan) — preferred over ACE-inhibitor/ARB;
  (2) **beta-blocker**; (3) **MRA** (spironolactone/eplerenone); (4) **SGLT2 inhibitor** (dapagliflozin/
  empagliflozin) — now standard in HFrEF **and** with benefit in HFpEF. (2m)
- **Diabetes — outcome-driven choice (1½m):** therapy is now selected by **comorbidity, not just glucose** —
  add **SGLT2 inhibitors** (heart failure / CKD) and **GLP-1 receptor agonists** (atherosclerotic CV disease,
  obesity) regardless of HbA1c when those conditions coexist; metformin remains first-line glucose control.
  (1½m)
- **Other recent points (1m):** **vericiguat** (sGC stimulator) for worsening HFrEF; **iron repletion** for
  HF with iron deficiency; continuous glucose monitoring and **time-in-range** as a diabetes metric. (1m)
- **Pearl (½m):** the unifying theme is **cardiorenal-metabolic overlap** — drugs once seen as "diabetes
  drugs" (SGLT2i) are now core **heart-failure and kidney** therapy. (½m)
