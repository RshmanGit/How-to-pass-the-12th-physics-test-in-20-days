### Q1. Short note: Multiple sclerosis (5m)
<!-- id: sys01-saq-q1 -->

**Restated:** Write a short note on multiple sclerosis (MS).

- **Definition (1m):** chronic **immune-mediated demyelinating** disease of the CNS characterised by lesions
  **disseminated in space and time**; commonest in young adult women. (1m)
- **Clinical features (1½m):** relapsing-remitting course (commonest); optic neuritis (painful visual loss),
  internuclear ophthalmoplegia, sensory/motor deficits, cerebellar signs, bladder dysfunction;
  **Uhthoff's phenomenon** (worsening with heat), **Lhermitte's sign**. (1½m)
- **Investigations (1½m):** **MRI brain + spine** — periventricular/juxtacortical/infratentorial/cord
  plaques (dissemination in space + time); **CSF oligoclonal bands**; visual evoked potentials. Diagnosis
  by the **McDonald criteria** (see `03-criteria.md`). (1½m)
- **Management (1m):** **acute relapse** — IV methylprednisolone; **disease-modifying therapy** (interferon-β,
  glatiramer, dimethyl fumarate, natalizumab, ocrelizumab) to reduce relapses; symptomatic care +
  rehabilitation. (1m)

### Q2. Short note: Dementia (5m)
<!-- id: sys01-saq-q2 -->

**Restated:** Write a short note on dementia.

- **Definition (1m):** acquired, **progressive** decline in ≥2 cognitive domains (memory, language,
  executive, visuospatial) causing loss of function, with **clear consciousness** (distinguishes it from
  delirium). (1m)
- **Causes / types (1½m):** **Alzheimer's disease** (commonest), **vascular dementia**, **dementia with
  Lewy bodies**, **frontotemporal dementia**; reversible mimics — B12 deficiency, hypothyroidism, normal-
  pressure hydrocephalus, depression ("pseudodementia"). (1½m)
- **Assessment (1½m):** history + cognitive testing (**MMSE/MoCA**); bloods (B12, TFT, calcium, glucose);
  **neuroimaging (CT/MRI)** to exclude structural/reversible cause. (1½m)
- **Management (1m):** treat reversible causes; **cholinesterase inhibitors** (donepezil/rivastigmine/
  galantamine) for Alzheimer's, **memantine** in moderate-severe disease; manage behavioural symptoms,
  caregiver support, safety. (1m)

### Q3. Short note: CNS tuberculosis / tuberculous meningitis (5m)
<!-- id: sys01-saq-q3 -->

**Restated:** Write a short note on tuberculous meningitis (CNS TB).

- **Definition (1m):** subacute meningitis from haematogenous spread of *Mycobacterium tuberculosis* to the
  meninges (± tuberculoma); a leading cause of chronic meningitis in endemic areas. (1m)
- **Clinical features (1m):** subacute fever, headache, vomiting, neck stiffness; **cranial nerve palsies**
  (esp. VI), altered sensorium; complications — **hydrocephalus**, vasculitic infarcts, SIADH. (1m)
- **Investigations (1½m):** **CSF** — high protein, **low glucose**, lymphocytic pleocytosis, high ADA;
  **CBNAAT/GeneXpert** + AFB culture; **CT/MRI** — basal meningeal enhancement, hydrocephalus, infarcts,
  tuberculomas. (1½m)
- **Management (1½m):** **anti-tubercular therapy** (RIPE — rifampicin, isoniazid, pyrazinamide,
  ethambutol — extended continuation phase, total ~9–12 months) **+ adjunctive corticosteroids (dexamethasone, tapered over 6–8 weeks)** (reduce
  mortality/complications); manage hydrocephalus (shunt/drainage). (1½m)

**What to tabulate:** [ ] **CSF in TB vs bacterial vs viral meningitis** (cells, protein, glucose) — ~1m

**Learn it:** Harrison's — *Tuberculosis* / *Chronic Meningitis*. · API — *Tropical Neurology / TB*. [verify]

### Q4. Short note: Status epilepticus — definition and management (5m)
<!-- id: sys01-saq-q4 -->

**Restated:** Write a short note on the management of status epilepticus.

- **Definition (1m):** a single seizure ≥ **5 minutes**, or ≥2 seizures without recovery of consciousness
  between them; a neurological emergency (risk of neuronal injury rises with duration). (1m)
- **Immediate (1m):** **ABC**, high-flow oxygen, IV access, monitor; **check capillary glucose** (give
  glucose + thiamine if hypoglycaemic); bloods for electrolytes, AED levels, toxicology. (1m)
- **Stepwise drug therapy (2m):**
  - **Step 1 — benzodiazepine:** IV **lorazepam 0.1 mg/kg (≈4 mg)**, may repeat once (or IM midazolam /
    rectal diazepam). (1m)
  - **Step 2 — second-line AED (doses differ by agent, per ESETT):** IV **levetiracetam 60 mg/kg** /
    **sodium valproate 40 mg/kg** / **(fos)phenytoin 20 mg/kg PE**. (½m)
  - **Step 3 — refractory:** intubate + **general anaesthesia** (midazolam/propofol/thiopentone) in ICU
    with continuous EEG. (½m)
- **Always treat the precipitant** (infection, withdrawal, metabolic, non-adherence). (1m)
