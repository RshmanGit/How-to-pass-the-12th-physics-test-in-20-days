### Q1. McDonald criteria for multiple sclerosis — principle and components (5m)
<!-- id: sys01-crit-q1 -->

**Restated:** Outline the McDonald criteria for diagnosing multiple sclerosis.

- **Principle (2m):** MS is diagnosed by demonstrating **dissemination in space (DIS)** and **dissemination
  in time (DIT)** of CNS demyelinating lesions, **after excluding better explanations**. The criteria
  combine clinical attacks with MRI (and CSF) so the diagnosis can be made earlier — even after a single
  clinical attack. (2m)
- **Dissemination in space (1m):** ≥1 T2 lesion in ≥2 of the typical CNS regions —
  **periventricular, (juxta)cortical, infratentorial, spinal cord** (the **2017** revision's four regions). (1m)
- **Dissemination in time (1m):** a new T2/enhancing lesion on follow-up MRI, **or** simultaneous presence
  of gadolinium-**enhancing and non-enhancing** lesions, **or** a second clinical attack. (1m)
- **CSF substitution (1m):** in a typical clinical isolated syndrome with DIS, **CSF-specific oligoclonal
  bands** can substitute for DIT to confirm the diagnosis. (1m)
- **⚠ Edition note:** the above is the **2017 McDonald revision** (still widely taught/examined in India).
  The **2024 revision** adds the **optic nerve as a 5th region** and lets DIS across enough regions waive the
  separate DIT requirement — **confirm which edition Mitsu's examiners use** before mugging.

### Q2. Glasgow Coma Scale — components and scoring (5m)
<!-- id: sys01-crit-q2 -->

**Restated:** Describe the Glasgow Coma Scale (GCS) and its use.

- **Purpose (1m):** a reproducible bedside scale grading **impaired consciousness**, used for triage,
  serial monitoring and prognosis (e.g. in head injury). Score **3 (worst) to 15 (best).** (1m)
- **Three components (3m):**

| Response | Score range | Best → worst |
|---|---|---|
| **Eye opening (E)** | 1–4 | spontaneous (4) · to speech (3) · to pain (2) · none (1) |
| **Verbal response (V)** | 1–5 | oriented (5) · confused (4) · inappropriate words (3) · incomprehensible sounds (2) · none (1) |
| **Best motor response (M)** | 1–6 | obeys (6) · localises pain (5) · withdraws (4) · abnormal flexion (3) · extension (2) · none (1) |

  (3m for the full table; ½m partial if components named without the per-level scores.)

- **Use / interpretation (1m):** total = E + V + M; **≤8 = coma** (consider airway protection/intubation);
  document the breakdown (e.g. **E3V4M5 = 12**), not just the total, and trend it over time. (1m)
