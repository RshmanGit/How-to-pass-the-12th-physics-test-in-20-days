### Q1. Short note: Irritable bowel syndrome (5m)
<!-- id: sys06-saq-q1 -->

**Restated:** Write a short note on irritable bowel syndrome (IBS).

- **Definition (1m):** a **functional GI disorder** — chronic **recurrent abdominal pain related to
  defaecation** with altered bowel habit (frequency/form), **without** structural or biochemical
  abnormality (a positive clinical diagnosis, not pure exclusion). (1m)
- **Clinical features (1½m):** abdominal pain **relieved by defaecation**, bloating, altered stool
  frequency/form; subtypes **IBS-C / IBS-D / IBS-M**; symptoms worse with stress; **no nocturnal symptoms**.
  **Red flags mandating work-up:** age >50/new onset, weight loss, PR bleeding, anaemia, nocturnal/
  progressive symptoms, family history of colorectal cancer/IBD. (1½m)
- **Diagnosis (1m):** clinical, by **Rome IV criteria**; limited tests to exclude mimics — FBC, CRP,
  **coeliac serology, faecal calprotectin** (to exclude IBD); colonoscopy only if red flags. (1m)
- **Management (1m):** **reassurance + dietary advice (low-FODMAP)**, fibre adjustment; **antispasmodics**
  (mebeverine, hyoscine) for pain; **loperamide** for diarrhoea, **laxatives** for constipation; low-dose
  **TCA/SSRI** for refractory pain/gut-brain modulation; address psychological factors. (1m)

### Q2. Short note: Approach to upper gastrointestinal bleeding (5m)
<!-- id: sys06-saq-q2 -->

**Restated:** Write a short note on the approach to a patient with upper GI bleeding.

- **Definition (1m):** bleeding from a source **proximal to the ligament of Treitz**, presenting as
  **haematemesis ("coffee-ground" or fresh), melaena**, or (if brisk) haematochezia with shock. (1m)
- **Causes (1m):** **peptic ulcer disease** (commonest), **oesophageal/gastric varices**,
  Mallory-Weiss tear, erosive gastritis/oesophagitis, malignancy, vascular lesions. (1m)
- **Resuscitate first (1½m):** **ABC**, two large-bore IV cannulae, fluids/**blood** (restrictive
  transfusion target **Hb ~7 g/dL**), correct coagulopathy; **risk stratify** (Glasgow-Blatchford pre-scope,
  Rockall post-scope); **PPI** (IV pantoprazole); **if variceal suspected — terlipressin + prophylactic
  IV antibiotic (ceftriaxone)**. (1½m)
- **Definitive (½m):** **upper GI endoscopy within 24 h** (urgently if unstable) — therapeutic: adrenaline
  injection + clip/thermal for ulcers, **band ligation** for varices; rescue = TIPS/surgery; **eradicate
  H. pylori / stop NSAIDs** afterwards. (½m)

### Q3. Short note: Hepatic encephalopathy (5m)
<!-- id: sys06-saq-q3 -->

**Restated:** Write a short note on hepatic encephalopathy.

- **Definition (1m):** a **reversible neuropsychiatric syndrome** in liver failure/portosystemic shunting,
  due to failure to clear **gut-derived neurotoxins, chiefly ammonia**, which cross the blood-brain
  barrier. (1m)
- **Clinical features / grading (1½m):** spectrum from impaired concentration to coma; **flapping tremor
  (asterixis)**, fetor hepaticus, altered sleep, confusion. **West Haven grades I–IV** (I = mild confusion
  → IV = coma). (1½m)
- **Precipitants (1½m):** **GI bleeding, infection (incl. SBP), constipation, dehydration/diuretics,
  electrolyte disturbance (hypokalaemia, hyponatraemia), sedatives, high protein load, TIPS,
  non-adherence**. (1½m)
- **Management (1m):** **treat the precipitant**; **lactulose** (titrate to 2–3 soft stools/day — first-line)
  ± **rifaximin 550 mg BD**; ensure nutrition (do **not** over-restrict protein); correct electrolytes;
  consider transplant in recurrent cases. (1m)

encephalopathy*. [verify]

### Q4. Short note: Alcoholic liver disease (5m)
<!-- id: sys06-saq-q4 -->

**Restated:** Write a short note on alcoholic liver disease (ALD).

- **Definition / spectrum (1m):** a spectrum of liver injury from chronic excess alcohol —
  **fatty liver (steatosis) → alcoholic hepatitis → cirrhosis**; steatosis is reversible, cirrhosis is
  not. (1m)
- **Clinical features (1½m):** asymptomatic hepatomegaly (steatosis); **alcoholic hepatitis** — jaundice,
  fever, tender hepatomegaly, anorexia, may precipitate liver failure; cirrhosis with its stigmata/
  complications. Signs of dependence, withdrawal. (1½m)
- **Investigations (1m):** **AST:ALT ratio >2:1** (both usually <300), raised **GGT** and **MCV**,
  low albumin, raised bilirubin/INR; USG (fatty/cirrhotic liver); exclude other causes. Severity of
  alcoholic hepatitis by **Maddrey's discriminant function (DF ≥32 = severe)**. (1m)
- **Management (1½m):** **abstinence** is the single most important step; treat **withdrawal**
  (benzodiazepines), thiamine/vitamin replacement, nutrition; **severe alcoholic hepatitis (DF ≥32)** →
  **prednisolone 40 mg/day × 28 days** (assess with **Lille score** at day 7); manage cirrhosis
  complications; transplant in selected abstinent patients. (1½m)
