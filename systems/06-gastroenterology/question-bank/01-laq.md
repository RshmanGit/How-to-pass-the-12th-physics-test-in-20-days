### Q1. Cirrhosis of liver & portal hypertension — aetiopathogenesis, clinical features, investigations, complications and management (15m)
<!-- id: sys06-laq-q1 -->

**Restated:** Discuss the aetiopathogenesis, clinical features, investigations, complications and management
of cirrhosis of the liver with portal hypertension.

**Definition (1m)**
- Cirrhosis = the **end-stage, diffuse, irreversible** result of chronic liver injury — **diffuse fibrosis
  with conversion of normal architecture into structurally abnormal regenerative nodules**. **Portal
  hypertension** = portal venous pressure gradient (**HVPG**) **>5 mmHg**; clinically significant at
  **≥10 mmHg** (varices) and bleeding risk at **≥12 mmHg**. (1m)

**Aetiopathogenesis (3m)**
- **Causes (1½m):** **alcohol** and **viral hepatitis B/C** (commonest worldwide); **NAFLD/NASH** (rising);
  autoimmune hepatitis; cholestatic — **primary biliary cholangitis, primary sclerosing cholangitis**;
  metabolic — **haemochromatosis, Wilson's disease, α1-antitrypsin deficiency**; cardiac/Budd-Chiari;
  cryptogenic. (1½m)
- **Pathogenesis (1½m):** chronic injury → **hepatic stellate cell activation** → collagen deposition
  (fibrosis) + regenerative nodules → distortion of sinusoids → **increased intrahepatic resistance**;
  combined with **splanchnic vasodilatation** (nitric oxide) and **hyperdynamic circulation** raising portal
  inflow → **portal hypertension**. Portosystemic collaterals open (gastro-oesophageal, rectal,
  paraumbilical). (1½m)

**Clinical features (3m)**
- **Compensated** — often asymptomatic; fatigue, anorexia. **Decompensated (the marks)** — jaundice,
  ascites, variceal bleed, encephalopathy. (1m)
- **Stigmata of chronic liver disease:** spider naevi, palmar erythema, gynaecomastia, testicular atrophy,
  Dupuytren's, leuconychia, clubbing, parotidomegaly, **caput medusae**, splenomegaly. (1m)
- **Of portal hypertension:** splenomegaly, ascites, dilated abdominal-wall veins, oesophageal varices. (1m)

**Investigations (3m)**
- **Bloods (1m):** LFTs (AST/ALT, bilirubin, **low albumin**, raised globulins), **prolonged PT/INR**,
  thrombocytopenia (hypersplenism); aetiology screen — viral serology, autoantibodies, ferritin,
  caeruloplasmin, α1-antitrypsin. (1m)
- **Imaging (1m):** **USG abdomen** — coarse/nodular liver, splenomegaly, ascites, dilated portal vein
  (>13 mm), collaterals; **transient elastography (FibroScan)** for fibrosis; Doppler for portal flow/
  Budd-Chiari. (1m)
- **Endoscopy + staging (1m):** **upper GI endoscopy** to screen for/grade **varices**; ascitic fluid
  analysis (**SAAG ≥1.1 g/dL** = portal hypertensive); **liver biopsy** if diagnosis unclear; score with
  **Child-Pugh / MELD** (see `03-criteria.md`). (1m)

**Complications + Management (5m)**
- **Variceal bleeding (2m):** resuscitate; **terlipressin** (or octreotide/somatostatin) + **prophylactic
  IV ceftriaxone 1 g/day**; urgent **endoscopic band ligation** (sclerotherapy if needed); rescue =
  balloon tamponade / **TIPS**. **Primary/secondary prophylaxis:** non-selective β-blocker (**propranolol
  / carvedilol**) ± band ligation. (2m)
- **Ascites + SBP (1½m):** salt restriction + **spironolactone ± furosemide**; large-volume paracentesis
  with **albumin** cover; **SBP** (ascitic neutrophils **>250/mm³**) → **IV cefotaxime 2 g 8-hrly** +
  albumin; norfloxacin/ciprofloxacin prophylaxis. (1½m)
- **Other (1m):** **hepatic encephalopathy** — lactulose + rifaximin (see `02-saq.md`);
  **hepatorenal syndrome** — terlipressin + albumin; **hepatocellular carcinoma** surveillance (6-monthly
  USG ± AFP). (1m)
- **Definitive (½m):** treat the cause (antivirals, abstinence); **liver transplantation** for
  decompensated disease (MELD-prioritised). (½m)

**What to draw / tabulate (independent marks):**
- [ ] **Diagram — portal venous system + sites of portosystemic anastomosis** (oesophageal, rectal, paraumbilical) — ~1m
- [ ] **Table — causes of ascites by SAAG** (≥1.1 vs <1.1 g/dL) — ~1m

### Q2. Acute pancreatitis — aetiopathogenesis, clinical features, investigations and management (15m)
<!-- id: sys06-laq-q2 -->

**Restated:** Discuss the aetiopathogenesis, clinical features, investigations, severity assessment and
management of acute pancreatitis.

**Definition (1m)**
- Acute pancreatitis = an **acute inflammatory process of the pancreas** with variable involvement of
  peripancreatic tissues/remote organs, caused by **inappropriate intra-acinar activation of pancreatic
  enzymes** → autodigestion. Diagnosis (≥2 of 3): **typical pain · amylase/lipase >3× upper limit ·
  characteristic imaging**. (1m)

**Aetiopathogenesis (3m)**
- **Causes — "GET SMASHED" (2m):** **G**allstones and **E**thanol (alcohol) = ~80%; also **T**rauma/post-
  **ERCP**, **S**teroids, **M**umps/infection, **A**utoimmune, **S**corpion sting, **H**yperlipidaemia
  (triglycerides >1000 mg/dL)/**H**ypercalcaemia, **E**RCP, **D**rugs (azathioprine, thiazides, valproate). (2m)
- **Pathogenesis (1m):** premature **trypsinogen → trypsin** activation within acinar cells → cascade
  activation of other enzymes → **autodigestion**, microvascular injury, oedema, **fat necrosis** and a
  **systemic inflammatory response (SIRS)** that can progress to organ failure. (1m)

**Clinical features (3m)**
- **Severe constant epigastric pain radiating to the back**, relieved by sitting forward; nausea, vomiting. (1m)
- Tachycardia, fever, dehydration, **epigastric tenderness/guarding**, reduced bowel sounds (ileus). (1m)
- **Haemorrhagic signs** (severe): **Cullen's** (periumbilical) and **Grey-Turner's** (flank) bruising;
  features of organ failure (hypotension, hypoxia, oliguria). (1m)

**Investigations (3m)**
- **Enzymes (1m):** **serum lipase** (more specific, stays elevated longer) and **amylase >3×** upper limit
  of normal; magnitude does **not** indicate severity. (1m)
- **Severity + cause (1m):** FBC, urea, **calcium**, glucose, LFTs, LDH, **CRP** (>150 at 48 h = severe),
  ABG; **USG abdomen** for gallstones; score with **Ranson / modified Glasgow / APACHE-II** (see
  `03-criteria.md`). (1m)
- **Imaging (1m):** erect CXR/AXR to exclude perforation; **contrast-enhanced CT at 48–72 h** to assess
  necrosis/complications (too early underestimates); MRCP/ERCP if biliary obstruction. (1m)

**Management (5m)**
- **Supportive — the core (2m):** admit; **aggressive IV fluid resuscitation** (balanced crystalloid,
  goal-directed); **oxygen**; **analgesia** (opioids); antiemetics; correct electrolytes (Ca, Mg);
  monitor urine output; **HDU/ICU if severe/organ failure**. (2m)
- **Nutrition (1m):** **early enteral feeding** (oral/NJ) within 24–72 h once tolerated — **NOT prolonged
  NBM/TPN**; enteral feeding maintains gut barrier and lowers infection. (1m)
- **Treat the cause (1m):** gallstone pancreatitis with **cholangitis/obstruction → urgent ERCP +
  sphincterotomy**; **cholecystectomy** during the same admission for mild biliary pancreatitis; manage
  hypertriglyceridaemia/hypercalcaemia. (1m)
- **Complications (1m):** **infected pancreatic necrosis** → carbapenem + delayed **step-up** (percutaneous
  drainage → minimally invasive necrosectomy); **pseudocyst** (drain if symptomatic/>6 wks); routine
  **prophylactic antibiotics are NOT recommended** in sterile necrosis. (1m)

**What to draw / tabulate (independent marks):**
- [ ] **Table — "GET SMASHED" aetiologies** grouped (obstructive / toxic-metabolic / other) — ~1m
- [ ] **Flowchart — severity-stratified pathway** (diagnose → score → fluids/analgesia → CT 48–72h → treat cause/complications) — ~1m

**Learn it:** Harrison's — *Acute and Chronic Pancreatitis*. · Davidson's — *Diseases of the pancreas*. ·
API — *Gastroenterology → Pancreatitis*. [verify current edition]

### Q3. Viral hepatitis — types, clinical features, investigations and management (10m)
<!-- id: sys06-laq-q3 -->

**Restated:** Classify the hepatitis viruses and discuss the clinical features, investigations and
management of acute viral hepatitis.

**Definition (1m)**
- Viral hepatitis = **systemic infection predominantly affecting the liver** causing hepatocyte
  inflammation/necrosis, caused chiefly by hepatotropic viruses **A, B, C, D, E**. (1m)

**Aetiopathogenesis / types (2m)**
- **Faecal–oral, self-limiting, no chronicity:** **HAV** and **HEV** (HEV dangerous in **pregnancy** —
  high mortality; chronic only in immunosuppressed). (1m)
- **Parenteral/sexual/vertical, may become chronic:** **HBV** (DNA virus; ~5% adults → chronic),
  **HCV** (RNA; ~50–85% → chronic), **HDV** (defective — needs HBsAg, co-/super-infection). Injury is
  largely **immune-mediated** (cytotoxic T-cell). (1m)

**Clinical features (2m)**
- **Prodrome:** malaise, anorexia, nausea, low-grade fever, RUQ discomfort; smokers develop distaste for
  cigarettes. (1m)
- **Icteric phase:** jaundice, dark urine, pale stools, tender hepatomegaly; most acute illness resolves.
  **Watch for acute liver failure** (encephalopathy, coagulopathy). (1m)

**Investigations (2m)**
- **LFTs (1m):** markedly raised **ALT/AST** (ALT often >1000), raised bilirubin; **PT/INR** is the key
  prognostic marker (rising INR → failure). (1m)
- **Serology / virology (1m):** **anti-HAV IgM**; **HBsAg, anti-HBc IgM, HBeAg, HBV DNA**; **anti-HCV →
  HCV RNA**; anti-HEV IgM; **anti-HDV** if HBsAg-positive. (1m)

**Management (3m)**
- **Acute — mainly supportive (1½m):** rest, hydration, nutrition, antiemetics; **avoid hepatotoxins
  (alcohol, paracetamol overuse)**; most A/E and acute B resolve without antivirals; monitor INR/sensorium
  for **ALF** (transplant referral if it develops). (1½m)
- **Chronic / specific (1m):** **chronic HBV** — nucleos(t)ides **tenofovir / entecavir** (± peg-IFN);
  **HCV** — **direct-acting antivirals** (e.g. sofosbuvir-based regimens, 8–12 weeks, >95% cure);
  treat HDV via HBV. (1m)
- **Prevention (½m):** **HAV and HBV vaccines**; HBV — universal infant + at-risk; HBIG + vaccine for
  neonates of HBsAg+ mothers/needlestick; blood-product screening, safe injections. (½m)

**What to draw / tabulate (independent marks):**
- [ ] **Table — Hepatitis A–E** (genome, transmission, chronicity, vaccine) — ~1½m
- [ ] **Table — HBV serological patterns** (acute / chronic / resolved / vaccinated) — ~1m

**Learn it:** Harrison's — *Acute Viral Hepatitis* / *Chronic Hepatitis*. · Davidson's — *Viral hepatitis*. ·
API — *Hepatology → Viral hepatitis*. [verify current edition]

### Q4. Acute liver failure — definition, causes, clinical features, investigations and management (10m)
<!-- id: sys06-laq-q4 -->

**Restated:** Discuss the causes, clinical features, complications, investigations and management of acute
liver failure (fulminant hepatic failure).

**Definition (1m)**
- Acute liver failure (ALF) = **acute severe liver injury with coagulopathy (INR ≥1.5) and any degree of
  encephalopathy**, in a patient **without pre-existing cirrhosis** and an illness **<26 weeks**.
  Classified hyperacute/acute/subacute by jaundice-to-encephalopathy interval. (1m)

**Aetiopathogenesis (2m)**
- **Drugs/toxins (1m):** **paracetamol (acetaminophen) overdose** — the commonest cause in the West;
  idiosyncratic drug reactions (anti-TB drugs, anti-epileptics, halothane), **Amanita** mushroom, herbal
  toxins. (1m)
- **Other (1m):** **viral** (A, B, **E — esp. pregnancy**); **acute fatty liver of pregnancy / HELLP**;
  Wilson's disease; autoimmune hepatitis; **Budd-Chiari**; ischaemic ("shock liver"). Final common path =
  **massive hepatocyte necrosis** → loss of synthetic, detoxifying and metabolic function. (1m)

**Clinical features (2m)**
- **Encephalopathy** (graded I–IV) and **jaundice**; coagulopathy → bleeding; nausea, RUQ pain. (1m)
- **Complications (the dangerous core):** **cerebral oedema/raised ICP** (leading cause of death),
  hypoglycaemia, infection/sepsis, **AKI/hepatorenal**, lactic acidosis, multi-organ failure. (1m)

**Investigations (2m)**
- **Severity/synthetic (1m):** **PT/INR** (key prognostic), glucose (**hypoglycaemia**), ammonia,
  ABG/lactate, U&E/creatinine, LFTs, FBC. (1m)
- **Cause work-up (1m):** **paracetamol level**, viral serology, autoimmune markers, caeruloplasmin/copper
  (Wilson's), pregnancy test, USG/Doppler liver (Budd-Chiari). (1m)

**Management (3m)**
- **ICU supportive (1½m):** manage in a **liver/ICU unit**; airway protection if grade III–IV
  encephalopathy; **N-acetylcysteine** (specific for paracetamol; benefit even in non-paracetamol ALF);
  treat **hypoglycaemia** (10% dextrose), correct coagulopathy only if bleeding/procedure; **nurse head-up
  30°, manage raised ICP** (mannitol/hypertonic saline); broad-spectrum antibiotics for sepsis; renal
  support (CRRT) as needed. (1½m)
- **Specific antidotes/treatment (½m):** NAC (paracetamol), antivirals (HBV), penicillin/silibinin
  (Amanita), delivery (pregnancy-related). (½m)
- **Transplant (1m):** **early referral to a transplant centre**; list using **King's College Criteria**
  (paracetamol: pH <7.3, **or** INR >6.5 + creatinine >300 + grade III/IV encephalopathy; non-paracetamol:
  INR >6.5, or 3 of 5 adverse factors). **Liver transplantation is the definitive life-saving option.** (1m)

**What to draw / tabulate (independent marks):**
- [ ] **Table — West Haven grades of encephalopathy** (I–IV) — ~1m
- [ ] **Flowchart — ALF pathway** (recognise → ICU/NAC → treat cause + ICP → King's criteria → transplant) — ~1m

**Learn it:** Harrison's — *Acute Liver Failure*. · Davidson's — *Acute liver failure*. · API —
*Hepatology → Hepatic failure*. [verify current edition — King's College criteria]

### Q5. Inflammatory bowel disease — Crohn's disease & ulcerative colitis (10m)
<!-- id: sys06-laq-q5 -->

**Restated:** Compare and discuss the aetiopathogenesis, clinical features, investigations and management of
ulcerative colitis and Crohn's disease.

**Definition (1m)**
- Inflammatory bowel disease (IBD) = chronic relapsing-remitting **idiopathic inflammation of the GI tract**.
  **Ulcerative colitis (UC)** = mucosal inflammation **confined to the colon**, continuous from the rectum;
  **Crohn's disease (CD)** = **transmural** inflammation affecting **any part of the gut (mouth to anus),
  skip lesions**. (1m)

**Aetiopathogenesis (2m)**
- **Multifactorial:** dysregulated **mucosal immune response to gut microbiota** in a genetically
  susceptible host (e.g. **NOD2/CARD15** in CD) + environmental triggers. (1m)
- **Smoking** — *protective* in UC but a *risk factor/worsens* CD; appendicectomy protective in UC. CD shows
  **transmural granulomatous** inflammation; UC shows **continuous mucosal** inflammation with crypt
  abscesses. (1m)

**Clinical features (3m)**
- **UC (1m):** **bloody diarrhoea with mucus**, tenesmus, urgency, lower abdominal cramps; severity by
  **Truelove-Witts**; risk of **toxic megacolon**. (1m)
- **CD (1m):** abdominal pain, **non-bloody diarrhoea**, weight loss; **perianal disease (fistulae,
  abscess), mouth ulcers**, strictures and obstruction; right iliac fossa mass. (1m)
- **Extra-intestinal (1m):** arthritis/sacroiliitis, **erythema nodosum, pyoderma gangrenosum**, anterior
  uveitis/episcleritis, **primary sclerosing cholangitis** (UC), gallstones/renal stones (CD). (1m)

**Investigations (2m)**
- **Bloods/stool (1m):** anaemia, raised **CRP/ESR/platelets**, low albumin; **faecal calprotectin**
  (mucosal inflammation); stool culture/*C. difficile* to exclude infection. (1m)
- **Endoscopy + imaging (1m):** **ileocolonoscopy + biopsy** is key — UC: continuous mucosal inflammation
  from rectum; CD: skip lesions, **cobblestoning**, granulomas; **MR enterography** for small-bowel CD;
  AXR for toxic megacolon. (1m)

**Management (2m)**
- **UC (1m):** **5-ASA (mesalazine)** induces + maintains remission in mild-moderate; corticosteroids for
  flares; immunomodulators (azathioprine) / **biologics (anti-TNF — infliximab)** for refractory;
  **colectomy is curative** (severe/toxic megacolon/dysplasia). (1m)
- **CD (1m):** induce with **corticosteroids** (budesonide ileal disease); maintain with **azathioprine/
  methotrexate** or **biologics (infliximab/adalimumab, ustekinumab, vedolizumab)**; **5-ASA less
  effective**; surgery for strictures/fistulae/abscess (**not curative**); smoking cessation. (1m)

**What to draw / tabulate (independent marks):**
- [ ] **Table — UC vs Crohn's** (site, depth, pattern, bleeding, fistula, smoking, histology, surgery) — ~1½m
- [ ] **Diagram — distribution** (UC continuous colonic vs CD skip lesions mouth-to-anus) — ~½m

**Learn it:** Harrison's — *Inflammatory Bowel Disease*. · Davidson's — *Inflammatory bowel disease*. ·
API — *Gastroenterology → IBD*. [verify current edition]
