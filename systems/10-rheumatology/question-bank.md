### Q1. Systemic lupus erythematosus — features, complications and management (10m)
<!-- id: sys10-q1 -->

**Restated:** Discuss the clinical features, complications and management of systemic lupus erythematosus
(SLE).

**Definition (1m)**
- SLE = a chronic, multisystem **autoimmune connective-tissue disease** characterised by **autoantibodies to
  nuclear antigens** and immune-complex–mediated tissue injury; relapsing-remitting course, marked female
  predominance (young women, ~9:1). (1m)

**Aetiopathogenesis (2m)**
- **Loss of self-tolerance** with defective clearance of apoptotic cells → exposure of nuclear antigens →
  **autoantibodies (ANA, anti-dsDNA, anti-Sm)** and **immune-complex deposition** → complement activation
  and inflammation in skin, joints, kidney, serosa, CNS. (1m)
- Multifactorial: **genetic** (HLA, complement C1q/C2/C4 deficiency), **hormonal** (oestrogen), and
  **environmental** triggers (UV light, drugs, EBV). **Type III hypersensitivity** is the dominant mechanism
  (± type II). (1m)

**Clinical features (3m)**
- **Constitutional:** fatigue, fever, weight loss. (½m)
- **Mucocutaneous:** photosensitive **malar ("butterfly") rash** sparing nasolabial folds, discoid rash,
  oral ulcers, non-scarring alopecia. (½m)
- **Musculoskeletal:** symmetrical **non-erosive arthritis/arthralgia** (small joints), myalgia;
  Jaccoud's arthropathy. (½m)
- **Renal — lupus nephritis** (major prognostic determinant): proteinuria, haematuria, casts. (½m)
- **Serositis** (pleuritis, pericarditis), **haematological** (cytopenias), and **neuropsychiatric**
  (seizures, psychosis). (½m)
- **Raynaud's phenomenon**; **secondary antiphospholipid syndrome** (thrombosis, recurrent miscarriage). (½m)

**Investigations (2m)**
- **Autoantibodies:** **ANA** — most sensitive screen (highly sensitive, low specificity); **anti-dsDNA**
  and **anti-Sm** — specific; anti-dsDNA titre + **low complement (C3/C4)** track disease activity. (1m)
- **Disease/organ assessment:** FBC (cytopenias), ESR raised with **normal/near-normal CRP** (CRP rise
  suggests infection/serositis), urinalysis + protein:creatinine ratio, renal biopsy for nephritis;
  antiphospholipid antibodies (lupus anticoagulant, anti-cardiolipin, anti-β2-GPI). (1m)

**Management (2m)**
- **General:** **photoprotection/sunscreen**, smoking cessation, cardiovascular-risk reduction; **avoid
  precipitating drugs**. (½m)
- **Pharmacological:** **hydroxychloroquine** for almost all patients (reduces flares — backbone therapy);
  **corticosteroids** for flares (lowest effective dose); **immunosuppressants** (azathioprine,
  mycophenolate mofetil, methotrexate) as steroid-sparing agents; **cyclophosphamide or mycophenolate** for
  severe lupus nephritis/CNS disease; **belimumab/rituximab** for refractory disease. (1m)
- **Complications to flag:** lupus nephritis → CKD; **accelerated atherosclerosis** (leading late cause of
  death); infection (disease + immunosuppression); antiphospholipid thrombosis — anticoagulate. (½m)

- [ ] **Table — key autoantibodies in SLE** (ANA / anti-dsDNA / anti-Sm / antiphospholipid: sensitivity vs
  specificity, clinical link) — ~1m
- [ ] **Figure — malar rash distribution** (cheeks + bridge, sparing nasolabial folds) — ~½m

- Harrison's — *Systemic Lupus Erythematosus* (Rheumatology). · Davidson's — *Lupus erythematosus and

### Q2. Rheumatoid arthritis — clinical features, extra-articular manifestations, investigations and management including DMARDs/biologics (10m)
<!-- id: sys10-q2 -->

**Restated:** Discuss the aetiopathogenesis, clinical features (including extra-articular manifestations),
investigations and management of rheumatoid arthritis, with emphasis on DMARDs and biologics.

**Definition (1m)**
- Rheumatoid arthritis (RA) = a chronic, **symmetrical, inflammatory, erosive polyarthritis** of an
  autoimmune nature, primarily affecting **small joints**, with **synovitis** as the central lesion and
  potential for systemic (extra-articular) involvement. (1m)

**Aetiopathogenesis (2m)**
- **Autoimmune synovitis:** activation of T/B cells and macrophages → pro-inflammatory cytokines
  (**TNF-α, IL-6, IL-1**) → proliferative inflamed synovium (**pannus**) that erodes cartilage and bone. (1m)
- **Autoantibodies:** **rheumatoid factor (RF)** and **anti-cyclic-citrullinated-peptide (anti-CCP/ACPA)** —
  the latter more specific and prognostic. **Genetic:** HLA-DR4/DR1 ("shared epitope"); **environmental:**
  smoking (strong), periodontitis. (1m)

**Clinical features (3m)**
- **Articular (2m):** insidious **symmetrical polyarthritis** of **MCP, PIP and wrist** joints (spares DIP);
  **early-morning stiffness >1 h**; warm, swollen, tender joints. Late **deformities** — ulnar deviation,
  swan-neck, boutonnière, Z-thumb, atlantoaxial subluxation. (2m)
- **Extra-articular (1m):** **rheumatoid nodules**, interstitial lung disease/pleural effusion, pericarditis
  and accelerated **cardiovascular disease**, **anaemia of chronic disease**, scleritis/episcleritis,
  **Felty's syndrome** (RA + splenomegaly + neutropenia), secondary Sjögren's, vasculitis, **AA
  amyloidosis**. (1m)

**Investigations (2m)**
- **Serology + inflammatory markers:** **RF** and **anti-CCP** (anti-CCP more specific); raised **ESR/CRP**;
  normocytic anaemia. (1m)
- **Imaging:** X-ray hands/feet — **soft-tissue swelling, juxta-articular osteopenia, joint-space narrowing,
  marginal erosions**; ultrasound/MRI detect early synovitis/erosions. Apply the **ACR/EULAR 2010
  classification criteria** (joints involved, serology, acute-phase reactants, duration). (1m)

**Management (2m)**
- **Principle — "treat-to-target", start DMARD early (½m):** aim for remission/low disease activity; the
  **window of opportunity** — early DMARD prevents erosion. (½m)
- **Conventional synthetic DMARDs (csDMARDs) (½m):** **methotrexate** first-line (oral/SC weekly, **with
  folic acid** on a non-MTX day; monitor FBC/LFTs); alternatives/add-ons — sulfasalazine, hydroxychloroquine,
  leflunomide. **Short-course/low-dose steroids** as a bridge during flares. NSAIDs for symptom relief. (½m)
- **Biologic / targeted DMARDs (½m):** if inadequate response to csDMARDs — **anti-TNF** (etanercept,
  infliximab, adalimumab), **IL-6 inhibitor** (tocilizumab), **B-cell depletion** (rituximab), abatacept;
  oral **JAK inhibitors** (tofacitinib, baricitinib). **Screen and exclude latent TB/hepatitis B before
  biologics**; warn of infection risk. (½m)
- **MDT / supportive (½m):** physiotherapy, occupational therapy, surgery for damaged joints; manage
  cardiovascular risk and osteoporosis. (½m)

**What to draw / tabulate (independent marks):**
- [ ] **Table — RA vs osteoarthritis** (small-joint symmetry, morning stiffness, DIP sparing, ESR/CRP) — ~1m
- [ ] **Diagram — typical hand deformities** (ulnar deviation, swan-neck, boutonnière) — ~½m
- [ ] **Flowchart — treat-to-target DMARD escalation** (MTX → combination → biologic/JAK) — ~½m

**Learn it:** Harrison's — *Rheumatoid Arthritis*. · Davidson's — *Rheumatoid arthritis*. · API —
*Rheumatology → RA*. [verify current edition — biologic/JAK guidance updates periodically]

### Q3. Short note: Seronegative spondyloarthropathies (5m)
<!-- id: sys10-q3 -->

**Restated:** Write a short note on the seronegative spondyloarthropathies.

- **Definition (1m):** a group of **RF-negative ("seronegative")** inflammatory arthritides sharing **axial
  (spine/sacroiliac) involvement, enthesitis, asymmetrical lower-limb oligoarthritis** and a strong
  **HLA-B27** association, with overlapping extra-articular features. (1m)
- **Members (1m):** **ankylosing spondylitis**, **psoriatic arthritis**, **reactive arthritis** (post
  GI/GU infection — Reiter's: arthritis + urethritis + conjunctivitis), **enteropathic arthritis** (IBD-
  associated), and undifferentiated spondyloarthropathy. (1m)
- **Clinical pearls (1½m):** **inflammatory back pain** (insidious, age <40, morning stiffness, improves
  with exercise / worse with rest, nocturnal); **enthesitis** (e.g. Achilles/plantar), **dactylitis**
  ("sausage digit"); **extra-articular** — anterior **uveitis**, psoriasis, IBD, aortitis. AS → **"bamboo
  spine"** and reduced chest expansion (positive **Schober's test**). (1½m)
- **Investigations + management (1½m):** HLA-B27 (supportive, not diagnostic), raised ESR/CRP, **X-ray/MRI
  sacroiliitis**; manage with **physiotherapy + NSAIDs** (first-line for axial disease), **anti-TNF / IL-17
  inhibitors** for refractory axial disease, csDMARDs (methotrexate/sulfasalazine) for peripheral arthritis;
  treat the underlying trigger/associated disease. (1½m)

**What to tabulate:** [ ] **The five spondyloarthropathies + their defining feature/association** — ~1m

**Learn it:** Harrison's — *The Spondyloarthritides*. · Davidson's — *Seronegative spondyloarthropathies*. ·
API — *Rheumatology*. [verify current edition]

### Q4. Short note: Systemic sclerosis (5m)
<!-- id: sys10-q4 -->

**Restated:** Write a short note on systemic sclerosis (scleroderma).

- **Definition (1m):** a multisystem autoimmune connective-tissue disease characterised by **excessive
  collagen deposition (fibrosis)** of skin and internal organs, **vasculopathy**, and disease-specific
  autoantibodies. (1m)
- **Subtypes (1m):** **limited cutaneous** SSc (skin distal to elbows/knees + face; includes the **CREST**
  pattern — Calcinosis, Raynaud's, oEsophageal dysmotility, Sclerodactyly, Telangiectasia; **anti-
  centromere** antibody) vs **diffuse cutaneous** SSc (proximal/truncal skin, early organ involvement;
  **anti-Scl-70 / anti-topoisomerase** antibody). (1m)
- **Clinical features (1½m):** almost universal **Raynaud's phenomenon** (often first); skin **tightening,
  sclerodactyly, digital ulcers/pits**, beaked nose, microstomia; GI **dysphagia/reflux**; **interstitial
  lung disease** and **pulmonary arterial hypertension** (major causes of death); **scleroderma renal
  crisis** (accelerated hypertension + AKI). (1½m)
- **Management (1½m):** **organ-targeted** — Raynaud's: cold avoidance + **calcium-channel blockers**
  (nifedipine); **renal crisis: ACE inhibitors** (life-saving — do not withhold for renal impairment);
  PAH: endothelin antagonists/PDE-5 inhibitors; ILD: immunosuppression (mycophenolate) ± antifibrotics;
  reflux: PPIs. No therapy reverses fibrosis — treat early. (1½m)

**What to tabulate:** [ ] **Limited (CREST, anti-centromere) vs diffuse (anti-Scl-70) SSc** — ~1m

**Learn it:** Harrison's — *Systemic Sclerosis (Scleroderma)*. · Davidson's — *Systemic sclerosis*. ·
API — *Rheumatology*. [verify current edition]

### Q5. Short note: Vasculitis — overview and classification (5m)
<!-- id: sys10-q5 -->

**Restated:** Write a short note on the vasculitides — definition and classification.

- **Definition (1m):** a heterogeneous group of disorders defined by **inflammation of blood-vessel walls**,
  causing vessel destruction (haemorrhage) and/or stenosis/occlusion (ischaemia, infarction); may be
  **primary** (idiopathic) or **secondary** (infection, drugs, connective-tissue disease, malignancy). (1m)
- **Classification by vessel size (2m):**
  - **Large-vessel:** **Giant-cell (temporal) arteritis** (elderly, headache/jaw claudication/visual loss,
    high ESR, link to polymyalgia rheumatica) and **Takayasu arteritis** (young women, "pulseless disease"). (1m)
  - **Medium-vessel:** **polyarteritis nodosa** (HBV-associated, spares lungs, microaneurysms) and
    **Kawasaki disease** (children, coronary aneurysms). (½m)
  - **Small-vessel:** **ANCA-associated** — granulomatosis with polyangiitis (c-ANCA/anti-PR3),
    eosinophilic GPA, microscopic polyangiitis (p-ANCA/anti-MPO); and **immune-complex** — IgA vasculitis
    (Henoch-Schönlein), cryoglobulinaemic. (½m)
- **Clinical clues (1m):** systemic features (fever, weight loss, raised ESR/CRP) **+ multi-organ
  ischaemia** — palpable purpura, mononeuritis multiplex, glomerulonephritis (pulmonary-renal syndrome),
  digital infarcts. Confirm by **ANCA, biopsy, or angiography**. (1m)
- **Principle of management (1m):** **corticosteroids** + immunosuppression (**cyclophosphamide or
  rituximab** for severe ANCA-associated/organ-threatening disease) for induction, then maintenance
  (azathioprine/methotrexate/rituximab); treat secondary causes (e.g. antivirals for HBV-PAN). (1m)

**What to tabulate:** [ ] **Vessel size → named vasculitis → marker** (large/medium/small with ANCA links) — ~1m

**Learn it:** Harrison's — *The Vasculitis Syndromes*. · Davidson's — *Vasculitis*. · API — *Rheumatology →
Vasculitides*. [verify current edition — Chapel Hill nomenclature]

### Q6. Short note: Gout (5m)
<!-- id: sys10-q6 -->

**Restated:** Write a short note on gout.

- **Definition (1m):** a crystal-induced arthropathy caused by deposition of **monosodium urate (MSU)
  crystals** in and around joints due to **hyperuricaemia**, presenting as recurrent acute inflammatory
  arthritis and, if untreated, chronic tophaceous disease. (1m)
- **Pathogenesis / precipitants (1m):** hyperuricaemia from **urate underexcretion** (commonest; renal
  disease, diuretics) or **overproduction** (high purine intake, alcohol/beer, fructose, myeloproliferative
  disease, tumour lysis). Attacks triggered by trauma, surgery, dehydration, starting urate-lowering
  therapy. (1m)
- **Clinical features (1½m):** sudden, severe, very **painful monoarthritis**, classically the **first
  metatarsophalangeal joint (podagra)**; red, hot, swollen. Chronic disease → **tophi** (helix of ear,
  fingers, Achilles), urate nephrolithiasis. **Synovial fluid: negatively birefringent, needle-shaped MSU
  crystals** under polarised light (definitive). (1½m)
- **Management (1½m):** **Acute attack** — **NSAIDs** OR **colchicine** (e.g. 0.5 mg 2–3×/day) OR
  **corticosteroids**; rest the joint. **Colchicine** is given low-dose — ACR favours **1.2 mg at once,
  then 0.6 mg 1 hour later** (cumulative <2 mg/day; older 0.5 mg 2–3×/day regimens are also used) — high-dose
  hourly dosing is now abandoned (toxicity). **Long-term urate-lowering** (after recurrent attacks/tophi/stones):
  **allopurinol** (xanthine-oxidase inhibitor; start low, titrate to target **serum urate <6 mg/dL**) or
  febuxostat; **do not start/stop during an acute attack without cover** and **co-prescribe NSAID/colchicine
  prophylaxis** when initiating. Lifestyle: reduce alcohol, weight loss, hydration. (1½m)

**What to tabulate:** [ ] **Gout vs pseudogout (CPPD)** — crystal shape, birefringence, joint, X-ray — ~1m

**Learn it:** Harrison's — *Gout and Other Crystal-Associated Arthropathies*. · Davidson's — *Crystal
arthropathies*. · API — *Rheumatology → Gout*. [verify current edition]

### Q7. Short note: Approach to polyarthritis (5m)
<!-- id: sys10-q7 -->

**Restated:** Write a short note on the clinical approach to a patient with polyarthritis.

- **Definition (1m):** **polyarthritis** = inflammatory involvement of **≥5 joints**; the approach is to
  characterise the pattern to narrow the cause (vs monoarthritis ≤1 / oligoarthritis 2–4). (1m)
- **Characterise the pattern (1½m):** **onset & duration** (acute <6 weeks vs chronic); **symmetry**
  (symmetrical small-joint → RA, SLE; asymmetrical large-joint/axial → spondyloarthropathy);
  **inflammatory vs mechanical** (inflammatory = morning stiffness >30–60 min, improves with use, swelling/
  warmth, raised ESR/CRP; mechanical/OA = worse with use, minimal stiffness); **migratory** (rheumatic
  fever, gonococcal) vs **additive**. Note DIP involvement (OA, psoriatic) vs sparing (RA). (1½m)
- **Look for extra-articular clues (1m):** rash (SLE, psoriasis, vasculitis), photosensitivity, oral/genital
  ulcers, Raynaud's, uveitis, nail/skin psoriasis, nodules, recent infection (reactive/viral), fever. (1m)
- **Investigations (1½m):** **FBC, ESR/CRP**, RFT/LFT, urinalysis; **autoantibodies** — RF, anti-CCP, ANA
  (± anti-dsDNA/ENA if ANA positive); serum **urate**; viral serology (parvovirus, hepatitis B/C, rubella,
  chikungunya/dengue in the tropics); **X-rays** of affected joints; **joint aspiration** if effusion (cell
  count, crystals, Gram stain/culture to exclude septic arthritis). **Always exclude septic arthritis in an
  acutely hot joint.** (1½m)

+ axial / migratory / DIP) — ~1m
