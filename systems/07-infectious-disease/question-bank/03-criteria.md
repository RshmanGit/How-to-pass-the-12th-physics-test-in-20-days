### Q1. WHO dengue severity classification (5m)
<!-- id: sys07-crit-q1 -->

**Restated:** Outline the WHO (2009) classification of dengue severity and its use.

- **Principle (1m):** the **2009 WHO revised classification** stratifies dengue by **severity for clinical
  triage** into **(1) dengue without warning signs, (2) dengue with warning signs, and (3) severe dengue** —
  guiding who is managed at home vs admitted vs ICU. (1m)
- **The three categories (3m):**

| Category | Defining features |
|---|---|
| **Dengue without warning signs (Group A)** | Fever + ≥2 of: nausea/vomiting, rash, aches/pains, leucopenia, positive tourniquet test; **no warning signs** |
| **Dengue with warning signs (Group B)** | The above **plus any warning sign**: abdominal pain/tenderness, persistent vomiting, clinical fluid accumulation, mucosal bleeding, lethargy/restlessness, **liver >2 cm**, rising haematocrit with rapid platelet fall |
| **Severe dengue (Group C)** | **(a) Severe plasma leakage** → shock (DSS) or fluid accumulation with respiratory distress; **(b) severe bleeding**; **(c) severe organ involvement** (AST/ALT ≥1000, impaired consciousness, heart/other organ failure) |

  (3m for the full table; ½m partial if categories named without their defining features.)

- **Use / interpretation (1m):** Group A → outpatient with daily review; **Group B → admit** for IV fluids
  and monitoring; **Group C → ICU/emergency** fluid resuscitation. Identifying **warning signs** marks the
  start of the **critical (leakage) phase** — the key decision point. (1m)

### Q2. SIRS criteria and qSOFA for sepsis (5m)
<!-- id: sys07-crit-q2 -->

**Restated:** Describe the SIRS criteria and the qSOFA score used in sepsis, and their use.

- **Purpose (1m):** **SIRS** (Systemic Inflammatory Response Syndrome) and **qSOFA** (quick SOFA) are bedside
  tools to **flag patients at risk of sepsis/deterioration**. Under **Sepsis-3**, sepsis = infection **+
  organ dysfunction** (SOFA rise ≥2); qSOFA is the rapid screen, SIRS the older inflammatory marker. (1m)
- **The two criteria sets (3m):**

| Tool | Criteria (positive if…) |
|---|---|
| **SIRS** (≥2 of 4) | **Temp** >38 °C or <36 °C · **HR** >90/min · **RR** >20/min (or PaCO₂ <32 mmHg) · **WBC** >12,000 or <4,000/µL (or >10% bands) |
| **qSOFA** (≥2 of 3) | **RR ≥22/min** · **altered mentation** (GCS <15) · **SBP ≤100 mmHg** |

  (3m for both sets fully reproduced; ½m partial if a tool is named without its threshold values.)

- **Use / interpretation (1m):** **qSOFA ≥2** outside ICU identifies higher risk of poor outcome → escalate,
  investigate for organ dysfunction and start the **sepsis Hour-1 bundle** (cultures, lactate, antibiotics,
  fluids). SIRS ≥2 is sensitive but **non-specific** (occurs in non-infective states), which is why Sepsis-3
  moved to SOFA/qSOFA. (1m)

**Worked example (interpretation):** A patient with **RR 26, confused (GCS 13), SBP 95** meets **all 3 qSOFA
criteria (score 3 ≥ 2)** → high risk → activate the sepsis bundle.
