### Q1. Short note: Scrub typhus (5m)
<!-- id: sys07-saq-q1 -->

**Restated:** Write a short note on scrub typhus.

- **Definition (1m):** an acute **zoonotic rickettsial** infection by **Orientia tsutsugamushi**, transmitted
  by the bite of the larval **trombiculid mite (chigger)**; common in rural/agricultural India, a leading
  cause of acute febrile illness with thrombocytopenia. (1m)
- **Clinical features (1½m):** fever, headache, myalgia, lymphadenopathy; the pathognomonic **eschar** (a
  painless black necrotic crust at the bite site) — look in skin folds/groin/axilla; rash; complications —
  **pneumonitis/ARDS, meningoencephalitis, AKI, hepatitis, myocarditis, MODS**. (1½m)
- **Investigations (1½m):** **IgM ELISA** (mainstay); **Weil-Felix (OX-K agglutination)** — cheap, supportive;
  PCR from eschar/blood; supportive — thrombocytopenia, transaminitis. (1½m)
- **Management (1m):** **doxycycline 100 mg BD** (first-line; dramatic defervescence in 48 h) **or
  azithromycin 500 mg/day** (preferred in pregnancy/children) for ~7 days; supportive/ICU care for severe
  disease. (1m)

**What to tabulate:** [ ] **Eschar + the rickettsial triad** (fever + eschar + rash) and complications — ~½m

### Q2. Short note: Extrapulmonary tuberculosis (5m)
<!-- id: sys07-saq-q2 -->

**Restated:** Write a short note on extrapulmonary tuberculosis (EPTB).

- **Definition (1m):** tuberculosis involving **organs other than the lungs**, from haematogenous/lymphatic
  spread of *Mycobacterium tuberculosis*; more common in children, the immunosuppressed and HIV
  co-infection. (1m)
- **Common sites / features (1½m):** **lymph node (commonest — cervical, "scrofula")**, **pleural effusion**
  (exudative, lymphocytic, high ADA), **spine (Pott's disease)**, TB meningitis, abdominal/peritoneal,
  genitourinary, pericardial, miliary (disseminated). (1½m)
- **Investigations (1½m):** tissue/fluid for **AFB, CBNAAT/GeneXpert (detects rifampicin resistance)** and
  **culture**; histopathology — **caseating granuloma**; fluid analysis (lymphocytic, **high ADA**, low
  glucose); imaging of the involved site. (1½m)
- **Management (1m):** **anti-tubercular therapy (RIPE — rifampicin, isoniazid, pyrazinamide, ethambutol)**
  intensive phase then continuation; **extended duration** for CNS/bone/disseminated (often 9–12 months);
  **adjunctive corticosteroids** for TB meningitis and pericarditis. (1m)

**What to tabulate:** [ ] **EPTB site ⇒ key clue/test** (lymph node / pleura / spine / meninges) — ~½m

**Learn it:** Harrison's — *Tuberculosis*. · Davidson's — *Tuberculosis*. · API — *TB / RNTCP–NTEP guidelines*.

### Q3. Short note: HIV/AIDS and antiretroviral therapy (ART) overview (5m)
<!-- id: sys07-saq-q3 -->

**Restated:** Write a short note on HIV/AIDS and an overview of antiretroviral therapy.

- **Definition (1m):** **HIV** is a retrovirus that infects and depletes **CD4+ T lymphocytes**, causing
  progressive immunodeficiency; **AIDS** = advanced HIV defined by an **AIDS-defining illness or CD4 <200
  cells/µL**. Spread sexually, by blood, and vertically (mother-to-child). (1m)
- **Clinical course (1½m):** acute seroconversion illness → asymptomatic latency → symptomatic/AIDS with
  **opportunistic infections** (PCP/*Pneumocystis*, TB, oesophageal candidiasis, cryptococcal meningitis,
  toxoplasmosis, CMV) and HIV-associated malignancies (Kaposi's, lymphoma). (1½m)
- **Diagnosis & monitoring (1½m):** screening **ELISA → confirmatory** test (rapid antibody/antigen);
  **CD4 count** (immune status) and **viral load** (treatment response/monitoring). (1½m)
- **ART overview (1m):** **start ART in all, regardless of CD4** ("test and treat"); standard first-line is a
  **combination of ≥3 drugs**, commonly **two NRTIs + an integrase inhibitor** (e.g.
  **tenofovir + lamivudine + dolutegravir, "TLD"**); add **OI prophylaxis** (e.g. co-trimoxazole when CD4
  low); lifelong adherence to suppress viral load. (1m)

**What to tabulate:** [ ] **CD4 thresholds ⇒ opportunistic infection risk** (e.g. <200 PCP, <100 toxo, <50 CMV) — ~½m

**Learn it:** Harrison's — *Human Immunodeficiency Virus Disease: AIDS*. · Davidson's — *HIV infection and
AIDS*. · API — *HIV / NACO ART guidelines*. [verify current edition]

### Q4. Short note: Recognition of sepsis (5m)
<!-- id: sys07-saq-q4 -->

**Restated:** Write a short note on the recognition (and early management) of sepsis.

- **Definition (1m):** **Sepsis = life-threatening organ dysfunction caused by a dysregulated host response
  to infection** (Sepsis-3); **septic shock** = sepsis with persisting hypotension needing vasopressors to
  keep MAP ≥65 mmHg **and** lactate >2 mmol/L despite adequate fluids. (1m)
- **Recognition (1½m):** suspected/confirmed infection **+ organ dysfunction** — use **qSOFA** (≥2 of: RR ≥22,
  altered mentation, SBP ≤100) at the bedside and the **SOFA score** in ICU; warning signs — fever/hypothermia,
  tachycardia, tachypnoea, hypotension, oliguria, confusion, mottling, rising lactate. (1½m)
- **Investigations (1½m):** **blood cultures (before antibiotics)** + cultures from suspected sites, **serum
  lactate**, FBC, renal/liver function, coagulation, ABG; imaging/source identification. (1½m)
- **Early management — "Hour-1 bundle" (1m):** measure lactate, take cultures, give **broad-spectrum IV
  antibiotics within 1 hour**, **30 mL/kg crystalloid** for hypotension/lactate ≥4, start **vasopressors
  (noradrenaline first-line)** if hypotensive after fluids; **source control**. (1m)

**What to tabulate:** [ ] **qSOFA 3 criteria + Hour-1 bundle steps** — ~1m

**Learn it:** Harrison's — *Sepsis and Septic Shock*. · Davidson's — *Sepsis*. · Surviving Sepsis Campaign
guidelines. [verify current edition]

### Q5. Short note: Rational antibiotic use and antimicrobial resistance (5m)
<!-- id: sys07-saq-q5 -->

**Restated:** Write a short note on rational antibiotic use and antimicrobial resistance (AMR).

- **Definition (1m):** **Antimicrobial resistance (AMR)** = the ability of microorganisms to survive drugs that
  previously killed/inhibited them; driven by **misuse and overuse** of antibiotics — a major global and
  Indian public-health threat. **Antimicrobial stewardship** = coordinated efforts to optimise antibiotic
  use. (1m)
- **Mechanisms of resistance (1m):** enzymatic inactivation (e.g. **β-lactamases, ESBL, carbapenemases**),
  target alteration (e.g. MRSA *mecA*), reduced uptake/efflux pumps; spread via **plasmids/transposons**. (1m)
- **Principles of rational use (1½m):** prescribe only when bacterial infection is likely; **send cultures
  before starting**; choose the **narrowest effective agent** at the right dose/duration; **de-escalate** on
  culture results; avoid antibiotics for viral illness; complete (or appropriately shorten) the course;
  **antibiotic policy/restricted drugs** locally. (1½m)
- **Containment / pearls (1½m):** infection prevention (hand hygiene, vaccination), surveillance, the
  **WHO AWaRe classification (Access / Watch / Reserve)**, regulating over-the-counter sales, and limiting
  agricultural use; multidisciplinary stewardship programmes. (1½m)

**What to tabulate:** [ ] **Resistance mechanism ⇒ example organism/drug** (β-lactamase / MRSA / ESBL / CRE) — ~½m

**Learn it:** Harrison's — *Antibacterial Agents / Treatment & Prophylaxis of Bacterial Infections*. ·
Davidson's — *Principles of infectious disease — antimicrobial therapy*. · WHO AWaRe & national AMR policy.
