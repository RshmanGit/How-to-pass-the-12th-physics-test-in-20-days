### Q1. CURB-65 score for community-acquired pneumonia (5m)
<!-- id: sys05-crit-q1 -->

**Restated:** Describe the CURB-65 score and its use in community-acquired pneumonia.

- **Purpose (1m):** a validated bedside **severity score for CAP** that guides **site of care** (home vs
  ward vs ICU) and predicts mortality. One point for each of five criteria — total **0–5**. (1m)
- **The five components (2m):**

| Criterion | Threshold scoring 1 point |
|---|---|
| **C** — Confusion | new mental confusion (AMT ≤ 8 / disorientation) |
| **U** — Urea | blood urea **> 7 mmol/L** (≈ BUN > 19 mg/dL) |
| **R** — Respiratory rate | **≥ 30 / min** |
| **B** — Blood pressure | **systolic < 90 mmHg** or **diastolic ≤ 60 mmHg** |
| **65** — Age | **≥ 65 years** |

  (2m for the full table; 1m partial if components named without thresholds.)

- **Grading & use (1m):** **0–1 → low risk, outpatient/home**; **2 → consider short admission/supervised
  ward**; **3–5 → severe, admit, assess for ICU** (≥3 carries high mortality). (1m)
- **Worked example (1m):** a **70-year-old** (1, age) with **RR 32** (1) and **systolic BP 88 mmHg** (1),
  no confusion, urea normal → **CURB-65 = 3 → severe pneumonia, admit and assess for ICU.** (1m)

### Q2. Light's criteria — exudate vs transudate in pleural effusion (5m)
<!-- id: sys05-crit-q2 -->

**Restated:** State Light's criteria for distinguishing an exudative from a transudative pleural effusion.

- **Principle (1m):** Light's criteria classify a pleural effusion as an **exudate** if **any one** of three
  criteria is met; if **none** are met it is a **transudate**. They compare pleural-fluid protein and LDH
  against serum — a high yield, sensitive rule (paired pleural + serum samples required). (1m)
- **The three criteria — exudate if ANY is true (2m):**

| # | Criterion | Cut-off (exudate) |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Pleural-fluid protein / serum protein ratio | **> 0.5** |
| 2 | Pleural-fluid LDH / serum LDH ratio | **> 0.6** |
| 3 | Pleural-fluid LDH | **> ⅔ (0.67×) the upper limit of normal serum LDH** |

  (2m for the full table; 1m partial if the three ratios are named without cut-offs.)

- **Use / interpretation (1m):** **transudate** (none met) → systemic cause: heart failure, cirrhosis,
  nephrotic syndrome, hypoalbuminaemia; **exudate** (≥1 met) → local cause: infection (TB, parapneumonic/
  empyema), malignancy, pulmonary embolism, inflammatory. (1m)
- **Worked example (1m):** pleural protein/serum protein **= 0.6 (> 0.5)** → **criterion 1 met → exudate**,
  even if the LDH ratios were normal (any single positive criterion suffices). (1m)
