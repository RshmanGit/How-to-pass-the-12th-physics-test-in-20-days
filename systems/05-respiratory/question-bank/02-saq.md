### Q1. Short note: Interstitial lung disease (5m)
<!-- id: sys05-saq-q1 -->

**Restated:** Write a short note on interstitial lung disease (ILD).

- **Definition (1m):** a heterogeneous group of disorders causing diffuse **inflammation and/or fibrosis of
  the lung interstitium** (alveolar walls), producing a **restrictive** ventilatory defect with impaired gas
  transfer. (1m)
- **Causes / types (1½m):** **idiopathic pulmonary fibrosis (IPF)**; connective-tissue-disease–associated
  (RA, SLE, systemic sclerosis); **hypersensitivity pneumonitis** (organic dust/birds); occupational
  **pneumoconioses** (silicosis, asbestosis); sarcoidosis; drug-induced (amiodarone, methotrexate, bleomycin). (1½m)
- **Clinical features (1m):** progressive **exertional dyspnoea** and dry cough; **fine end-inspiratory
  "Velcro" crepitations** (bibasal), **clubbing** (esp. IPF); later cyanosis and cor pulmonale. (1m)
- **Investigations (1m):** **HRCT thorax** (reticular/ground-glass; **honeycombing** in IPF);
  **spirometry — restrictive pattern (reduced FVC, normal/raised FEV1/FVC) with reduced DLCO**; autoimmune
  serology; lung biopsy in selected cases. (1m)
- **Management (½m):** treat the cause/remove exposure; **antifibrotics (pirfenidone, nintedanib) for IPF**;
  immunosuppression for inflammatory/CTD-ILD; oxygen, rehab, transplant in end-stage. (½m)

**What to tabulate:** [ ] **Obstructive vs restrictive spirometry** (FEV1/FVC, FVC, DLCO) — ~1m

### Q2. Short note: Approach to pleural effusion (5m)
<!-- id: sys05-saq-q2 -->

**Restated:** Write a short note on the approach to pleural effusion.

- **Definition (1m):** abnormal accumulation of fluid in the **pleural space**; classified physiologically
  into **transudate** vs **exudate**. (1m)
- **Clinical features (1m):** breathlessness, pleuritic pain, dry cough; signs — reduced expansion,
  **stony-dull percussion**, **absent breath sounds and reduced vocal resonance** over the effusion. (1m)
- **Diagnostic approach (1½m):** **CXR** (blunted costophrenic angle, meniscus; ≥200–300 mL to show);
  **ultrasound-guided pleural aspiration (thoracocentesis)**; analyse fluid — protein, LDH, glucose, pH,
  cytology, Gram/AFB, ADA; apply **Light's criteria** to classify exudate vs transudate (see
  `03-criteria.md`). (1½m)
- **Causes (1m):** **transudate** — heart failure, cirrhosis, nephrotic syndrome, hypoalbuminaemia;
  **exudate** — TB (high ADA, lymphocytic), parapneumonic/empyema, malignancy, pulmonary embolism. (1m)
- **Management (½m):** treat the underlying cause; therapeutic drainage for large/symptomatic effusions;
  **intercostal drain for empyema**; pleurodesis for recurrent malignant effusion. (½m)

**What to tabulate:** [ ] **Transudate vs exudate causes** (and Light's cut-offs) — ~1m

**Learn it:** Harrison's — *Disorders of the Pleura*. · Davidson's — *Pleural disease*. [verify current edition]

### Q3. Short note: Bronchiectasis (5m)
<!-- id: sys05-saq-q3 -->

**Restated:** Write a short note on bronchiectasis.

- **Definition (1m):** **permanent, abnormal dilatation of bronchi** with destruction of their elastic and
  muscular walls, due to chronic infection/inflammation. (1m)
- **Causes (1m):** post-infective (**childhood pneumonia, TB, measles, whooping cough** — common in India),
  **cystic fibrosis**, ciliary dyskinesia, ABPA, immunodeficiency, bronchial obstruction. (1m)
- **Clinical features (1½m):** **chronic cough with copious purulent sputum** (often foul-smelling),
  recurrent chest infections, **haemoptysis**; coarse crepitations, **clubbing**; recurrent exacerbations. (1½m)
- **Investigations (1m):** **HRCT thorax is the investigation of choice** — dilated bronchi with
  **"signet-ring" sign**, thickened walls, lack of tapering; sputum culture; spirometry (obstructive);
  tests for the cause (sweat chloride, immunoglobulins). (1m)
- **Management (½m):** **airway clearance/chest physiotherapy** + postural drainage; antibiotics for
  exacerbations (sputum-guided); bronchodilators; treat the cause; surgery for localised disease/massive
  haemoptysis. (½m)

**What to tabulate:** [ ] **Causes of bronchiectasis** (post-infective / congenital / obstructive) — ~½m

**Learn it:** Harrison's — *Bronchiectasis*. · Davidson's — *Respiratory → Bronchiectasis*. [verify edition]

### Q4. Short note: Respiratory failure and oxygen therapy (5m)
<!-- id: sys05-saq-q4 -->

**Restated:** Write a short note on respiratory failure and oxygen therapy.

- **Definition (1m):** failure of gas exchange — **PaO2 < 60 mmHg** (8 kPa) on room air, **± PaCO2 >
  50 mmHg** (6.7 kPa). (1m)
- **Types (1½m):** **Type I (hypoxaemic)** — low PaO2 with normal/low PaCO2 (V/Q mismatch — pneumonia,
  ARDS, PE, oedema); **Type II (hypercapnic / ventilatory)** — low PaO2 **with high PaCO2** (alveolar
  hypoventilation — COPD, neuromuscular disease, chest-wall disease, opioids). (1½m)
- **Clinical features (1m):** **hypoxia** — dyspnoea, cyanosis, restlessness, tachycardia; **hypercapnia** —
  drowsiness, flapping tremor (asterixis), warm peripheries, bounding pulse, headache, confusion. (1m)
- **Oxygen therapy (1m):** treat the cause; **Type I — high-flow O2 to target SpO2 94–98%**; **Type II /
  COPD at risk of CO2 retention — controlled O2 to target SpO2 88–92%** (Venturi mask); **NIV (BiPAP)** for
  persistent hypercapnic acidosis, invasive ventilation if it fails. (1m)
- **Pearl (½m):** uncontrolled high-flow O2 in chronic type-II failure can cause **CO2 narcosis** — titrate
  to target saturation and recheck ABG. (½m)

**What to tabulate:** [ ] **Type I vs Type II respiratory failure** (PaO2, PaCO2, O2 target, causes) — ~1m

**Learn it:** Harrison's — *Respiratory Failure*. · Davidson's — *Respiratory failure & oxygen therapy*.
