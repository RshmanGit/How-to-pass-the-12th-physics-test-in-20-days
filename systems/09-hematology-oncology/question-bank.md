### Q1. Approach to a patient with anaemia — classification, clinical features, investigations and management (10m)
<!-- id: sys09-q1 -->

**Restated:** Discuss the clinical approach to a patient with anaemia, including morphological
classification, investigations and principles of management.

**Definition (1m)**
- Anaemia = a reduction in **haemoglobin concentration below the age/sex-specific reference**
  (WHO: <13 g/dL in men, <12 g/dL in non-pregnant women, <11 g/dL in pregnancy), reflecting reduced
  red-cell oxygen-carrying capacity. (1m)

**Aetiopathogenesis / classification (3m)**
- **Morphological (by MCV) — the exam workhorse (2m):**
  - **Microcytic (MCV <80 fL):** iron deficiency (commonest), thalassaemia, anaemia of chronic disease
    (late), sideroblastic. (½m)
  - **Normocytic (MCV 80–100 fL):** acute blood loss, anaemia of chronic disease, haemolysis (early),
    renal anaemia, marrow failure. (½m)
  - **Macrocytic (MCV >100 fL):** megaloblastic (B12/folate deficiency) vs non-megaloblastic (alcohol,
    liver disease, hypothyroidism, myelodysplasia, reticulocytosis). (1m)
- **Kinetic mechanism (1m):** decreased production (marrow failure, deficiency), increased destruction
  (haemolysis), or blood loss — the **reticulocyte count** separates hypoproliferative (low retics) from
  haemolysis/blood loss (high retics). (1m)

**Clinical features (2m)**
- **General (anaemic syndrome):** fatigue, exertional dyspnoea, palpitations, pallor (conjunctival/palmar),
  tachycardia, flow murmur; in severe/decompensated disease — angina, heart failure. (1m)
- **Cause-specific clues:** koilonychia/angular stomatitis/pica (iron deficiency); glossitis + peripheral
  neuropathy/subacute combined degeneration (B12); jaundice + splenomegaly (haemolysis); bone pain/
  bleeding/infections (marrow infiltration). (1m)

**Investigations (2m)**
- **First line:** **FBC with indices (MCV, MCH, RDW)**, **peripheral blood film**, **reticulocyte count**. (1m)
- **Targeted by morphology:** iron studies (ferritin, TIBC, transferrin saturation) for microcytic;
  **B12, folate** for macrocytic; haemolysis screen (LDH↑, indirect bilirubin↑, haptoglobin↓, DCT/Coombs);
  renal/liver/thyroid function; **bone marrow examination** if pancytopenia or unexplained. (1m)

**Management (2m)**
- **Treat the cause, not just the number:** oral iron (ferrous sulphate 200 mg = 65 mg elemental,
  1–3×/day) for iron deficiency + find the source of loss; **IM/IV B12 (hydroxocobalamin)** for B12
  deficiency, **oral folic acid 5 mg/day** for folate (give B12 first/together to avoid precipitating
  subacute combined degeneration); erythropoietin for renal anaemia. (1m)
- **Transfuse packed red cells** only for symptomatic/severe anaemia (e.g. Hb <7 g/dL, or <8 with cardiac
  disease) — restrictive threshold; correct the underlying disorder. (1m)

- [ ] **Table — iron deficiency vs anaemia of chronic disease vs thalassaemia trait** (ferritin, TIBC, RDW) — ~1m

### Q2. Acute leukaemias — classification, clinical features, investigations and management (10m)
<!-- id: sys09-q2 -->

**Restated:** Discuss the classification, clinical features, investigations and management of acute
leukaemias (AML and ALL).

**Definition (1m)**
- Acute leukaemia = a **malignant clonal proliferation of immature haematopoietic precursors (blasts)** in
  the bone marrow (≥20% blasts), with marrow failure and frequent peripheral-blood involvement;
  **acute myeloid (AML)** vs **acute lymphoblastic (ALL)**. (1m)

**Aetiopathogenesis / classification (2m)**
- **Pathogenesis:** acquired genetic lesions → maturation arrest + uncontrolled proliferation of blasts →
  marrow replacement → cytopenias. (½m)
- **AML** — adults/elderly; FAB/WHO subtypes; **Auer rods**; **APML (t(15;17), PML-RARA)** is an emergency
  (DIC). **ALL** — commonest childhood cancer; B- and T-lineage; **t(9;22) Philadelphia** confers worse
  prognosis. (1m)
- **Risk factors:** prior chemo/radiation, benzene, Down syndrome, antecedent MDS/myeloproliferative
  disease. (½m)

**Clinical features (2m)**
- **Marrow failure:** anaemia (fatigue, pallor), **neutropenia** (fever/infections), **thrombocytopenia**
  (bruising, bleeding, petechiae). (1m)
- **Infiltration:** hepatosplenomegaly, lymphadenopathy (more in ALL), bone pain, gum hypertrophy (monocytic
  AML), CNS or testicular involvement (ALL); **APML → bleeding/DIC**. (1m)

**Investigations (2m)**
- **FBC + film:** anaemia, thrombocytopenia, variable WCC with **circulating blasts**. (½m)
- **Bone marrow aspirate/biopsy — diagnostic:** **≥20% blasts**; **immunophenotyping (flow cytometry)** to
  assign lineage; **cytogenetics/molecular** (t(15;17), t(9;22), FLT3, etc.) for risk and targeted therapy. (1m)
- **Supportive work-up:** coagulation/DIC screen (esp. APML), LDH/urate, renal function, **tumour lysis**
  labs, LP for CNS disease in ALL. (½m)

**Management (3m)**
- **General/supportive (1m):** blood-product support, **neutropenic-sepsis protocol** (broad-spectrum
  antibiotics within 1 h of fever), tumour-lysis prophylaxis (hydration + **allopurinol/rasburicase**),
  central venous access. (1m)
- **AML (1m):** **induction "7+3"** — cytarabine (continuous infusion ×7 days) + an anthracycline
  (daunorubicin ×3 days) → consolidation; **allogeneic stem-cell transplant** for high-risk. **APML** —
  **all-trans retinoic acid (ATRA) ± arsenic trioxide** (highly curable; start ATRA on suspicion). (1m)
- **ALL (1m):** prolonged multi-agent protocol — **induction → consolidation → CNS prophylaxis (intrathecal
  methotrexate) → maintenance (≈2–3 years)**; add a **tyrosine-kinase inhibitor (imatinib/dasatinib)** if
  Philadelphia-positive. (1m)

**What to draw / tabulate (independent marks):**
- [ ] **Table — AML vs ALL** (age, markers, Auer rods, cytogenetics, regimen) — ~1m
- [ ] **Flowchart — febrile neutropenia pathway** (fever → cultures → empirical antibiotics ≤1 h) — ~½m

**Learn it:** Harrison's — *Acute Myeloid Leukemia* / *Acute Lymphoid Leukemia*. · Davidson's — *Leukaemias*. ·
API — *Haematology → Acute leukaemia*. [verify current edition — regimens evolve]

### Q3. Lymphoma — Hodgkin vs non-Hodgkin: classification, clinical features, investigations and management (10m)
<!-- id: sys09-q3 -->

**Restated:** Compare Hodgkin and non-Hodgkin lymphoma; discuss clinical features, investigations,
staging and principles of management.

**Definition (1m)**
- Lymphoma = a **malignant clonal proliferation of lymphoid cells**, arising mainly in lymph nodes/lymphoid
  tissue; **Hodgkin lymphoma (HL)** is defined by **Reed-Sternberg cells**, while **non-Hodgkin lymphoma
  (NHL)** is a heterogeneous group of B- and T-cell tumours. (1m)

**Aetiopathogenesis / classification (2m)**
- **HL:** bimodal age (young adults + elderly); **EBV** association; spreads **contiguously** node-to-node;
  classical (nodular sclerosing commonest) vs nodular lymphocyte-predominant. (1m)
- **NHL:** mostly B-cell; **indolent** (follicular) vs **aggressive** (diffuse large B-cell — commonest) vs
  **highly aggressive** (Burkitt — c-MYC, "starry-sky"); spreads **non-contiguously/extranodal**;
  associations — immunosuppression/HIV, EBV, H. pylori (MALT), HTLV-1, autoimmune disease. (1m)

**Clinical features (2m)**
- **Painless lymphadenopathy** (rubbery, firm); **B symptoms** = fever, drenching night sweats, weight loss
  >10% in 6 months; hepatosplenomegaly. (1m)
- **HL-specific clues:** Pel-Ebstein fever, **alcohol-induced nodal pain**, mediastinal mass. **NHL:**
  more often extranodal (GI, skin, CNS), marrow involvement, more advanced at presentation. (1m)

**Investigations (2m)**
- **Excisional lymph-node biopsy — diagnostic** (immunohistochemistry; **CD15/CD30 + Reed-Sternberg** in
  classical HL). (1m)
- **Staging:** **CT / PET-CT** (FDG); bone-marrow biopsy if indicated; **Ann Arbor stage I–IV ± A/B
  (B symptoms) ± E (extranodal)/S (spleen)**; FBC, LDH (prognosis), ESR, HIV/hepatitis serology, baseline
  cardiac/pulmonary function before therapy. (1m)

**Management (3m)**
- **Hodgkin lymphoma (1½m):** chemotherapy **ABVD** (Adriamycin/doxorubicin, Bleomycin, Vinblastine,
  Dacarbazine) ± **involved-field radiotherapy** for early-stage; highly curable. Monitor for
  bleomycin lung toxicity and anthracycline cardiotoxicity. (1½m)
- **Non-Hodgkin lymphoma (1½m):** **diffuse large B-cell → R-CHOP** (**Rituximab**, Cyclophosphamide,
  Hydroxydaunorubicin/doxorubicin, Oncovin/vincristine, Prednisolone) — anti-CD20 added to chemo;
  **indolent (follicular)** — watch-and-wait if asymptomatic; **Burkitt** — intensive regimen + **tumour-
  lysis prophylaxis** + CNS prophylaxis. (1½m)

**What to draw / tabulate (independent marks):**
- [ ] **Table — Hodgkin vs non-Hodgkin** (cell, spread, age, marrow, prognosis, regimen) — ~1½m
- [ ] **Table — Ann Arbor staging I–IV with A/B/E/S suffixes** — ~½m

**Learn it:** Harrison's — *Hodgkin's Lymphoma* / *Non-Hodgkin's Lymphoma*. · Davidson's — *Lymphomas*. ·
API — *Haematology / Oncology → Lymphoma*. [verify current edition]

### Q4. Short note: Tumour markers (5m)
<!-- id: sys09-q4 -->

**Restated:** Write a short note on tumour markers — definition, important examples and clinical use.

- **Definition (1m):** substances (proteins, hormones, antigens) produced by tumour cells or by the host in
  response to a tumour, **detectable in blood/body fluids/tissue**; mainly used for **monitoring and
  prognosis**, rarely for screening (limited sensitivity/specificity). (1m)
- **Important markers — checklist (2½m):**
  - **AFP** — hepatocellular carcinoma, non-seminomatous germ-cell tumours. (½m)
  - **CEA** — colorectal carcinoma (monitoring recurrence); also other GI/lung tumours. (½m)
  - **CA 19-9** — pancreatic and biliary carcinoma. (½m)
  - **CA 125** — epithelial ovarian carcinoma. · **CA 15-3** — breast carcinoma. (½m)
  - **PSA** — prostate carcinoma. · **β-hCG** — germ-cell tumours, choriocarcinoma, hydatidiform mole. ·
    **Calcitonin** — medullary thyroid carcinoma. · **Thyroglobulin** — differentiated thyroid cancer
    follow-up. (½m)
- **Clinical use / caveat (1½m):** best for **monitoring response and detecting recurrence**, and some for
  prognosis; **not diagnostic alone** — raised in benign conditions (e.g. CA 125 in endometriosis, CEA in
  smokers, AFP in cirrhosis/pregnancy). Exceptions used to guide therapy: **PSA** and **AFP/β-hCG** in
  testicular tumours. (1½m)

**What to tabulate:** [ ] **Marker ⇒ associated tumour table** (AFP, CEA, CA19-9, CA125, CA15-3, PSA, β-hCG,
calcitonin) — ~1m

**Learn it:** Harrison's — *Cancer Markers* / *Approach to the Patient with Cancer*. · API — *Oncology →

### Q5. Short note: Tumour lysis syndrome (5m)
<!-- id: sys09-q5 -->

**Restated:** Write a short note on tumour lysis syndrome (TLS).

- **Definition (1m):** an **oncological emergency** from massive tumour-cell breakdown (spontaneous or after
  chemotherapy) releasing intracellular contents, producing the metabolic tetrad of **hyperkalaemia,
  hyperphosphataemia, hyperuricaemia and hypocalcaemia** (the last secondary to phosphate). (1m)
- **At-risk / pathogenesis (1m):** bulky, rapidly proliferating, chemosensitive tumours — **Burkitt
  lymphoma, ALL, high-grade NHL, high-WCC acute leukaemias**; cell lysis floods nucleic acids (→ urate),
  potassium and phosphate; calcium phosphate and urate crystals deposit in tubules → **acute kidney
  injury**. (1m)
- **Clinical features (1m):** nausea/vomiting, lethargy, **cardiac arrhythmias** (hyperkalaemia),
  tetany/seizures (hypocalcaemia), oliguric acute kidney injury; usually within **12–72 h** of treatment. (1m)
- **Management (2m):** **prevention is key** — **aggressive IV hydration** + **allopurinol** (xanthine-
  oxidase inhibitor) for low/intermediate risk, **rasburicase** (recombinant urate oxidase) for high
  risk/established TLS; treat **hyperkalaemia** (calcium gluconate, insulin-dextrose, etc.); correct
  phosphate; **avoid calcium** unless symptomatic hypocalcaemia; **haemodialysis** for refractory
  hyperkalaemia/AKI; monitor electrolytes 6–8 hourly. (2m)

**What to tabulate:** [ ] **Cairo-Bishop metabolic tetrad** (K⁺↑, PO₄↑, urate↑, Ca²⁺↓) with thresholds — ~1m

**Learn it:** Harrison's — *Oncologic Emergencies → Tumor Lysis Syndrome*. · Davidson's — *Complications of
cancer*. · API — *Oncology → Oncological emergencies*. [verify current edition]

### Q6. Short note: Paraneoplastic syndromes (5m)
<!-- id: sys09-q6 -->

**Restated:** Write a short note on paraneoplastic syndromes.

- **Definition (1m):** clinical syndromes caused by a tumour **at a site distant from the primary or its
  metastases**, mediated by **hormones, cytokines or immune (autoantibody) mechanisms** rather than direct
  mass effect; may precede the cancer diagnosis. (1m)
- **Endocrine examples (1½m):** **SIADH** (small-cell lung cancer → hyponatraemia); **ectopic ACTH**
  (Cushing's — small-cell lung); **hypercalcaemia** (PTHrP — squamous-cell lung, renal, breast);
  hypoglycaemia (IGF-2). (1½m)
- **Neurological / haematological / dermatological (1½m):** **Lambert-Eaton myasthenic syndrome** and
  cerebellar degeneration (anti-Hu/anti-Yo, small-cell lung); polycythaemia (renal cell/HCC, erythropoietin);
  **acanthosis nigricans, dermatomyositis** (GI/lung); migratory thrombophlebitis (**Trousseau sign**,
  pancreatic). (1½m)
- **Principle / pearl (1m):** treating the **underlying tumour** is the definitive therapy; syndromes track
  with tumour activity and can be the first clue to malignancy. (1m)

**What to tabulate:** [ ] **Syndrome ⇒ mediator ⇒ commonest tumour** table (SIADH, ectopic ACTH,
hypercalcaemia, LEMS) — ~1m

**Learn it:** Harrison's — *Paraneoplastic Syndromes: Endocrinologic/Hematologic* & *Neurologic*. · Davidson's
— *Non-metastatic effects of malignancy*. · API — *Oncology*. [verify current edition]

### Q7. Short note: Vitamin B12 and folate deficiency (5m)
<!-- id: sys09-q7 -->

**Restated:** Write a short note on the causes, features and management of vitamin B12 and folate deficiency.

- **Definition (1m):** deficiency of vitamin B12 (cobalamin) and/or folate impairs DNA synthesis →
  **megaloblastic (macrocytic) anaemia** with ineffective erythropoiesis; both present with macrocytosis,
  but **only B12 deficiency causes neurological disease**. (1m)
- **Causes (1½m):** **B12** — **pernicious anaemia** (anti-intrinsic-factor / parietal-cell antibodies),
  gastrectomy, ileal disease/resection (terminal ileum absorption), strict veganism, metformin.
  **Folate** — poor diet/alcoholism, malabsorption, increased demand (pregnancy, haemolysis), drugs
  (methotrexate, phenytoin, trimethoprim). (1½m)
- **Clinical features (1½m):** anaemic symptoms + **glossitis, angular stomatitis**; **B12-specific
  neurology** — peripheral neuropathy and **subacute combined degeneration of the cord** (dorsal columns +
  corticospinal: paraesthesiae, ↓proprioception/vibration, ataxia, brisk knees + absent ankles). (1½m)
- **Investigations + management (1m):** macrocytosis with **hypersegmented neutrophils**, low serum
  B12/folate, raised LDH + indirect bilirubin (ineffective erythropoiesis). Treat: **IM hydroxocobalamin
  1 mg** (loading then 3-monthly maintenance) for B12; **oral folic acid 5 mg/day** for folate — **always
  correct/replace B12 before or with folate** to avoid precipitating subacute combined degeneration. (1m)

**What to tabulate:** [ ] **B12 vs folate** (source, store duration, absorption site, neurology yes/no) — ~1m

**Learn it:** Harrison's — *Megaloblastic Anemias*. · Davidson's — *Macrocytic anaemias*. · API —
*Haematology → Megaloblastic anaemia*. [verify current edition]

### Q8. Short note: Bleeding disorders — haemophilia and immune thrombocytopenia (ITP) (5m)
<!-- id: sys09-q8 -->

**Restated:** Write a short note on haemophilia and immune thrombocytopenic purpura (ITP).

- **Definition (1m):** **Haemophilia** = X-linked recessive deficiency of clotting factor — **A (factor
  VIII)** or **B (factor IX, "Christmas disease")** → a **coagulation-factor** bleeding disorder. **ITP** =
  acquired **immune-mediated platelet destruction** (anti-platelet antibodies) → a **platelet** bleeding
  disorder. (1m)
- **Clinical pattern (1½m):** **Coagulation defect (haemophilia)** — **deep bleeds**: haemarthrosis,
  muscle haematoma, delayed/prolonged bleeding after trauma/surgery, males affected. **Platelet defect
  (ITP)** — **mucocutaneous bleeds**: petechiae, purpura, epistaxis, menorrhagia, gum bleeding. (1½m)
- **Investigations (1½m):** **Haemophilia** — **prolonged aPTT** with normal PT/platelets/bleeding time,
  corrected by mixing study; **reduced factor VIII or IX assay** (severity by level: <1% severe). **ITP** —
  **isolated thrombocytopenia** with normal PT/aPTT, normal/increased marrow megakaryocytes; diagnosis of
  exclusion. (1½m)
- **Management (1m):** **Haemophilia** — replace the deficient factor (**recombinant factor VIII/IX**),
  desmopressin for mild haemophilia A, tranexamic acid for mucosal bleeds; avoid IM injections/NSAIDs.
  **ITP** — treat if bleeding or very low count: **corticosteroids** ± **IVIG** (first line), then
  thrombopoietin-receptor agonists (eltrombopag) / rituximab / splenectomy. (1m)

**What to tabulate:** [ ] **Coagulation vs platelet bleeding** (site, PT/aPTT/platelets/BT pattern) — ~1m

**Learn it:** Harrison's — *Coagulation Disorders* / *Disorders of Platelets and Vessel Wall*. · Davidson's
— *Bleeding disorders*. · API — *Haematology → Bleeding disorders*. [verify current edition]

### Q9. Short note: Multiple myeloma (5m)
<!-- id: sys09-q9 -->

**Restated:** Write a short note on multiple myeloma.

- **Definition (1m):** a malignant clonal proliferation of **plasma cells** in the bone marrow producing a
  **monoclonal immunoglobulin (M-protein/paraprotein)**, with end-organ damage. (1m)
- **Clinical features — "CRAB" (1½m):** **C**alcium elevated (hypercalcaemia — thirst, confusion,
  constipation); **R**enal impairment (light-chain cast nephropathy); **A**naemia (marrow infiltration,
  fatigue); **B**one lesions (**lytic "punched-out" lesions**, bone pain, pathological fractures); plus
  recurrent infections and hyperviscosity. (1½m)
- **Investigations (1½m):** **serum/urine protein electrophoresis → M-band**; **serum free light chains** +
  immunofixation; **Bence Jones protein** in urine; **bone marrow ≥10% clonal plasma cells**; **skeletal
  survey / whole-body MRI/PET** for lytic lesions (NOT bone scan — lesions are lytic); ↑ESR, rouleaux on
  film, raised calcium, β2-microglobulin (prognosis). (1½m)
- **Management (1m):** **bortezomib-based triplets** (e.g. bortezomib + lenalidomide + dexamethasone) →
  **autologous stem-cell transplant** in fit patients; **bisphosphonates** (zoledronate) for bone disease/
  hypercalcaemia; hydration + treat renal failure; analgesia/radiotherapy for bone pain. (1m)

**What to tabulate:** [ ] **CRAB criteria** (hyperCalcaemia / Renal / Anaemia / Bone) — ~1m

**Learn it:** Harrison's — *Plasma Cell Disorders → Multiple Myeloma*. · Davidson's — *Multiple myeloma and
paraproteinaemias*. · API — *Haematology → Plasma cell dyscrasias*. [verify current edition]
