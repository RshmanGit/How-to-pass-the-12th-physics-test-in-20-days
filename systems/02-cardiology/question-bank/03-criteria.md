### Q1. Modified Duke criteria for infective endocarditis (5m)
<!-- id: sys02-crit-q1 -->

**Restated:** Outline the modified Duke criteria for diagnosing infective endocarditis (IE).

- **Principle (1m):** IE is diagnosed by combining **major** and **minor** criteria built from
  microbiology (blood cultures), echocardiography and predisposing/clinical features; **definite IE** needs
  pathological proof **or** a defined combination of clinical criteria. (1m)
- **Major criteria (2m):**

| Major criterion | What counts |
|---|---|
| **1. Positive blood cultures** | typical IE organism from **2 separate** cultures (viridans strep, *S. bovis*, HACEK, *S. aureus*, community enterococci); **or** persistently positive cultures; **or** single positive for *Coxiella burnetii* |
| **2. Evidence of endocardial involvement** | **echocardiography** — vegetation, abscess, new prosthetic dehiscence; **or new valvular regurgitation** |

  (2m for both majors with their content.)

- **Minor criteria (1½m):** **(1) Predisposition** — predisposing heart condition or IV drug use;
  **(2) Fever** ≥38°C; **(3) Vascular phenomena** — emboli, septic infarcts, Janeway lesions, mycotic
  aneurysm; **(4) Immunological phenomena** — Osler's nodes, Roth spots, glomerulonephritis, rheumatoid
  factor; **(5) Microbiological** — positive culture not meeting a major. (1½m)
- **Diagnostic thresholds (½m):** **Definite IE = 2 major, OR 1 major + 3 minor, OR 5 minor**
  (or pathological criteria). **Possible IE = 1 major + 1 minor, OR 3 minor.** (½m)

### Q2. CHA₂DS₂-VASc and HAS-BLED scores in atrial fibrillation (5m)
<!-- id: sys02-crit-q2 -->

**Restated:** Describe the CHA₂DS₂-VASc and HAS-BLED scores and their use in atrial fibrillation.

- **Purpose (1m):** in **non-valvular AF**, **CHA₂DS₂-VASc** estimates annual **stroke/thromboembolic risk**
  to guide anticoagulation, while **HAS-BLED** estimates **major bleeding risk** to flag and correct
  modifiable risk factors — **not** to deny anticoagulation. (1m)
- **CHA₂DS₂-VASc (2m):**

| Factor | Points |
|---|---|
| **C** — Congestive heart failure / LV dysfunction | 1 |
| **H** — Hypertension | 1 |
| **A₂** — Age **≥75** | 2 |
| **D** — Diabetes mellitus | 1 |
| **S₂** — prior **Stroke / TIA / thromboembolism** | 2 |
| **V** — Vascular disease (MI, PAD, aortic plaque) | 1 |
| **A** — Age **65–74** | 1 |
| **Sc** — Sex category **female** | 1 |

  (2m for the full table; max score 9.)

- **HAS-BLED (1m):** **H**ypertension (uncontrolled), **A**bnormal renal/liver function (1 each),
  **S**troke, **B**leeding history/predisposition, **L**abile INR, **E**lderly (>65), **D**rugs/alcohol
  (1 each) — **score ≥3 = high bleeding risk** → review and correct modifiable factors. (1m)
- **Use + worked example (1m):** anticoagulate when CHA₂DS₂-VASc **≥2 in men / ≥3 in women** (consider at 1).
  *Example —* a **78-year-old hypertensive diabetic woman with prior TIA**: Age ≥75 (**2**) + Hypertension
  (**1**) + Diabetes (**1**) + prior TIA (**2**) + female (**1**) = **score 7** → high stroke risk →
  **anticoagulate (DOAC).** (1m)

### Q3. Modified Jones criteria for rheumatic fever (5m)
<!-- id: sys02-crit-q3 -->

**Restated:** Outline the modified (revised) Jones criteria for diagnosing acute rheumatic fever (ARF).

- **Principle (1m):** ARF is diagnosed on the **Jones criteria** combining **major** and **minor** features,
  **plus evidence of preceding group A streptococcal (GAS) infection** (raised/rising **ASO titre**, positive
  throat culture or rapid antigen test). (1m)
- **Major criteria — "CASES / JONES" (2m):**

| Major | Note |
|---|---|
| **Carditis** | clinical and/or subclinical (echo) — the only feature causing chronic RHD |
| **Arthritis** | migratory polyarthritis (large joints) |
| **Sydenham's chorea** | |
| **Erythema marginatum** | |
| **Subcutaneous nodules** | |

  (2m for the five majors.)

- **Minor criteria (1m):** **arthralgia**, **fever**, **raised acute-phase reactants (ESR/CRP)**, **prolonged
  PR interval** on ECG. (Note: in the 2015 revision, a feature used as a major is not also counted as a
  minor.) (1m)
- **Diagnosis + thresholds (1m):** evidence of preceding GAS **plus** either **2 major** **or** **1 major +
  2 minor** = ARF (first episode). The **2015 AHA revision** stratifies by population risk (low- vs
  moderate/high-risk, e.g. India) and accepts **subclinical carditis** and monoarthritis in high-risk groups —
  **confirm which version Mitsu's examiners use.** (1m)
