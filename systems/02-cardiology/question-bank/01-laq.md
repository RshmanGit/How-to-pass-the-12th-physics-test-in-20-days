### Q1. Acute coronary syndrome / myocardial infarction — aetiopathogenesis, clinical features, investigations and management (15m)
<!-- id: sys02-laq-q1 -->

**Restated:** Discuss the aetiopathogenesis, clinical features, investigations and management of acute
coronary syndrome (ACS), including ST-elevation myocardial infarction (STEMI).

**Definition (1m)**
- ACS = a spectrum of acute myocardial ischaemia caused by **abrupt reduction in coronary blood flow**,
  comprising **unstable angina (UA), NSTEMI and STEMI**. UA = ischaemic chest pain at rest/crescendo without
  troponin rise; **NSTEMI** = ischaemic symptoms + **troponin rise** without persistent ST-elevation;
  **STEMI** = ischaemic symptoms + **persistent ST-elevation** (or new LBBB) from complete vessel occlusion. (1m)

**Aetiopathogenesis (3m)**
- **Atherosclerotic plaque rupture/erosion** with superimposed **thrombosis** is the dominant mechanism. (1m)
- **STEMI** = complete, occlusive, fibrin-rich thrombus → transmural infarction; **NSTEMI/UA** = partial or
  intermittent occlusion / distal embolisation → subendocardial ischaemia. (1m)
- **Risk factors:** age, male sex, smoking, hypertension, diabetes, dyslipidaemia, family history, obesity;
  non-atherosclerotic causes — coronary spasm, dissection (SCAD), embolism, vasculitis, cocaine. (1m)

**Clinical features (3m)**
- **Central crushing retrosternal chest pain** >20 min, radiating to left arm/jaw, **not relieved by rest/GTN**;
  with sweating, nausea, dyspnoea, sense of impending doom. (1m)
- **Atypical/silent** presentations — elderly, diabetics, women: dyspnoea, syncope, epigastric pain, fatigue. (½m)
- **Signs:** anxious, pale, sweaty; tachy/bradycardia, hypo/hypertension; **S4/S3**, new murmur (MR/VSD),
  basal crepitations (LVF); raised JVP in RV infarct. (½m)
- **Killip class** stratifies pump failure (I = no failure → IV = cardiogenic shock). (1m)

**Investigations (3m)**
- **12-lead ECG — FIRST, within 10 min:** STEMI = **ST-elevation ≥1 mm in ≥2 contiguous limb leads or ≥2 mm
  in chest leads / new LBBB**; localise (inferior II/III/aVF, anterior V1–V4, lateral I/aVL/V5–V6; do **right-sided
  + posterior leads** if inferior). NSTEMI/UA = ST-depression/T-inversion or normal. (1½m)
- **Cardiac troponin (hs-cTnI/T)** — serial (0/1–3 h); rise-and-fall confirms infarction (negative in UA). (1m)
- **Supportive:** CXR, FBC, U&E, glucose, lipids; **echocardiography** for regional wall-motion/complications;
  **coronary angiography** is definitive for anatomy/revascularisation. (½m)

**Management (5m)**
- **Immediate / "MONA-plus" (1½m):** oxygen only if SpO₂ <90%; **GTN** for pain (avoid in RV infarct/hypotension);
  **morphine** for refractory pain; **dual antiplatelet — aspirin 300 mg loading** + a **P2Y₁₂ inhibitor
  (ticagrelor 180 mg / clopidogrel 300–600 mg / prasugrel 60 mg)**; **anticoagulation** (UFH / enoxaparin /
  fondaparinux). (1½m)
- **STEMI — reperfusion (the mark-magnet) (2m):**
  - **Primary PCI** = preferred if achievable within **120 min** of first medical contact. (1m)
  - **Fibrinolysis** (e.g. **tenecteplase weight-adjusted bolus**, or streptokinase/alteplase) if PCI not
    available within 120 min and presentation **<12 h**, no contraindication; transfer for angiography after
    ("pharmaco-invasive"). (½m)
  - **NSTEMI/UA:** risk-stratify (**GRACE score**) → early invasive angiography (within 24–72 h) for
    high risk. (½m)
- **Secondary prevention / long-term (1½m):** **dual antiplatelet ~12 months** then aspirin; **high-intensity
  statin** (atorvastatin 80 mg); **ACE inhibitor** (esp. LV dysfunction); **beta-blocker**; aldosterone
  antagonist if EF ≤40% + HF/diabetes; **cardiac rehabilitation**, smoking cessation, BP/glucose/lipid control. (1½m)

**What to draw / tabulate (independent marks):**
- [ ] **Table — UA vs NSTEMI vs STEMI** (ECG, troponin, occlusion, ST) — ~1m
- [ ] **Flowchart — STEMI reperfusion pathway** (ECG ≤10 min → PCI ≤120 min vs lysis <12 h → 2° prevention) — ~1m

### Q2. Congestive heart failure — aetiopathogenesis, clinical features, investigations and management (15m)
<!-- id: sys02-laq-q2 -->

**Restated:** Discuss the aetiopathogenesis, clinical features, investigations and management of congestive
(chronic) heart failure, including acute decompensation.

**Definition (1m)**
- Heart failure = a **clinical syndrome** in which the heart fails to pump blood at a rate sufficient for the
  metabolic needs of tissues (or does so only at raised filling pressures). Classified by ejection fraction:
  **HFrEF (≤40%)**, **HFmrEF (41–49%)**, **HFpEF (≥50%)**. (1m)

**Aetiopathogenesis (3m)**
- **Causes:** ischaemic heart disease (commonest), **hypertension**, valvular disease, cardiomyopathies,
  arrhythmias, high-output states (anaemia, thyrotoxicosis, AV fistula). (1m)
- **Pathophysiology:** myocardial injury → ↓cardiac output → **neurohormonal activation** of the
  **RAAS** and **sympathetic nervous system** → salt/water retention, vasoconstriction, raised preload/afterload. (1m)
- Initially compensatory but maladaptive long-term → **ventricular remodelling**, hypertrophy/dilatation and
  progressive pump failure (the rationale for RAAS/sympathetic blockade). (1m)

**Clinical features (3m)**
- **Left HF:** exertional **dyspnoea, orthopnoea, paroxysmal nocturnal dyspnoea**, fatigue; basal
  **crepitations**, S3 gallop. (1m)
- **Right HF:** **raised JVP**, dependent **pitting oedema**, tender **hepatomegaly**, ascites. (1m)
- **NYHA functional class** I–IV grades exertional limitation; look for the precipitant (ischaemia, infection,
  arrhythmia, non-adherence, anaemia). (1m)

**Investigations (3m)**
- **BNP / NT-proBNP** — raised; good rule-out if normal. (1m)
- **Echocardiography — key test:** EF, chamber size, wall motion, valves, diastolic function. (1m)
- **Supportive:** ECG, CXR (cardiomegaly, upper-lobe diversion, Kerley B lines, effusions, alveolar oedema);
  FBC, U&E, TFT, glucose, iron studies; coronary angiography if ischaemic aetiology suspected. (1m)

**Management (5m)**
- **HFrEF disease-modifying "four pillars" (the mark-magnet) (2½m):**
  - **ACE inhibitor / ARB** — or **ARNI (sacubitril–valsartan)** in place of ACEi. (½m)
  - **Beta-blocker** (bisoprolol / carvedilol / metoprolol succinate) — start low, uptitrate when stable. (½m)
  - **Mineralocorticoid-receptor antagonist** (spironolactone / eplerenone). (½m)
  - **SGLT2 inhibitor** (dapagliflozin / empagliflozin) — reduces HF hospitalisation/mortality. (½m)
  - All shown to **reduce mortality** in HFrEF; titrate to target with monitoring of BP, K⁺, renal function. (½m)
- **Symptom relief (1m):** **loop diuretic** (furosemide) for congestion (symptomatic, not mortality);
  salt/fluid restriction, daily weights; treat the precipitant. (1m)
- **Device/advanced therapy (½m):** **ICD** for EF ≤35% (sudden-death prevention); **CRT** if QRS ≥130 ms/LBBB;
  transplant/LVAD in end-stage. (½m)
- **Acute decompensated HF / pulmonary oedema (1m):** sit up, **high-flow O₂ / non-invasive ventilation**,
  **IV furosemide**, IV nitrate if BP adequate; treat trigger; inotropes/vasopressors only if hypotensive. (1m)

**What to draw / tabulate (independent marks):**
- [ ] **Table — left vs right heart failure** (symptoms, signs) — ~1m
- [ ] **Flowchart — HFrEF four pillars + diuretic** (ACEi/ARNI · β-blocker · MRA · SGLT2i) — ~1m

**Learn it:** Harrison's — *Heart Failure: Management* & *Pathophysiology*. · Davidson's — *Heart failure*. ·
API — *Cardiology → Heart failure*. [verify current edition]

### Q3. Infective endocarditis — aetiopathogenesis, clinical features, investigations and management (10m)
<!-- id: sys02-laq-q3 -->

**Restated:** Discuss the aetiopathogenesis, clinical features, investigations and management of infective
endocarditis (IE).

**Definition (1m)**
- IE = **microbial infection of the endocardial surface of the heart**, typically the heart valves,
  characterised by **vegetations** (platelet-fibrin-organism masses); classified as native-valve,
  prosthetic-valve or IV-drug-user (right-sided) IE, and acute vs subacute. (1m)

**Aetiopathogenesis (2m)**
- **Organisms:** *Staphylococcus aureus* (commonest acute, IVDU, prosthetic), **viridans streptococci**
  (subacute, post-dental, abnormal valves), *Enterococci*, **HACEK** group; culture-negative (prior
  antibiotics, *Coxiella*, *Bartonella*). (1m)
- **Pathogenesis:** endothelial damage → sterile platelet-fibrin thrombus → **transient bacteraemia**
  (dental/procedural) → microbial colonisation → **vegetation**. Risk: rheumatic/congenital valve disease,
  prosthetic valves, prior IE, IV drug use. (1m)

**Clinical features (2m)**
- **Fever** (most common) + **new/changing heart murmur**; malaise, sweats, weight loss (subacute). (1m)
- **Peripheral / immunological signs:** **splinter haemorrhages, Janeway lesions** (painless palms/soles),
  **Osler's nodes** (painful pulps), **Roth spots**, splenomegaly, clubbing, microscopic haematuria. (½m)
- **Complications:** heart failure (valve destruction), **systemic/septic emboli** (stroke, splenic/renal
  infarcts), mycotic aneurysm, glomerulonephritis. (½m)

**Investigations (2m)**
- **Blood cultures — ≥3 sets** from different sites before antibiotics (cornerstone of diagnosis). (1m)
- **Echocardiography:** **TTE first, TOE more sensitive** — vegetations, abscess, new regurgitation,
  prosthetic dehiscence. (½m)
- **Diagnosis by the modified Duke criteria** (see `03-criteria.md`); raised ESR/CRP, anaemia,
  haematuria; ECG for conduction delay (aortic root abscess). (½m)

**Management (3m)**
- **Antimicrobial therapy (the mark-magnet) (2m):** **prolonged IV bactericidal antibiotics (4–6 weeks)**,
  guided by culture/sensitivity. Empirical (native valve, acute) — a β-lactam-based regimen such as
  **IV ampicillin + (flu)cloxacillin + gentamicin**, or **IV vancomycin + gentamicin** if MRSA risk/penicillin
  allergy (regimen varies by guideline — confirm local protocol); viridans strep —
  **IV benzylpenicillin/ceftriaxone ± gentamicin**; *S. aureus* (MSSA) —
  **flucloxacillin**, (MRSA) — **vancomycin/daptomycin**; prosthetic valve adds **rifampicin**. (2m)
- **Surgery (½m):** for **heart failure** from valve destruction, **uncontrolled infection / abscess**,
  large/mobile vegetations with recurrent emboli, or prosthetic-valve dehiscence. (½m)
- **Prophylaxis (½m):** antibiotic prophylaxis before high-risk dental procedures **only for high-risk
  cardiac lesions** (prosthetic valve, prior IE, certain congenital disease); good oral/dental hygiene. (½m)

**What to draw / tabulate (independent marks):**
- [ ] **Table — common organisms by setting** (native subacute / acute / IVDU / prosthetic) — ~1m
- [ ] **List — peripheral stigmata** (Osler / Janeway / Roth / splinter) labelled — ~½m

**Learn it:** Harrison's — *Infective Endocarditis*. · Davidson's — *Infective endocarditis*. · API —
*Cardiology → Endocarditis*. [verify current edition]

### Q4. Systemic hypertension including hypertensive emergencies — aetiopathogenesis, clinical features, investigations and management (10m)
<!-- id: sys02-laq-q4 -->

**Restated:** Discuss the causes, evaluation and management of systemic hypertension, including hypertensive
emergencies.

**Definition (1m)**
- Hypertension = persistently raised arterial BP; commonly **≥140/90 mmHg** (office, on repeated readings).
  **Hypertensive emergency** = severe BP elevation (often **>180/120 mmHg**) **with acute target-organ
  damage**; **hypertensive urgency** = severe BP **without** acute organ damage. (1m)

**Aetiopathogenesis (2m)**
- **Primary (essential) ~90–95%** — multifactorial: genetic, salt intake, obesity, RAAS/sympathetic
  activation, ageing arterial stiffness. (1m)
- **Secondary (~5–10%)** — renal parenchymal/renovascular disease, **primary aldosteronism**,
  phaeochromocytoma, Cushing's, coarctation, OSA, drugs (NSAIDs, steroids, OCP). Consider if young, severe,
  resistant or sudden-onset. (1m)

**Clinical features (2m)**
- Usually **asymptomatic** ("silent killer"); headache, dizziness when severe. (½m)
- **Target-organ damage:** LVH/heart failure, IHD; stroke/TIA; **hypertensive retinopathy**; chronic kidney
  disease/proteinuria; peripheral arterial disease. (1m)
- **Emergency presentations:** encephalopathy, acute LVF/pulmonary oedema, ACS, aortic dissection,
  acute kidney injury, eclampsia, papilloedema. (½m)

**Investigations (2m)**
- **Confirm BP** — repeated readings / ambulatory or home BP monitoring (exclude white-coat). (½m)
- **Assess organ damage + risk:** ECG (LVH), urinalysis (protein/haematuria), U&E/eGFR, fasting glucose,
  lipids, fundoscopy. (1m)
- **Screen for secondary cause** when indicated: renal imaging, plasma aldosterone:renin ratio, plasma/urine
  metanephrines, etc. (½m)

**Management (3m)**
- **Lifestyle (1m):** salt restriction, weight loss, the DASH diet, exercise, limit alcohol, stop smoking. (1m)
- **Pharmacological — chronic (1m):** first-line **ACE inhibitor/ARB**, **calcium-channel blocker**, or
  **thiazide-like diuretic** (β-blockers for specific indications); combine to reach target (commonly
  <140/90, lower if diabetic/CKD); add **spironolactone** for resistant hypertension. (1m)
- **Hypertensive emergency (the mark-magnet) (1m):** admit; **controlled IV reduction — lower MAP by no more
  than ~25% in the first hour** (avoid organ hypoperfusion) using **IV labetalol / nicardipine / nitroprusside
  / GTN** per context (e.g. labetalol/esmolol + nitroprusside for aortic dissection); urgency → oral agents,
  gradual reduction over days. (1m)

**What to draw / tabulate (independent marks):**
- [ ] **Table — emergency vs urgency** (BP, organ damage, route, speed of lowering) — ~1m
- [ ] **List — causes of secondary hypertension** (renal / endocrine / vascular / drugs) — ~½m

**Learn it:** Harrison's — *Hypertensive Vascular Disease*. · Davidson's — *Hypertension*. · API —
*Cardiology → Systemic hypertension*. [verify current edition]

### Q5. Pulmonary hypertension — aetiopathogenesis, clinical features, investigations and management (10m)
<!-- id: sys02-laq-q5 -->

**Restated:** Discuss the classification, clinical features, investigations and management of pulmonary
hypertension (PH).

**Definition (1m)**
- Pulmonary hypertension = a **mean pulmonary artery pressure (mPAP) >20 mmHg at rest** on right-heart
  catheterisation; pulmonary arterial hypertension (PAH) further requires **PVR ≥2 Wood units** with a
  **PCWP ≤15 mmHg** (pre-capillary). (1m)

**Aetiopathogenesis / classification (2m)**
- **WHO 5 groups (2m):** **Group 1** — pulmonary arterial hypertension (idiopathic, heritable, drug-induced,
  connective-tissue disease, congenital heart disease, portal hypertension, HIV); **Group 2** — left heart
  disease (commonest); **Group 3** — lung disease/hypoxia (COPD, ILD); **Group 4** — chronic thromboembolic
  PH (CTEPH); **Group 5** — multifactorial/unclear. (2m)

**Clinical features (2m)**
- **Exertional dyspnoea and fatigue** (earliest), exertional chest pain/syncope, palpitations. (1m)
- **Signs of pulmonary hypertension / RV failure:** **loud P2**, RV heave, right-sided S4, **raised JVP with
  prominent a/v waves**, tricuspid regurgitation murmur, **hepatomegaly, ascites, peripheral oedema**. (1m)

**Investigations (2m)**
- **Transthoracic echocardiography** — screening test: estimates **PA systolic pressure**, RV size/function. (1m)
- **Right-heart catheterisation — gold standard:** confirms mPAP, PCWP, PVR and vasoreactivity. (½m)
- **Identify cause/group:** ECG (RVH, RAD), CXR, PFTs + ABG, **V/Q scan** (mandatory to exclude CTEPH),
  CTPA, autoimmune screen, HIV, abdominal USS. (½m)

**Management (3m)**
- **General (½m):** treat the **underlying cause/group**; diuretics for RV-failure congestion, oxygen for
  hypoxia, anticoagulation in selected (esp. CTEPH), vaccination, supervised rehabilitation. (½m)
- **Group 1 PAH–specific therapy (the mark-magnet) (1½m):** **vasoreactivity-positive** → high-dose
  **calcium-channel blockers**; otherwise targeted agents across three pathways — **endothelin-receptor
  antagonists** (bosentan, ambrisentan, macitentan), **PDE-5 inhibitors / sGC stimulator** (sildenafil,
  tadalafil, riociguat), **prostacyclin pathway** (epoprostenol, treprostinil, selexipag); combination
  therapy for higher-risk patients. (1½m)
- **Group-specific / advanced (1m):** Group 2/3 → optimise left-heart/lung disease (PAH drugs not indicated);
  **Group 4 (CTEPH) → pulmonary endarterectomy** (potentially curative) ± riociguat/balloon angioplasty;
  refractory disease → lung transplantation. (1m)

- [ ] **Table — WHO 5 groups of PH** (with example cause each) — ~1m
- [ ] **Table — three PAH drug pathways** (endothelin / NO–PDE5 / prostacyclin) — ~1m
