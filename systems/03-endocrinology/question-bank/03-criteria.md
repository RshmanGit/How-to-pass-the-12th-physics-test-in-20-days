### Q1. Diagnostic criteria for diabetes mellitus (ADA) (5m)
<!-- id: sys03-crit-q1 -->

**Restated:** State the American Diabetes Association (ADA) criteria for diagnosing diabetes mellitus.

- **Principle (1m):** diabetes is diagnosed by demonstrating **chronic hyperglycaemia** using **any one** of
  the four criteria below; **in the absence of unequivocal hyperglycaemia, the abnormal result should be
  confirmed by repeat testing** on a separate day. (1m)
- **The four diagnostic criteria (3m):**

| Test | Diabetes threshold | Note |
|---|---|---|
| **Fasting plasma glucose (FPG)** | **≥126 mg/dL (7.0 mmol/L)** | fasting = no caloric intake ≥8 h |
| **2-h plasma glucose on 75 g OGTT** | **≥200 mg/dL (11.1 mmol/L)** | standard oral glucose tolerance test |
| **HbA1c** | **≥6.5% (48 mmol/mol)** | standardised assay |
| **Random plasma glucose** | **≥200 mg/dL (11.1 mmol/L)** | **only with classic symptoms / hyperglycaemic crisis** |

  (3m for the full table; partial credit per correctly stated threshold.)

- **Prediabetes / interpretation (1m):** **FPG 100–125** (impaired fasting glucose), **2-h OGTT 140–199**
  (impaired glucose tolerance), or **HbA1c 5.7–6.4%** = prediabetes (increased risk). The random-glucose
  criterion requires symptoms; the other three need confirmation if asymptomatic. (1m)
