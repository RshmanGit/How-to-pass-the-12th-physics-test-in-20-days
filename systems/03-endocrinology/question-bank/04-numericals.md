### Q1. Anion gap in diabetic ketoacidosis (5m)
<!-- id: sys03-num-q1 -->

**Restated:** Define the anion gap, give its formula, and calculate it for a patient in DKA with
Na‚Āļ 130, K‚Āļ 5.0, Cl‚ĀĽ 95, HCO‚āÉ‚ĀĽ 8 mmol/L. State the interpretation.

- **Concept & formula (1¬Ĺm):** the **anion gap (AG)** estimates unmeasured plasma anions =
  **AG = [Na‚Āļ] ‚ąí ([Cl‚ĀĽ] + [HCO‚āÉ‚ĀĽ])** (potassium often omitted). **Normal ‚Čą 8‚Äď12 mmol/L**. In DKA, accumulating
  **ketoacids (ő≤-hydroxybutyrate, acetoacetate)** raise the gap ‚Üí **high-anion-gap metabolic acidosis**. (1¬Ĺm)
- **Substitution (1¬Ĺm):** AG = 130 ‚ąí (95 + 8) = 130 ‚ąí 103. (1¬Ĺm)
- **Result (1m):** **AG = 27 mmol/L.** (1m)
- **Interpretation (1m):** 27 is **well above the normal 8‚Äď12** ‚Üí a **high-anion-gap metabolic acidosis**,
  consistent with DKA (the low HCO‚āÉ‚ĀĽ of 8 confirms severe acidosis). Track the **gap and HCO‚āÉ‚ĀĽ** to monitor
  resolution. *(Optional: if albumin is low, correct AG up by ~2.5 per 1 g/dL fall in albumin.)* (1m)

### Q2. Serum/effective osmolality and corrected sodium (5m)
<!-- id: sys03-num-q2 -->

**Restated:** Give the formulae for serum osmolality, effective osmolality and corrected sodium. For a patient
with Na‚Āļ 130 mmol/L, glucose 600 mg/dL and urea (BUN) 28 mg/dL, calculate each and interpret.

- **Formulae (1¬Ĺm):**
  - **Serum osmolality = 2 √ó [Na‚Āļ] + (glucose √∑ 18) + (BUN √∑ 2.8)** (mOsm/kg; glucose & BUN in mg/dL).
  - **Effective osmolality (tonicity) = 2 √ó [Na‚Āļ] + (glucose √∑ 18)** ‚ÄĒ **omits urea** (urea crosses membranes
    freely, so it does not drive water shifts). Used to define HHS (**>320 mOsm/kg**).
  - **Corrected Na‚Āļ = measured Na‚Āļ + 1.6 √ó [(glucose ‚ąí 100) √∑ 100]** (hyperglycaemia dilutes Na‚Āļ). (1¬Ĺm)
- **Substitution (1¬Ĺm):**
  - glucose √∑ 18 = 600 √∑ 18 = 33.3; BUN √∑ 2.8 = 28 √∑ 2.8 = 10.
  - Serum osmolality = (2 √ó 130) + 33.3 + 10 = 260 + 33.3 + 10.
  - Effective osmolality = 260 + 33.3.
  - Corrected Na‚Āļ = 130 + 1.6 √ó [(600 ‚ąí 100) √∑ 100] = 130 + 1.6 √ó 5 = 130 + 8. (1¬Ĺm)
- **Results (1m):** **Serum osmolality ‚Čą 303 mOsm/kg ¬∑ Effective osmolality ‚Čą 293 mOsm/kg ¬∑ Corrected
  Na‚Āļ = 138 mmol/L.** (1m)
- **Interpretation (1m):** the **measured Na‚Āļ of 130 is pseudohyponatraemia** ‚ÄĒ once corrected for the high
  glucose the true Na‚Āļ is **138 (normal)**. Effective osmolality 293 is **<320**, so this does **not** meet the
  HHS osmolality threshold; reassess as glucose is treated. (1m)
