### Q1. Short note: Pheochromocytoma (5m)
<!-- id: sys03-saq-q1 -->

**Restated:** Write a short note on pheochromocytoma.

- **Definition (1m):** a **catecholamine-secreting tumour of the adrenal medulla chromaffin cells**
  (extra-adrenal = paraganglioma); follows the **"rule of 10s"** (~10% bilateral, extra-adrenal, malignant,
  familial). (1m)
- **Clinical features (1½m):** **paroxysmal** episodes of the classic triad — **headache, palpitations,
  sweating** — with **hypertension** (sustained or episodic); pallor, anxiety, tremor; may present with
  hypertensive crisis. Part of **MEN-2, von Hippel-Lindau, NF-1**. (1½m)
- **Investigations (1½m):** **plasma free metanephrines / 24-h urinary fractionated metanephrines and
  catecholamines** (most sensitive); **CT/MRI abdomen** to localise; **MIBG scan** for extra-adrenal/metastatic
  disease. (1½m)
- **Management (1m):** **surgical resection** (curative); **pre-operative α-blockade first (phenoxybenzamine)
  for 10–14 days, then add β-blocker** — **never β-block before α-block** (risk of unopposed α
  vasoconstriction → crisis); volume repletion pre-op. (1m)

### Q2. Short note: Graves' disease (5m)
<!-- id: sys03-saq-q2 -->

**Restated:** Write a short note on Graves' disease.

- **Definition (1m):** the commonest cause of **hyperthyroidism**; an **autoimmune** disorder in which
  **TSH-receptor stimulating antibodies** drive diffuse thyroid overactivity and gland enlargement. (1m)
- **Clinical features (1½m):** thyrotoxic symptoms (weight loss, heat intolerance, palpitations, tremor,
  diarrhoea, AF) + **Graves'-specific signs** — **diffuse goitre with bruit, ophthalmopathy (exophthalmos,
  lid lag), pretibial myxoedema, thyroid acropachy**. (1½m)
- **Investigations (1½m):** **↓TSH with ↑free T4/T3**; **TSH-receptor antibodies (TRAb) positive**;
  **diffusely increased radioiodine uptake** on scan; anti-TPO may be positive. (1½m)
- **Management (1m):** **antithyroid drugs** (carbimazole; PTU in first-trimester pregnancy), **β-blocker**
  (propranolol) for symptoms, **radioiodine** or **thyroidectomy** for definitive cure; treat ophthalmopathy
  (steroids, avoid radioiodine if active). (1m)

**What to tabulate:** [ ] **Graves'-specific signs** (eye, skin, goitre) vs general thyrotoxic features — ~½m

**Learn it:** Harrison's — *Thyrotoxicosis (Graves' disease)*. · Davidson's — *Thyroid gland*. [verify]

### Q3. Short note: Hypocalcaemia and hypercalcaemia (5m)
<!-- id: sys03-saq-q3 -->

**Restated:** Write a short note on the causes, features and management of hypo- and hypercalcaemia.

- **Hypocalcaemia — causes (1m):** **hypoparathyroidism** (post-thyroidectomy, autoimmune), **vitamin D
  deficiency**, chronic kidney disease, hypomagnesaemia, acute pancreatitis. (1m)
- **Hypocalcaemia — features & treatment (1½m):** **neuromuscular irritability** — perioral numbness, cramps,
  **carpopedal spasm**, **Chvostek's and Trousseau's signs**, tetany, seizures, prolonged QT. **Treat:** acute/
  symptomatic → **IV calcium gluconate**; chronic → oral calcium + **vitamin D / calcitriol**; correct Mg²⁺. (1½m)
- **Hypercalcaemia — causes (1m):** **primary hyperparathyroidism** (commonest outpatient) and **malignancy**
  (commonest inpatient — bone mets/PTHrP) account for ~90%; also vitamin D toxicity, sarcoidosis, thiazides. (1m)
- **Hypercalcaemia — features & treatment (1½m):** **"stones, bones, abdominal groans, psychic moans"** —
  renal stones, bone pain, constipation/abdominal pain, fatigue/confusion, polyuria, short QT. **Treat:**
  **aggressive IV 0.9% saline** rehydration first, then **IV bisphosphonate (zoledronate)**; calcitonin for
  rapid effect; treat the cause. (1½m)

### Q4. Short note: SIADH (Syndrome of Inappropriate ADH secretion) (5m)
<!-- id: sys03-saq-q4 -->

**Restated:** Write a short note on the syndrome of inappropriate antidiuretic hormone secretion (SIADH).

- **Definition (1m):** **euvolaemic hyponatraemia** caused by **inappropriate (non-osmotic) secretion of ADH
  (vasopressin)** → water retention with concentrated urine despite low plasma osmolality. (1m)
- **Causes (1m):** **CNS** (stroke, meningitis, trauma), **pulmonary** (pneumonia, TB), **malignancy**
  (small-cell lung carcinoma — ectopic ADH), **drugs** (carbamazepine, SSRIs, cyclophosphamide). (1m)
- **Diagnostic criteria (1½m):** **hyponatraemia + low plasma osmolality (<275 mOsm/kg)**, **inappropriately
  concentrated urine (urine osmolality >100)**, **urine sodium >30 mmol/L**, **clinical euvolaemia**, and
  **normal thyroid, adrenal and renal function** (exclude hypothyroidism, Addison's, diuretics). (1½m)
- **Management (1½m):** treat the cause; **fluid restriction** first-line; **hypertonic (3%) saline** for
  severe/symptomatic hyponatraemia (seizures); **vasopressin-receptor antagonists (vaptans)** or demeclocycline
  in selected cases. **Correct Na⁺ slowly (≤8–10 mmol/L per 24 h) — avoid osmotic demyelination.** (1½m)

**Learn it:** Harrison's — *Disorders of the Neurohypophysis (SIADH)* / *Hyponatremia*. · Davidson's —
