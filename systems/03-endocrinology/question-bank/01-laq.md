### Q1. Diabetes mellitus — classification, complications and management (15m)
<!-- id: sys03-laq-q1 -->

**Restated:** Classify diabetes mellitus. Discuss its complications and outline the management.

**Definition (1m)**
- Diabetes mellitus = a group of **metabolic disorders characterised by chronic hyperglycaemia** resulting
  from defects in **insulin secretion, insulin action, or both**, with disturbance of carbohydrate, fat and
  protein metabolism. (1m)

**Classification / aetiopathogenesis (3m)**
- **Type 1 DM (½m):** autoimmune destruction of pancreatic **β-cells** → absolute insulin deficiency;
  ketosis-prone; younger onset; islet/GAD antibodies positive. (½m)
- **Type 2 DM (1m):** **insulin resistance + relative insulin deficiency**; commonest (~90%); associated with
  obesity, sedentary lifestyle, family history, metabolic syndrome; usually older but rising in the young. (1m)
- **Gestational DM (½m):** glucose intolerance first recognised in pregnancy. (½m)
- **Other specific types (1m):** MODY (monogenic), pancreatic disease (chronic pancreatitis, fibrocalculous),
  endocrinopathies (Cushing's, acromegaly), drug-induced (steroids), secondary to other illness. (1m)

**Clinical features (2m)**
- **Classic osmotic symptoms (1m):** polyuria, polydipsia, polyphagia, weight loss, fatigue; T1DM may present
  with **DKA**. T2DM often **asymptomatic / incidental** or presents with complications. (1m)
- Recurrent infections (candidiasis, skin), poor wound healing, blurred vision, pruritus vulvae. (1m)

**Complications (3m)**
- **Acute (1m):** **DKA** (T1DM), **hyperosmolar hyperglycaemic state** (T2DM), **hypoglycaemia** (treatment-
  related), lactic acidosis. (1m)
- **Chronic microvascular (1m):** **retinopathy** (leading cause of adult blindness), **nephropathy**
  (microalbuminuria → ESRD), **peripheral & autonomic neuropathy** (glove-and-stocking, diabetic foot). (1m)
- **Chronic macrovascular (1m):** **coronary artery disease**, stroke, peripheral arterial disease; DM is a
  **CAD risk equivalent**. Also: diabetic foot (neuropathy + ischaemia + infection). (1m)

**Investigations (2m)**
- **Diagnosis:** fasting plasma glucose ≥126 mg/dL, 2-h OGTT ≥200, random ≥200 + symptoms, or **HbA1c ≥6.5%**
  (ADA — see `03-criteria.md`). (1m)
- **Monitoring/work-up:** **HbA1c** every 3 months (glycaemic control over ~3 months); urine
  albumin:creatinine ratio, renal function, fundus exam, lipid profile, foot exam. (1m)

**Management (4m)**
- **Lifestyle + education (1m):** medical nutrition therapy, weight reduction, exercise, smoking cessation,
  self-monitoring; **target HbA1c generally <7%** (individualise). (1m)
- **T2DM pharmacotherapy (1½m):** **metformin first-line** (500 mg–1 g BD, max ~2 g/day) unless
  contraindicated; add **SGLT2 inhibitor** (empagliflozin — cardiorenal benefit) or **GLP-1 agonist**, or
  sulfonylurea / DPP-4 inhibitor / pioglitazone by profile; **insulin** when oral agents fail. (1½m)
- **T1DM (½m):** **basal–bolus insulin** (long-acting glargine/detemir + rapid-acting with meals) or insulin
  pump; never stop basal insulin. (½m)
- **Risk-factor & complication management (1m):** BP control (**ACE inhibitor/ARB**, esp. with albuminuria),
  **statin**, antiplatelet in established CVD; annual retinal, renal and foot screening. (1m)

- [ ] **Table — micro- vs macrovascular complications** — ~1m

### Q2. Diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA) and hyperosmolar hyperglycaemic state (HHS) (10m)
<!-- id: sys03-laq-q2 -->

**Restated:** Discuss the pathogenesis, clinical features, diagnosis and management of diabetic ketoacidosis
and the hyperosmolar hyperglycaemic state.

**Definition (1m)**
- **DKA** = the triad of **hyperglycaemia, ketonaemia/ketonuria and high-anion-gap metabolic acidosis**,
  typically in T1DM. **HHS** = **severe hyperglycaemia + hyperosmolality with profound dehydration but
  minimal ketosis/acidosis**, typically in elderly T2DM. (1m)

**Aetiopathogenesis (2m)**
- **DKA (1m):** **absolute insulin deficiency + counter-regulatory hormone excess** (glucagon, catecholamines,
  cortisol) → unrestrained lipolysis → free fatty acids → hepatic **ketogenesis** (β-hydroxybutyrate,
  acetoacetate) → metabolic acidosis; hyperglycaemia → osmotic diuresis → dehydration + electrolyte loss. (1m)
- **HHS (½m):** **relative** insulin deficiency (enough to suppress ketogenesis but not hyperglycaemia) →
  extreme hyperglycaemia, osmotic diuresis, hyperosmolality. (½m)
- **Precipitants (½m):** **infection (commonest)**, missed insulin/non-adherence, new-onset diabetes,
  MI/stroke, drugs (steroids), surgery, pancreatitis. (½m)

**Clinical features (2m)**
- **DKA (1m):** polyuria, polydipsia, weight loss, **nausea/vomiting, abdominal pain**, **Kussmaul breathing**
  (deep sighing), **acetone (fruity) breath**, dehydration, drowsiness → coma. (1m)
- **HHS (1m):** **marked dehydration**, altered sensorium/coma, **focal neurological signs/seizures**;
  evolves over days; no Kussmaul breathing; higher mortality than DKA. (1m)

**Investigations (2m)**
- **DKA diagnostic triad:** plasma glucose >250 mg/dL (the **2024 ADA/EASD/JBDS consensus** lowered this to
  **≥200 mg/dL**, or any prior diabetes), **blood ketones ≥3 mmol/L** (or significant ketonuria),
  **pH <7.3 and/or bicarbonate <15 mmol/L** (consensus now uses **<18 mmol/L**); the raised **anion gap**
  supports the diagnosis but is **no longer a severity criterion** (see `04-numericals.md`). (1m)
- **HHS:** glucose typically **>600 mg/dL**, **effective serum osmolality >320 mOsm/kg**, pH >7.3,
  bicarbonate >18, minimal ketones. (½m)
- **Both:** U&E (K⁺, Na⁺ — correct Na⁺ for hyperglycaemia), creatinine, ABG, FBC, septic screen, ECG. (½m)

**Management (3m)**
- **Fluids — first priority (1m):** **IV 0.9% normal saline** — restore circulating volume (e.g. 1 L in the
  first hour, then titrate to deficit/output); switch to **5% dextrose-containing fluid once glucose
  <200–250 mg/dL** (DKA) to allow continued insulin without hypoglycaemia. (1m)
- **Insulin (1m):** **fixed-rate IV insulin infusion 0.1 units/kg/hour** (regular insulin); aim glucose fall
  ~50–75 mg/dL/hour; **do NOT stop insulin** until ketoacidosis resolves — overlap with SC insulin before
  stopping the infusion. (1m)
- **Potassium (½m):** total-body K⁺ is depleted despite a normal/high initial level; **add KCl to fluids once
  K⁺ <5.3 mmol/L and urine output adequate**; withhold insulin if K⁺ <3.3 until replaced. (½m)
- **Supportive (½m):** treat the precipitant (antibiotics for infection), monitor glucose/ketones/U&E hourly→
  2-hourly, VTE prophylaxis (esp. HHS), avoid routine bicarbonate (only if pH <6.9). (½m)

**What to draw / tabulate (independent marks):**
- [ ] **Table — DKA vs HHS** (glucose, ketones, pH, osmolality, typical patient, mortality) — ~1m
- [ ] **Flowchart — DKA management** (fluids → insulin → K⁺ → add dextrose → resolve) — ~1m

*Emergencies in diabetes*. · API — *Endocrinology → Diabetic emergencies*. [verify current edition]

### Q3. Thyroid disorders — hypothyroidism, hyperthyroidism and thyroid storm (15m)
<!-- id: sys03-laq-q3 -->

**Restated:** Discuss the causes, clinical features, investigations and management of hypothyroidism and
hyperthyroidism, including thyroid storm.

**Definition (1m)**
- **Hypothyroidism** = clinical state from **deficient thyroid hormone action**. **Hyperthyroidism /
  thyrotoxicosis** = clinical state from **excess circulating thyroid hormone**. **Thyroid storm** =
  life-threatening, decompensated thyrotoxicosis. (1m)

**Aetiopathogenesis / causes (3m)**
- **Hypothyroidism (1½m):** **primary** (gland failure) — **Hashimoto's autoimmune thyroiditis** (commonest in
  iodine-sufficient areas), **iodine deficiency** (commonest worldwide), post-thyroidectomy/radioiodine,
  drugs (amiodarone, lithium), congenital; **secondary** — pituitary/hypothalamic (low TSH). (1½m)
- **Hyperthyroidism (1½m):** **Graves' disease** (commonest — TSH-receptor stimulating antibody), **toxic
  multinodular goitre**, **toxic adenoma**, thyroiditis (transient), excess iodine/thyroxine. (1½m)

**Clinical features (3m)**
- **Hypothyroidism (1½m):** fatigue, weight gain, cold intolerance, constipation, dry skin, hoarse voice,
  bradycardia, **delayed relaxation of ankle reflex**, menorrhagia, depression; severe → **myxoedema coma**. (1½m)
- **Hyperthyroidism (1½m):** weight loss with increased appetite, heat intolerance, palpitations, tremor,
  anxiety, diarrhoea, **tachycardia/atrial fibrillation**, goitre; Graves'-specific — **ophthalmopathy
  (exophthalmos), pretibial myxoedema, diffuse goitre with bruit**. (1½m)

**Investigations (3m)**
- **Thyroid function tests (1½m):** **primary hypothyroidism — ↑TSH, ↓free T4**; subclinical — ↑TSH, normal
  T4. **Hyperthyroidism — ↓TSH, ↑free T4/T3**; secondary causes alter this pattern (interpret TSH+T4
  together). (1½m)
- **Aetiology (1½m):** **anti-TPO antibodies** (Hashimoto's), **TSH-receptor antibodies** (Graves'),
  **radioiodine uptake scan** (diffuse high in Graves', patchy in nodular, low in thyroiditis); ultrasound for
  nodules. (1½m)

**Management (5m)**
- **Hypothyroidism (1½m):** **levothyroxine** orally, once daily on an empty stomach; usual replacement
  ~1.6 µg/kg/day; **start low (25–50 µg) and uptitrate in the elderly/cardiac patients**; monitor TSH every
  6–8 weeks until normalised, then annually. (1½m)
- **Hyperthyroidism (2m):** (i) **Antithyroid drugs** — **carbimazole** (or methimazole), **propylthiouracil
  (PTU) preferred in first-trimester pregnancy/storm**; "block-and-replace" or titration; agranulocytosis is
  the key warning. (ii) **β-blocker (propranolol)** for symptom control. (iii) **Radioactive iodine** ablation
  or (iv) **surgery (thyroidectomy)** for definitive treatment / large goitre / suspected malignancy. (2m)
- **Thyroid storm — emergency (1½m):** ICU; **propranolol** + **PTU (loading)** + **iodine (Lugol's) given
  ≥1 h after PTU** to block release + **hydrocortisone IV** (impairs T4→T3 conversion, treats relative adrenal
  insufficiency); active cooling, IV fluids, treat precipitant (infection, surgery). (1½m)

**What to draw / tabulate (independent marks):**
- [ ] **Table — hypothyroidism vs hyperthyroidism** (symptoms, signs, TFT pattern) — ~1m
- [ ] **Table/flowchart — TFT interpretation** (TSH + free T4 → diagnosis) — ~1m

**Learn it:** Harrison's — *Hypothyroidism* and *Thyrotoxicosis & Other Causes of Hyperthyroidism*. ·
Davidson's — *Thyroid gland*. · API — *Endocrinology → Thyroid disorders*. [verify current edition]

### Q4. Cushing's syndrome — aetiology, clinical features, investigations and management (10m)
<!-- id: sys03-laq-q4 -->

**Restated:** Discuss the causes, clinical features, investigations and management of Cushing's syndrome.

**Definition (1m)**
- Cushing's **syndrome** = the clinical state of **chronic glucocorticoid (cortisol) excess** from any cause.
  **Cushing's disease** = the specific subtype due to an **ACTH-secreting pituitary adenoma**. (1m)

**Aetiopathogenesis / causes (2m)**
- **Exogenous (commonest overall) (½m):** prolonged **glucocorticoid therapy** (iatrogenic). (½m)
- **ACTH-dependent (1m):** **pituitary adenoma (Cushing's disease — commonest endogenous cause)**; **ectopic
  ACTH** (small-cell lung carcinoma, carcinoid). (1m)
- **ACTH-independent (½m):** **adrenal adenoma**, adrenal carcinoma, nodular hyperplasia. (½m)

**Clinical features (3m)**
- **Habitus (1½m):** **central (truncal) obesity, moon face, buffalo hump**, thin limbs from proximal
  myopathy; **thin skin, easy bruising, purple striae (>1 cm)**. (1½m)
- **Systemic (1½m):** **hypertension, glucose intolerance/diabetes**, **osteoporosis**, proximal muscle
  weakness, hirsutism/acne/menstrual irregularity, depression/psychosis, poor wound healing, immunosuppression;
  hypokalaemic alkalosis (esp. ectopic ACTH). (1½m)

**Investigations (2m)**
- **Confirm cortisol excess (1m) — any two of:** **overnight/low-dose dexamethasone suppression test** (failure
  to suppress), **24-h urinary free cortisol** (elevated), **late-night salivary cortisol** (loss of diurnal
  rhythm). (1m)
- **Localise the cause (1m):** measure **plasma ACTH** (suppressed → adrenal; normal/high → ACTH-dependent);
  **high-dose dexamethasone suppression** + **pituitary MRI**; **inferior petrosal sinus sampling**; CT
  chest/abdomen for ectopic/adrenal source. (1m)

**Management (2m)**
- **Definitive (1m):** **trans-sphenoidal resection** of pituitary adenoma (Cushing's disease);
  **adrenalectomy** for adrenal tumour; resect/treat ectopic source; taper/stop exogenous steroids where
  iatrogenic. (1m)
- **Medical / adjunct (1m):** **steroidogenesis inhibitors** — **ketoconazole, metyrapone** (control cortisol
  pre-op or when surgery not feasible); pituitary radiotherapy for residual disease; treat complications
  (BP, diabetes, osteoporosis, infection). (1m)

**What to draw / tabulate (independent marks):**
- [ ] **Diagram — habitus** (moon face, buffalo hump, central obesity, striae, thin limbs) — ~1m
- [ ] **Flowchart — diagnostic workup** (confirm excess → plasma ACTH → localise) — ~1m

**Learn it:** Harrison's — *Disorders of the Adrenal Cortex (Cushing's syndrome)*. · Davidson's —
*Adrenal cortex*. · API — *Endocrinology → Adrenal disorders*. [verify current edition]

### Q5. Adrenal insufficiency / Addison's disease and adrenal crisis (10m)
<!-- id: sys03-laq-q5 -->

**Restated:** Discuss the causes, clinical features, investigations and management of adrenal insufficiency,
including adrenal (Addisonian) crisis.

**Definition (1m)**
- Adrenal insufficiency = **deficient adrenocortical hormone production** (glucocorticoid ± mineralocorticoid).
  **Primary (Addison's disease)** = adrenal gland failure; **secondary** = pituitary ACTH deficiency. (1m)

**Aetiopathogenesis / causes (2m)**
- **Primary (Addison's) (1m):** **autoimmune adrenalitis** (commonest in the West), **tuberculosis**
  (commonest in India/endemic areas), adrenal haemorrhage (Waterhouse-Friderichsen), metastases,
  adrenoleukodystrophy. **Both gluco- and mineralocorticoid lost.** (1m)
- **Secondary (1m):** **abrupt withdrawal of long-term steroids** (commonest cause overall), pituitary
  disease/surgery. **Aldosterone preserved** (RAA-driven); no hyperpigmentation. (1m)

**Clinical features (2m)**
- **Chronic (1m):** fatigue, weakness, anorexia, **weight loss**, nausea, postural hypotension, salt craving;
  **hyperpigmentation** of skin creases/buccal mucosa (primary only — high ACTH/MSH). (1m)
- **Adrenal crisis (1m):** **hypotension/shock**, severe vomiting, abdominal pain, fever, confusion —
  precipitated by infection, surgery, or steroid withdrawal; a medical emergency. (1m)

**Investigations (2m)**
- **Biochemistry (1m):** **hyponatraemia + hyperkalaemia** (primary), hypoglycaemia; **low 8 am serum
  cortisol**; **short Synacthen (ACTH stimulation) test** = the confirmatory test (inadequate cortisol rise). (1m)
- **Cause/level (1m):** **plasma ACTH** (high in primary, low in secondary); adrenal autoantibodies (21-
  hydroxylase); imaging/TB workup of adrenals; investigate pituitary if secondary. (1m)

**Management (3m)**
- **Chronic replacement (1½m):** **glucocorticoid — hydrocortisone** orally in divided doses (e.g. ~15–25 mg/
  day, larger morning dose) **+ mineralocorticoid — fludrocortisone** (primary only); **sick-day rules — double
  the steroid dose during illness**; carry a **steroid card / emergency hydrocortisone**. (1½m)
- **Adrenal crisis — emergency (1½m):** **IV hydrocortisone 100 mg stat then 100–200 mg/24 h** (infusion or
  6-hourly); **rapid IV 0.9% saline** to correct volume/hypotension; **IV dextrose** for hypoglycaemia; treat
  the precipitant (e.g. infection); monitor electrolytes. (Mineralocorticoid not needed acutely — high-dose
  hydrocortisone covers it.) (1½m)

**What to draw / tabulate (independent marks):**
- [ ] **Table — primary vs secondary adrenal insufficiency** (cause, ACTH, Na/K, pigmentation, aldosterone) — ~1m
- [ ] **Flowchart — adrenal crisis management** (IV hydrocortisone → saline → dextrose → treat cause) — ~1m

**Learn it:** Harrison's — *Disorders of the Adrenal Cortex (Adrenal insufficiency)*. · Davidson's —
*Adrenal cortex → Hypoadrenalism*. · API — *Endocrinology → Adrenal disorders*. [verify current edition]
