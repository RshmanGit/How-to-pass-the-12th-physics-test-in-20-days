### Q1. Modified Wells score for pulmonary embolism (5m)
<!-- id: sys08-q1 -->

**Restated:** Outline the Modified Wells score for assessing the clinical probability of pulmonary embolism (PE).

- **Use (1m):** a **pre-test clinical probability** tool for PE; it directs the next step — low/PE-unlikely
  → **D-dimer** (negative rules out, avoids imaging); high/PE-likely → straight to **CT pulmonary
  angiography**. It does *not* diagnose PE. (1m)
- **Score table (3m):**

| Criterion | Points |
|---|---|
| Clinical signs/symptoms of DVT (leg swelling + pain on palpation) | 3.0 |
| PE is the **most likely** diagnosis (alternative less likely) | 3.0 |
| Heart rate > 100/min | 1.5 |
| Immobilisation ≥3 days or surgery in previous 4 weeks | 1.5 |
| Previous DVT/PE | 1.5 |
| Haemoptysis | 1.0 |
| Malignancy (treated within 6 months or palliative) | 1.0 |

  (3m for the full table; partial credit per correctly-weighted row.)

- **Interpretation (1m):** **Three-tier** — low <2, moderate 2–6, high >6. **Two-tier (dichotomised)** —
  **≤4 = "PE unlikely"** (do D-dimer), **>4 = "PE likely"** (do CTPA). *Worked example:* signs of DVT (3) +
  PE most likely (3) + HR>100 (1.5) = **7.5 → high / "PE likely" → CTPA.** (1m)

[ ] **The two-tier cut-off (≤4 vs >4 → D-dimer vs CTPA)** — ~1m

**Learn it:** Harrison's — *Deep Venous Thrombosis & Pulmonary Thromboembolism*. · Davidson's —
*Venous thromboembolism*. [verify current edition]

### Q2. Wells score for deep vein thrombosis (5m)
<!-- id: sys08-q2 -->

**Restated:** Outline the Wells score for the clinical probability of deep vein thrombosis (DVT).

- **Use (1m):** pre-test probability for lower-limb DVT; **low probability + negative D-dimer rules out DVT**
  without imaging, while higher scores proceed to **compression ultrasonography** of the leg veins. (1m)
- **Score table (3m):**

| Criterion | Points |
|---|---|
| Active cancer (treatment within 6 months or palliative) | +1 |
| Paralysis/paresis or recent plaster immobilisation of leg | +1 |
| Recently bedridden ≥3 days, or major surgery within 12 weeks | +1 |
| Localised tenderness along deep venous system | +1 |
| Entire leg swollen | +1 |
| Calf swelling >3 cm vs asymptomatic side (10 cm below tibial tuberosity) | +1 |
| Pitting oedema confined to symptomatic leg | +1 |
| Collateral superficial (non-varicose) veins | +1 |
| Previously documented DVT | +1 |
| **Alternative diagnosis at least as likely as DVT** | **−2** |

  (3m for the full table; note the single **−2** subtraction is the most-missed row.)

- **Interpretation (1m):** **two-tier** — **≤1 = "DVT unlikely"** (D-dimer; if negative, DVT excluded);
  **≥2 = "DVT likely"** (proceed to compression ultrasound). (Older three-tier: 0 low, 1–2 moderate, ≥3
  high.) (1m)

**What to draw / tabulate:** [ ] **The score table including the −2 alternative-diagnosis row** — ~2m ·
[ ] **≤1 vs ≥2 pathway (D-dimer vs ultrasound)** — ~1m

**Learn it:** Harrison's — *Deep Venous Thrombosis & Pulmonary Thromboembolism*. · Davidson's —
*Venous thromboembolism*. [verify current edition]

### Q3. Glasgow-Blatchford score for upper GI bleeding (5m)
<!-- id: sys08-q3 -->

**Restated:** Describe the Glasgow-Blatchford score (GBS) and its use in upper GI bleeding.

- **Use (1m):** a **pre-endoscopy** risk score using only clinical + lab data (no endoscopic findings) to
  identify low-risk patients with acute upper GI bleeding who may be **safely managed as outpatients**.
  A score of **0 (or ≤1)** identifies very-low-risk patients needing neither transfusion nor urgent
  endoscopy. (1m)
- **Score components (3m):**

| Parameter | Range of points |
|---|---|
| Blood urea (mmol/L: 6.5–<8 / 8–<10 / 10–<25 / ≥25) | 2 / 3 / 4 / 6 |
| Haemoglobin, men (g/L: 120–<130 / 100–<120 / <100) | 1 / 3 / 6 |
| Haemoglobin, women (g/L: 100–<120 / <100) | 1 / 6 |
| Systolic BP (mmHg: 100–109 / 90–99 / <90) | 1 / 2 / 3 |
| Pulse ≥100/min | 1 |
| Melaena | 1 |
| Syncope | 2 |
| Hepatic disease | 2 |
| Cardiac failure | 2 |

  (3m — credit the **urea, haemoglobin, SBP** weighted tiers; the +1/+2 binary flags are the rest.)

- **Interpretation (1m):** higher score → higher risk of needing intervention (transfusion, endoscopic
  therapy, surgery) or death. **Score 0** ⇒ low risk, consider outpatient management; any higher score
  ⇒ admit + endoscopy. (Contrast with the **Rockall** score, which is post-endoscopy.) (1m)

**What to draw / tabulate:** [ ] **The parameter/points table (urea, Hb by sex, SBP tiers + binary flags)**
— ~2m · [ ] **The score-0 = low-risk/outpatient rule** — ~1m

**Learn it:** Harrison's — *Gastrointestinal Bleeding*. · Davidson's — *Upper GI bleeding*. [verify]

### Q4. Alvarado score for acute appendicitis (5m)
<!-- id: sys08-q4 -->

**Restated:** Describe the Alvarado (MANTRELS) score for acute appendicitis.

- **Use (1m):** a clinical score (mnemonic **MANTRELS**) stratifying the **probability of acute
  appendicitis** in a patient with right-iliac-fossa pain, guiding discharge, observation, imaging or
  surgery. Total **10**. (1m)
- **Score table (3m):**

| Feature (MANTRELS) | Points |
|---|---|
| **M** — Migration of pain to RIF | 1 |
| **A** — Anorexia | 1 |
| **N** — Nausea/vomiting | 1 |
| **T** — Tenderness in RIF | 2 |
| **R** — Rebound tenderness | 1 |
| **E** — Elevated temperature (≥37.3 °C) | 1 |
| **L** — Leucocytosis (WBC >10 ×10⁹/L) | 2 |
| **S** — Shift of WBC to left (neutrophilia >75%) | 1 |
| **Total** | **10** |

  (3m — the two **2-point** items, RIF tenderness and leucocytosis, are the most-missed.)

- **Interpretation (1m):** **1–4** = appendicitis unlikely (consider discharge/alternative); **5–6** =
  compatible, **observe / image**; **7–10** = probable appendicitis, **surgical referral**. *Worked example:*
  migration (1) + RIF tenderness (2) + rebound (1) + leucocytosis (2) + left shift (1) = **7 → probable,
  refer for surgery.** (1m)

**What to draw / tabulate:** [ ] **The MANTRELS 8-feature table with the two 2-point items** — ~2m ·
[ ] **The 3-band cut-offs (≤4 / 5–6 / 7–10)** — ~1m

**Learn it:** API — *Acute abdomen / Appendicitis*. · Davidson's — *The acute abdomen*. [verify]

### Q5. APACHE II score — overview (5m)
<!-- id: sys08-q5 -->

**Restated:** Write a short note on the APACHE II severity-of-disease score.

- **Definition / use (1½m):** **A**cute **P**hysiology **A**nd **C**hronic **H**ealth **E**valuation II — an
  ICU severity score computed within **24 h of admission** that predicts **in-hospital mortality** and
  allows case-mix comparison between units. Higher score → higher predicted mortality. (1½m)
- **Three components (2m):**

| Component | What it scores | Max |
|---|---|---|
| **Acute Physiology Score (APS)** | 12 physiological variables — temperature, MAP, heart rate, respiratory rate, oxygenation (A-aDO₂/PaO₂), arterial pH, serum Na⁺, K⁺, creatinine, haematocrit, WBC, GCS | 0–60 |
| **Age points** | banded (≤44 → 0, up to ≥75 → 6) | 0–6 |
| **Chronic Health points** | severe organ insufficiency / immunocompromise (more if emergency surgery / non-operative) | 0–5 |

  Total = APS + age + chronic-health = **0–71**. (2m)

- **Interpretation (1½m):** roughly — **0–4 ≈ ~4%** predicted mortality, **5–9 ~8%**, **10–14 ~15%**,
  **15–19 ~25%**, **20–24 ~40%**, **25–29 ~55%**, **30–34 ~75%**, **≥35 ~85%** (figures vary with diagnosis
  category weighting). Scored once on the **worst values in the first 24 h**. (1½m)

**What to draw / tabulate:** [ ] **The three-component table (APS + age + chronic health → 0–71)** — ~1½m ·
[ ] **The mortality-by-score-band ladder** — ~1m

**Learn it:** Harrison's — *Approach to the Patient in the ICU* / Critical Care. · API — *Critical Care —
severity scoring*. [verify current edition — exact weights are from Knaus 1985]

### Q6. NYHA and CCS functional classifications (5m)
<!-- id: sys08-q6 -->

**Restated:** Describe the NYHA classification of heart failure and the CCS grading of angina.

- **Use (1m):** symptom-based **functional** gradings of cardiac limitation — **NYHA** for heart-failure
  breathlessness/fatigue, **CCS** (Canadian Cardiovascular Society) for **angina** on exertion. Both guide
  prognosis and treatment escalation and are quick to reassess at follow-up. (1m)
- **NYHA classification (2m):**

| Class | Limitation |
|---|---|
| **I** | No limitation; ordinary activity causes no symptoms |
| **II** | Slight limitation; comfortable at rest, ordinary activity → symptoms |
| **III** | Marked limitation; less-than-ordinary activity → symptoms (comfortable only at rest) |
| **IV** | Symptoms at rest; any activity worsens them |

  (2m for the four classes correctly distinguished.)

- **CCS angina classification (1½m):**

| Class | Angina threshold |
|---|---|
| **I** | Only with strenuous/prolonged exertion |
| **II** | Slight limitation — angina on walking >2 blocks / >1 flight of stairs, or in cold/after meals |
| **III** | Marked limitation — angina on walking 1–2 blocks / 1 flight |
| **IV** | Inability to do any activity without angina, or angina at rest |

  (1½m for the four grades.)

- **Pearl (½m):** both are **symptom/functional** scales (not anatomical); NYHA class is independent of
  ejection fraction and can change with therapy. (½m)

**What to draw / tabulate:** [ ] **NYHA I–IV table** — ~1½m · [ ] **CCS I–IV table** — ~1m

**Learn it:** Harrison's — *Heart Failure* and *Ischaemic Heart Disease / Angina*. · Davidson's —
*Cardiovascular disease — functional assessment*. [verify]

### Q7. Centor / McIsaac score for streptococcal pharyngitis (5m)
<!-- id: sys08-q7 -->

**Restated:** Describe the Centor score (with McIsaac age modification) for sore throat.

- **Use (1m):** estimates the probability that acute pharyngitis is **group A streptococcal**, to guide
  whether to test (RADT/throat culture) and whether to give antibiotics — reducing unnecessary
  prescribing. (1m)
- **Centor criteria + McIsaac age modifier (2½m):**

| Criterion | Points |
|---|---|
| **C** — tonsillar exudate | +1 |
| Tender anterior **cervical** lymphadenopathy | +1 |
| History of **fever** (>38 °C) | +1 |
| **Absence of cough** | +1 |
| **McIsaac age modifier:** 3–14 years | +1 |
| 15–44 years | 0 |
| ≥45 years | −1 |

  (2½m — the **age modifier rows** are the McIsaac addition and the commonly-missed part.)

- **Interpretation (1½m):** **≤0 → no test/antibiotic; 1 → no antibiotic; 2–3 → test (RADT/culture), treat
  if positive; ≥4 → high probability, test ± empirical antibiotic.** First-line treatment if confirmed:
  **penicillin V / amoxicillin** (macrolide if penicillin-allergic). *Worked example:* exudate (1) + tender
  nodes (1) + fever (1) + no cough (1), age 30 (0) = **4 → high probability, test ± treat.** (1½m)

**What to draw / tabulate:** [ ] **The 4 Centor criteria + 3-band age modifier table** — ~2m ·
[ ] **The score → test/treat decision ladder** — ~1m

**Learn it:** Harrison's — *Pharyngitis / Streptococcal infections*. · API — *Upper respiratory infections*.

### Q8. MMSE and MoCA cognitive screening tools (5m)
<!-- id: sys08-q8 -->

**Restated:** Write a short note on the MMSE and MoCA as bedside cognitive screening tools.

- **Use (1m):** brief, scored **cognitive screening** instruments (both out of **30**) used to detect and
  serially monitor cognitive impairment/dementia and delirium — **screening, not diagnostic** tools; results
  are adjusted for education and language. (1m)
- **MMSE — domains (2m):**

| Domain | Marks |
|---|---|
| Orientation (time + place) | 10 |
| Registration (3 words) | 3 |
| Attention/calculation (serial 7s or WORLD backwards) | 5 |
| Recall (3 words) | 3 |
| Language (naming, repetition, 3-stage command, read, write, copy pentagons) | 9 |
| **Total** | **30** |

  (2m for the domains summing to 30.)

- **Interpretation + MoCA (1½m):** **MMSE:** ≥24 normal; **18–23 mild, 10–17 moderate, <10 severe**
  impairment. **MoCA** (also /30) adds **executive function (trail-making, clock-drawing), abstraction and
  more demanding recall**, so it is **more sensitive for mild cognitive impairment**; **normal ≥26**, and
  **+1 point if ≤12 years' education**. (1½m)
- **Pearl (½m):** MMSE is relatively insensitive to **frontal/executive** dysfunction and early MCI —
  prefer **MoCA** when those are the concern. (½m)

**What to draw / tabulate:** [ ] **MMSE domain/marks table (=30)** — ~1½m · [ ] **MMSE severity bands +
MoCA ≥26 cut-off / education adjustment** — ~1m

**Learn it:** Harrison's — *Dementia* / *Cognitive Assessment*. · Davidson's — *Cognitive function testing*.
