# Chapter 2 — Electrostatic Potential & Capacitance: MCQs + Assertion–Reason

> Section A (1 mark each). One-mark objective and assertion–reason items on potential, equipotential surfaces, E = −dV/dr, capacitance, dielectrics, series/parallel combos and energy. Answer + one-line reason after each.

## Multiple Choice Questions (1m each)

### Q1. Work done moving a charge on an equipotential surface (1m)
A charge is moved between two points on the same equipotential surface. The work done by the electric field is:
- (a) maximum  (b) qV  (c) zero  (d) infinite

**Answer: (c) zero.**
Reason: W = q(V_A − V_B); on an equipotential surface V_A = V_B, so W = 0.

### Q2. Potential at the centre of a uniformly charged ring/shell (1m)
The electric potential at the centre of a charged conducting spherical shell of radius R carrying charge Q is:
- (a) zero  (b) kQ/R²  (c) kQ/R  (d) infinite

**Answer: (c) kQ/R.**
Reason: Inside a conductor V is constant and equals the surface value kQ/R, even though E = 0 inside.

### Q3. Relation between E and V (1m)
The electric field is related to potential by:
- (a) E = +dV/dr  (b) E = −dV/dr  (c) E = V·r  (d) E = dV/dt

**Answer: (b) E = −dV/dr.**
Reason: Field points from high to low potential; the negative sign shows E is the negative potential gradient.

### Q4. SI unit of capacitance (1m)
The farad is equivalent to:
- (a) C/V  (b) V/C  (c) C·V  (d) J/C

**Answer: (a) C/V.**
Reason: C = Q/V, so 1 F = 1 coulomb per volt.

### Q5. Effect of dielectric on parallel-plate capacitance (1m)
Inserting a dielectric slab of constant K to completely fill a parallel-plate capacitor changes its capacitance to:
- (a) C/K  (b) KC  (c) C  (d) K²C

**Answer: (b) KC.**
Reason: C = Kε₀A/d; the dielectric increases capacitance by the factor K.

### Q6. Capacitors in series — equivalent value (1m)
Two capacitors of 6 µF and 3 µF in series give an equivalent capacitance of:
- (a) 9 µF  (b) 2 µF  (c) 18 µF  (d) 4.5 µF

**Answer: (b) 2 µF.**
Reason: 1/C_eq = 1/6 + 1/3 = 1/2 ⇒ C_eq = 2 µF (series value is smaller than the smallest).

### Q7. Charge on series capacitors (1m)
For capacitors connected in series across a battery, the quantity that is the same on every capacitor is:
- (a) potential difference  (b) charge  (c) capacitance  (d) energy

**Answer: (b) charge.**
Reason: In series the same charge is induced through every capacitor; the voltage divides in inverse ratio of C.

### Q8. Energy stored in a capacitor (1m)
A capacitor of capacitance C charged to potential V stores energy:
- (a) CV  (b) ½CV  (c) ½CV²  (d) C²V

**Answer: (c) ½CV².**
Reason: U = ½CV² = ½QV = Q²/2C (three equivalent forms).

### Q9. Energy density of an electric field (1m)
The energy stored per unit volume in a region of electric field E (vacuum) is:
- (a) ½ε₀E  (b) ½ε₀E²  (c) ε₀E²  (d) ½E²/ε₀

**Answer: (b) ½ε₀E².**
Reason: u = U/Volume = ½ε₀E²; it depends only on the field, not on the plates.

### Q10. Capacitor disconnected, then dielectric inserted (1m)
A charged parallel-plate capacitor is disconnected from the battery and a dielectric (K) is then inserted. Which quantity stays constant?
- (a) potential difference  (b) charge  (c) capacitance  (d) energy

**Answer: (b) charge.**
Reason: Isolated plates can't lose charge; Q is fixed, so V = Q/C falls and C rises by K.

### Q11. Potential due to a dipole on the equatorial line (1m)
The electric potential at any point on the equatorial plane (perpendicular bisector) of an electric dipole is:
- (a) maximum  (b) kp/r²  (c) zero  (d) 2kp/r²

**Answer: (c) zero.**
Reason: The two equal-and-opposite charges are equidistant from any equatorial point, so their potentials cancel.

### Q12. Three identical capacitors — series vs parallel ratio (1m)
For three identical capacitors C, the ratio of equivalent capacitance in parallel to that in series is:
- (a) 3 : 1  (b) 1 : 3  (c) 9 : 1  (d) 1 : 9

**Answer: (c) 9 : 1.**
Reason: Parallel = 3C; series = C/3; ratio = 3C ÷ (C/3) = 9.

### Q13. Equipotential surface around a point charge (1m)
Equipotential surfaces due to an isolated point charge are:
- (a) planes  (b) concentric spheres  (c) cylinders  (d) ellipsoids

**Answer: (b) concentric spheres.**
Reason: V = kQ/r is constant for constant r, i.e. on spheres centred on the charge; field lines are radial and ⟂ to them.

### Q14. Capacitor energy when voltage is doubled (1m)
If the voltage across a capacitor is doubled (C unchanged), the stored energy becomes:
- (a) 2×  (b) 4×  (c) ½×  (d) unchanged

**Answer: (b) 4×.**
Reason: U = ½CV² ∝ V², so doubling V quadruples U.

### Q15. Spacing between equipotential surfaces and field strength (1m)
Where equipotential surfaces (drawn for equal potential steps) are crowded close together, the electric field is:
- (a) weak  (b) zero  (c) strong  (d) constant

**Answer: (c) strong.**
Reason: E = −dV/dr; for a fixed ΔV, small spacing dr means large E.

## Assertion–Reason (1m each)

> Mark: (a) Both A and R true, R correct explanation of A; (b) Both true, R NOT the correct explanation; (c) A true, R false; (d) A false, R true.

### Q16. A-R: Work on equipotential surface (1m)
**Assertion (A):** No work is done in moving a charge along an equipotential surface.
**Reason (R):** The electric field is everywhere perpendicular to an equipotential surface.

**Answer: (a).** Both true and R explains A: since E ⟂ surface, the displacement along the surface is ⟂ to the force, so W = F·d = 0.

### Q17. A-R: E inside a charged conductor (1m)
**Assertion (A):** The electric potential inside a charged hollow conductor is constant.
**Reason (R):** The electric field inside the conductor is zero.

**Answer: (a).** Both true and R explains A: E = −dV/dr = 0 inside ⇒ V cannot change ⇒ V is constant throughout.

### Q18. A-R: Dielectric increases capacitance (1m)
**Assertion (A):** Inserting a dielectric slab increases the capacitance of a parallel-plate capacitor.
**Reason (R):** The dielectric reduces the net electric field between the plates by polarisation.

**Answer: (a).** Both true and R explains A: polarisation sets up an opposing field, reducing net E (and V = Ed); since C = Q/V, lower V at fixed Q means higher C.

### Q19. A-R: Energy on inserting dielectric with battery connected (1m)
**Assertion (A):** When a dielectric is inserted into a capacitor that stays connected to a battery, the stored energy increases.
**Reason (R):** With the battery connected, V is constant and U = ½CV² rises because C increases.

**Answer: (a).** Both true and R explains A: V fixed, C → KC, so U = ½CV² grows by factor K (extra energy supplied by the battery).

### Q20. A-R: Series capacitance value (1m)
**Assertion (A):** The equivalent capacitance of capacitors in series is less than the smallest individual capacitance.
**Reason (R):** In series the reciprocals add: 1/C_eq = Σ 1/Cᵢ.

**Answer: (a).** Both true and R explains A: adding positive reciprocals makes 1/C_eq larger than each 1/Cᵢ, hence C_eq smaller than the smallest C.

### Q21. A-R: Potential is scalar (1m)
**Assertion (A):** The net potential at a point due to several charges is the algebraic sum of individual potentials.
**Reason (R):** Electric potential is a scalar quantity.

**Answer: (a).** Both true and R explains A: being scalar, contributions add algebraically (with sign) — no vector resolution needed, unlike the field.

### Q22. A-R: Potential at dipole's equatorial point (1m)
**Assertion (A):** The potential at every point on the equatorial line of a dipole is zero, but the electric field there is not zero.
**Reason (R):** Potential is scalar (the ±q contributions cancel) while field is a vector (the ±q contributions add).

**Answer: (a).** Both true and R explains A: equal-and-opposite potentials cancel (V = 0), but the field vectors combine to a non-zero resultant directed antiparallel to p.
