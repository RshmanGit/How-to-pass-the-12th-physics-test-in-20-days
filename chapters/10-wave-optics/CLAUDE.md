# Chapter 10 — Wave Optics (Content Map)

> Per-directory content map for Claude Code. Pointers + status only.

## Chapter

- **Number / name:** Chapter 10 — Wave Optics
- **Approx weightage:** ~4 marks (part of the Optics unit). Typically yields 1 derivation/long
  answer (YDSE or single-slit or Huygens) + 1 short answer (polarisation/coherence) + 1 numerical
  on the board paper.
- **Difficulty:** Medium. Derivations are standard and high-yield; numericals are short, plug-in,
  and repeat almost verbatim from NCERT.
- **Layout:** SINGLE file.

## Important / repeated questions (high board frequency)

- **Huygens' principle — laws of reflection** (Q16) and **laws of refraction / Snell's law** (Q17).
  One of these two appears almost every year as a 3-5m derivation.
- **YDSE fringe-width derivation β = λD/d** + bright/dark conditions (Q18) — the single most
  repeated 5m question in the chapter.
- **Single-slit diffraction + width of central maximum 2λD/a** (Q19) — recurring 3-5m derivation.
- **Polarisation by reflection + Brewster's law μ = tan iₚ + iₚ + r = 90°** (Q14, Q20) — perennial.
- **Coherent sources: definition + conditions; why two bulbs fail** (Q10, Q7) — frequent 1-2m.
- **Effect of immersing YDSE in water (β → β/μ)** (Q2) — common MCQ.
- **Malus's law numerical (two/three polaroids)** (Q27) and **fringe-width numericals** (Q21, Q22).
- **Polaroid sunglasses / glare** case study (Q30).

### Key derivations (mark-carrying)
- Laws of reflection by Huygens' construction — Q16
- Laws of refraction / Snell's law by Huygens' construction — Q17
- YDSE fringe width β = λD/d (+ bright/dark conditions, I = 4I₀cos²(φ/2)) — Q18
- Single-slit minima a sinθ = nλ and central-max width 2λD/a — Q19
- Brewster's law and proof reflected ⟂ refracted (iₚ + r = 90°) — Q14, Q20

### Repeated NCERT / board numericals (verified values)
- Red 6500 Å, d = 1 mm, D = 1 m → β = 0.65 mm — Q21
- d = 0.24 mm, D = 1.2 m, β = 0.03 cm → λ = 600 nm — Q22
- Two wavelengths 650 nm & 520 nm: 3rd bright = 1950(D/d) nm; coincidence at 2600(D/d) nm
  (n₁:n₂ = 4:5) — Q23 (NCERT)
- d = 0.28 mm, D = 1.4 m, 4th bright at 1.2 cm → λ = 600 nm — Q24 (NCERT)
- Single slit: λ = 600 nm, a = 1 mm, D = 2 m → 1st dark at 1.2 mm, central width 2.4 mm — Q25
- Brewster angle, μ = 1.5 → iₚ = 56.3°, r = 33.7° — Q26 (NCERT)
- Malus, two polaroids at 30°, I₀ unpolarised → 3I₀/8 — Q27
- Water μ = 1.33, λ = 589 nm → v = 2.26×10⁸ m/s, λ_w ≈ 443 nm — Q28 (NCERT)
- Intensity ratio 81:1 → I_max:I_min = 25:16 — Q29

## Syllabus / scope notes (2025-26)

- All topics here are in the current rationalised CBSE Class 12 syllabus: Huygens' principle &
  wavefronts; reflection/refraction by wavefronts; interference & YDSE (fringe width retained as a
  standard board derivation); single-slit diffraction (qualitative + central-maximum width);
  polarisation, Malus's law, Brewster's angle.
- **Deliberately excluded** (deleted / not required in 2025-26): the **Doppler effect of light /
  red shift**, and detailed **Fresnel-distance / validity-of-ray-optics** numericals. Some source
  pages still list these (and Doppler redshift numericals) — they were dropped here to keep scope
  current. Resolving power remains conceptually in syllabus but is low-frequency; not included as a
  full question.
