# Chapter 1 — Electric Charges and Fields: Numericals (worked solutions)

> Repeated board / NCERT numericals. Each is solved as: formula → substitution → answer with units. Take k = 1/4πε₀ = 9×10⁹ N m² C⁻², ε₀ = 8.85×10⁻¹² C² N⁻¹ m⁻².

---

### Q1. Force between two point charges (2m)

Two small spheres carry charges 2×10⁻⁷ C and 3×10⁻⁷ C and are 30 cm apart in air. Find the force between them.

**Formula:** F = k·q₁q₂/r²
**Substitute:** F = (9×10⁹)(2×10⁻⁷)(3×10⁻⁷)/(0.30)²
= (9×10⁹)(6×10⁻¹⁴)/0.09
**Answer:** **F = 6×10⁻³ N** (repulsive, since both charges are positive).

---

### Q2. Find the separation given the force (2m)

A charge of 0.4 µC experiences a force of 0.2 N due to a charge of −0.8 µC. Find the distance between them.

**Formula:** r = √(k·q₁q₂/F)
**Substitute:** r = √[(9×10⁹)(0.4×10⁻⁶)(0.8×10⁻⁶)/0.2]
= √[(9×10⁹)(3.2×10⁻¹³)/0.2] = √(1.44×10⁻²)
**Answer:** **r = 0.12 m = 12 cm.** By Newton's third law the force on the second charge is also **0.2 N** (opposite direction; attractive).

---

### Q3. Coulomb force, then change of charge and distance (3m)

Two identical spheres each carry 6.5×10⁻⁷ C and are 50 cm apart.
(i) Find the mutual force.
(ii) Each charge is doubled and the separation is halved — find the new force.

**(i)** F = k q²/r² = (9×10⁹)(6.5×10⁻⁷)²/(0.5)²
= (9×10⁹)(4.225×10⁻¹³)/0.25
**= 1.52×10⁻² N (repulsive).**

**(ii)** Charge → 2q (each), distance → r/2.
F' = k(2q)(2q)/(r/2)² = 16·(k q²/r²) = 16 × 1.52×10⁻²
**= 0.243 N.**

### Q4. Field at the midpoint of two opposite charges (3m)

Charges +3 µC and −3 µC are placed 20 cm apart. Find the electric field at the midpoint and the force on a −1.5×10⁻⁹ C charge placed there.

**Field of each at midpoint (r = 0.10 m):** E₁ = k q/r² = (9×10⁹)(3×10⁻⁶)/(0.10)² = 2.7×10⁶ N C⁻¹.
**Both fields point the same way** (from + toward −), so they add:
E = 2 × 2.7×10⁶ = **5.4×10⁶ N C⁻¹**, directed from + to − charge.
**Force on test charge:** F = qE = (1.5×10⁻⁹)(5.4×10⁶) = **8.1×10⁻³ N** (toward the +3 µC charge, since the charge is negative).

### Q5. Torque on a dipole in a uniform field (2m)

A dipole of moment 4×10⁻⁹ C m is held at 30° to a uniform field of 5×10⁴ N C⁻¹. Find the torque.

**Formula:** τ = pE sinθ
**Substitute:** τ = (4×10⁻⁹)(5×10⁴)(sin30°) = (4×10⁻⁹)(5×10⁴)(0.5)
**Answer:** **τ = 1×10⁻⁴ N m.**

---

### Q6. Dipole moment of a charge pair (2m)

Charges +2.5×10⁻⁷ C and −2.5×10⁻⁷ C are located at (0, 0, +15 cm) and (0, 0, −15 cm). Find the net charge and the dipole moment.

**Net charge:** +2.5×10⁻⁷ + (−2.5×10⁻⁷) = **0.**
**Dipole moment:** p = q × 2a = (2.5×10⁻⁷)(0.30) = **7.5×10⁻⁸ C m**, directed from −q to +q, i.e. along the **+z axis**.

### Q7. Flux through a square — field parallel and at an angle (3m)

A uniform field E = 3×10³ î N C⁻¹ passes through a square of side 10 cm.
(a) Plane of the square is parallel to the y-z plane.
(b) Plane makes a 60° angle with the x-axis.

**Area:** A = (0.10)² = 1×10⁻² m².
**(a)** Area vector along x, parallel to E → θ = 0°:
φ = E A cos0° = (3×10³)(1×10⁻²)(1) = **30 N m² C⁻¹.**
**(b)** The normal makes 60° with E (plane tilted 60° from x means normal is 60° from E):
φ = E A cos60° = (3×10³)(1×10⁻²)(0.5) = **15 N m² C⁻¹.**

### Q8. Net flux and enclosed charge for a cube (3m)

A point charge of 2.0 µC is at the centre of a cube of edge 9.0 cm. (a) Find the net flux through the cube. (b) Find the flux through one face.

**(a)** By Gauss's law, net flux = q/ε₀ = (2.0×10⁻⁶)/(8.85×10⁻¹²)
= **2.26×10⁵ N m² C⁻¹.** (Independent of edge length.)
**(b)** By symmetry, flux through one face = (1/6)(2.26×10⁵) = **3.76×10⁴ N m² C⁻¹.**

---

### Q9. Flux due to a point charge above a square (3m)

A +10 µC charge is at the centre of a square of side 10 cm, held at a height 5 cm above the plane of the square. Find the flux through the square.

**Reasoning:** Imagine the charge at the centre of a cube of edge 10 cm; this square is then one face. Flux through one face = (1/6)(q/ε₀).
φ = (1/6)(10×10⁻⁶/8.85×10⁻¹²) = (1/6)(1.13×10⁶)
**Answer:** **φ ≈ 1.88×10⁵ N m² C⁻¹.**

---

### Q10. Conducting sphere — charge from field (2m)

The electric field 20 cm from the centre of a 10 cm radius conducting sphere is 1.5×10³ N C⁻¹ directed radially inward. Find the net charge on the sphere.

**Formula (treat as point charge outside):** q = E r²/k = E r²·4πε₀
**Substitute:** q = (1.5×10³)(0.20)²/(9×10⁹) = (1.5×10³)(0.04)/(9×10⁹)
**Answer:** **q = 6.67×10⁻⁹ C = 6.67 nC**, and it is **negative** (field points inward).

---

### Q11. Charge and flux from surface charge density (3m)

A conducting sphere of diameter 2.4 m carries a uniform surface charge density of 80 µC m⁻². Find (a) the total charge, (b) the total flux leaving the surface.

**Radius:** R = 1.2 m → Area = 4πR² = 4π(1.2)² = 18.1 m².
**(a)** Q = σ·A = (80×10⁻⁶)(18.1) = **1.45×10⁻³ C.**
**(b)** φ = Q/ε₀ = (1.45×10⁻³)/(8.85×10⁻¹²) = **1.63×10⁸ N m² C⁻¹.**

---

### Q12. Null point between two like charges (3m)

Charges +q and +9q are 10a apart. Find the point on the line joining them where the net field is zero.

**Set the fields equal at distance x from +q:**
k·q/x² = k·9q/(10a − x)²
**Take square roots:** (10a − x)/x = 3 → 10a − x = 3x → 4x = 10a.
**Answer:** **x = 2.5a from +q** (and 7.5a from +9q). The null point lies between them, closer to the smaller charge.

---

### Q13. Flux through a sphere enclosing several charges (2m)

Charges +2q, −q, and +3q lie inside a closed surface. Find the net flux through the surface.

**Net enclosed charge:** +2q − q + 3q = +4q.
**Flux:** φ = q_enclosed/ε₀ = **4q/ε₀.**
(Charges outside the surface contribute zero net flux.)

### Q14. Electron in a uniform field — acceleration (2m)

An electron (m = 9.1×10⁻³¹ kg, e = 1.6×10⁻¹⁹ C) is placed in a uniform field of 2×10⁴ N C⁻¹. Find its acceleration.

**Formula:** a = F/m = eE/m
**Substitute:** a = (1.6×10⁻¹⁹)(2×10⁴)/(9.1×10⁻³¹) = (3.2×10⁻¹⁵)/(9.1×10⁻³¹)
**Answer:** **a ≈ 3.52×10¹⁵ m s⁻²** (directed opposite to E, since the electron is negative).

---

### Q15. Surface charge density ratio of two connected spheres (3m)

Two conducting spheres of radii R₁ and R₂ (R₁ < R₂) are connected by a thin wire. Show how their surface charge densities compare.

**On connection both reach the same potential:** V₁ = V₂
k q₁/R₁ = k q₂/R₂ → q₁/q₂ = R₁/R₂.
**Surface charge density σ = q/4πR²:**
σ₁/σ₂ = (q₁/q₂)(R₂²/R₁²) = (R₁/R₂)(R₂²/R₁²) = **R₂/R₁.**
**Conclusion:** σ ∝ 1/R — the smaller sphere has the **larger** surface charge density (basis of action of points / lightning conductors).

---

### Q16. Three charges on an equilateral triangle (3m)

Three charges each +q sit at the corners of an equilateral triangle of side L. Find the magnitude of the net force on any one charge.

**Each pair force:** F = k q²/L².
**Two such forces act on one charge at 60° to each other.**
F_net = √(F² + F² + 2F²cos60°) = F√(2 + 1) = **√3·F = √3·k q²/L²**, directed radially outward (away from the centroid).

---

### Q17. Charge enclosed from net flux (Q1.13-type) (2m)

The net outward flux through the surface of a box is 8.0×10³ N m² C⁻¹. Find the net charge inside.

**Formula:** q = ε₀·φ
**Substitute:** q = (8.85×10⁻¹²)(8.0×10³)
**Answer:** **q = 7.08×10⁻⁸ C ≈ 0.07 µC.** If the net flux were zero it would only mean the *net* charge is zero, not that there is no charge.
