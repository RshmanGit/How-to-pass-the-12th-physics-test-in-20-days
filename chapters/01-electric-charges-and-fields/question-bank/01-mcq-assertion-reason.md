# Chapter 1 — Electric Charges and Fields: MCQs & Assertion-Reason (Section A, 1m each)

> One-mark objective drill. Each MCQ gives the correct option + a one-line reason; each Assertion-Reason uses the standard CBSE option key (a/b/c/d).

**Assertion-Reason option key (used throughout this file):**
- (a) Both A and R are true, and R is the correct explanation of A.
- (b) Both A and R are true, but R is NOT the correct explanation of A.
- (c) A is true but R is false.
- (d) A is false but R is true (or both false).

## Multiple Choice Questions

### Q1. SI unit and nature of electric flux (1m)

Electric flux through a surface is best described as:

(A) a vector with unit N C⁻¹
(B) a scalar with unit N m² C⁻¹
(C) a scalar with unit C m⁻²
(D) a vector with unit V m

**Answer: (B)** — Flux φ = E·A·cosθ is a scalar (dot product of two vectors); unit N m² C⁻¹ (= V m).

---

### Q2. Charge is quantised (1m)

Which of the following is NOT a possible value of charge on a body? (e = 1.6×10⁻¹⁹ C)

(A) 4.8×10⁻¹⁹ C
(B) 3.2×10⁻¹⁹ C
(C) 2.4×10⁻¹⁹ C
(D) 1.6×10⁻¹⁹ C

**Answer: (C)** — Charge must be an integral multiple of e. 2.4×10⁻¹⁹ C = 1.5e, not an integer → impossible.

---

### Q3. Coulomb force and medium (1m)

The force between two point charges in vacuum is F. When a medium of dielectric constant K = 5 fills the space between them (separation unchanged), the new force is:

(A) 5F
(B) F/5
(C) 25F
(D) F/25

**Answer: (B)** — F_medium = F_vacuum / K = F/5. The medium reduces the force by factor K.

---

### Q4. Force vs separation (1m)

If the distance between two fixed point charges is halved, the Coulomb force between them becomes:

(A) half
(B) double
(C) one-fourth
(D) four times

**Answer: (D)** — F ∝ 1/r²; halving r multiplies F by 4.

---

### Q5. Electric field inside a conductor (1m)

In electrostatic equilibrium, the electric field at any point inside a charged conductor is:

(A) maximum at the centre
(B) σ/ε₀
(C) zero
(D) σ/2ε₀

**Answer: (C)** — Free charges redistribute on the surface until the net interior field is zero.

---

### Q6. Field just outside a charged conductor (1m)

The electric field just outside the surface of a charged conductor with surface charge density σ is:

(A) σ/2ε₀
(B) σ/ε₀
(C) σ/4πε₀
(D) zero

**Answer: (B)** — E = σ/ε₀, directed normal to the surface (from Gauss's law on a pillbox; field exists on one side only).

---

### Q7. Field of an infinite plane sheet (1m)

The electric field due to an infinite charged plane sheet of surface charge density σ:

(A) decreases as 1/r²
(B) decreases as 1/r
(C) is independent of distance from the sheet
(D) increases with distance

**Answer: (C)** — E = σ/2ε₀, a constant (uniform field) independent of r.

---

### Q8. Field of an infinite line charge (1m)

The electric field due to an infinitely long straight charged wire of linear charge density λ at distance r varies as:

(A) 1/r²
(B) 1/r
(C) r
(D) constant

**Answer: (B)** — E = λ/2πε₀r, so E ∝ 1/r.

---

### Q9. Flux and enclosed charge (1m)

A point charge is placed at the centre of a cube. The flux through one face is:

(A) q/ε₀
(B) q/6ε₀
(C) q/8ε₀
(D) zero

**Answer: (B)** — Total flux = q/ε₀ is shared equally among 6 faces by symmetry → q/6ε₀.

---

### Q10. Flux when radius changes (1m)

A charge q is enclosed by a spherical Gaussian surface. If the radius of the sphere is doubled, the flux through it:

(A) doubles
(B) becomes one-fourth
(C) remains unchanged
(D) halves

**Answer: (C)** — By Gauss's law φ = q_enclosed/ε₀ depends only on enclosed charge, not on the size/shape of the surface.

---

### Q11. Flux through a surface enclosing a dipole (1m)

The net electric flux through a closed surface that encloses an electric dipole is:

(A) p/ε₀
(B) 2q/ε₀
(C) zero
(D) q/ε₀

**Answer: (C)** — A dipole has net charge zero, so q_enclosed = 0 → φ = 0.

---

### Q12. Torque on a dipole (1m)

An electric dipole of moment p is placed in a uniform field E. The torque is maximum when the angle θ between p and E is:

(A) 0°
(B) 45°
(C) 90°
(D) 180°

**Answer: (C)** — τ = pE sinθ is maximum (= pE) at θ = 90°.

---

### Q13. Stable equilibrium of a dipole (1m)

A dipole in a uniform electric field is in stable equilibrium when:

(A) p is perpendicular to E
(B) p is parallel to E (θ = 0°)
(C) p is antiparallel to E (θ = 180°)
(D) at every orientation

**Answer: (B)** — Potential energy U = −pE cosθ is minimum at θ = 0° → stable equilibrium.

---

### Q14. Net force on a dipole in a uniform field (1m)

The net translational force on an electric dipole placed in a uniform electric field is:

(A) pE
(B) 2qE
(C) zero
(D) pE sinθ

**Answer: (C)** — Equal and opposite forces (+qE and −qE) cancel; only a torque acts (net force is non-zero only in a non-uniform field).

---

### Q15. Axial vs equatorial field of a dipole (1m)

For a short dipole, the ratio of the axial field to the equatorial field at equal distances r is:

(A) 1:1
(B) 1:2
(C) 2:1
(D) 4:1

**Answer: (C)** — E_axial = 2kp/r³ and E_equatorial = kp/r³, so the ratio is 2:1.

---

### Q16. Field lines cannot intersect (1m)

Two electric field lines can never intersect because:

(A) field lines are always straight
(B) the field would have two directions at the point of intersection
(C) field lines repel each other
(D) the field is zero at the intersection

**Answer: (B)** — A tangent gives the field direction; two tangents at one point would mean two directions for E, which is impossible.

---

### Q17. Dimensional formula of electric field (1m)

The dimensional formula of electric field intensity is:

(A) [M L T⁻² A⁻¹]
(B) [M L T⁻³ A⁻¹]
(C) [M L² T⁻³ A⁻¹]
(D) [M L T⁻² A⁻²]

**Answer: (B)** — E = F/q = N/C = (kg·m·s⁻²)/(A·s) = [M L T⁻³ A⁻¹].

---

### Q18. Null point between two like charges (1m)

Charges +q and +9q are placed a distance 10a apart. The point on the line joining them where the resultant field is zero lies at a distance from +q equal to:

(A) 2.5a
(B) 5a
(C) 7.5a
(D) a

**Answer: (A)** — Setting fields equal: q/x² = 9q/(10a−x)² → (10a−x) = 3x → x = 2.5a.

## Assertion-Reason Questions

### Q19. AR: Flux independent of surface shape (1m)

**Assertion (A):** The net flux through a closed surface depends only on the charge enclosed, not on the shape or size of the surface.
**Reason (R):** Gauss's law states φ = q_enclosed/ε₀.

**Answer: (a)** — Both true and R correctly explains A. Gauss's law makes flux depend solely on enclosed charge.

---

### Q20. AR: Field inside a uniformly charged shell (1m)

**Assertion (A):** The electric field inside a uniformly charged spherical shell is zero.
**Reason (R):** The charge enclosed by any Gaussian surface drawn inside the shell is zero.

**Answer: (a)** — Both true; with q_enclosed = 0 inside, Gauss's law gives E = 0, so R explains A.

---

### Q21. AR: Force on a dipole in uniform field (1m)

**Assertion (A):** A dipole placed in a uniform electric field experiences a net force.
**Reason (R):** The two charges of the dipole experience equal and opposite forces.

**Answer: (d)** — A is false (net force is zero in a uniform field); R is true. The equal-and-opposite forces are exactly why the net force vanishes.

---

### Q22. AR: Field of a plane sheet (1m)

**Assertion (A):** The field due to an infinite plane sheet of charge is uniform and independent of distance.
**Reason (R):** The electric field due to a point charge varies as 1/r².

**Answer: (b)** — Both statements are true, but R (a point-charge fact) does not explain A (the plane-sheet result arises from symmetry/Gauss's law).

---

### Q23. AR: Quantisation of charge (1m)

**Assertion (A):** A body can have a charge of 1.0×10⁻¹⁹ C.
**Reason (R):** Charge is quantised in integral multiples of e = 1.6×10⁻¹⁹ C.

**Answer: (d)** — A is false (1.0×10⁻¹⁹ C is not an integer multiple of e); R is true.

---

### Q24. AR: Conductor surface and field direction (1m)

**Assertion (A):** The electric field at the surface of a charged conductor is perpendicular to the surface.
**Reason (R):** If there were a tangential component, free charges would move along the surface and the conductor would not be in electrostatic equilibrium.

**Answer: (a)** — Both true; R correctly explains why the field must be normal to the surface.

---

### Q25. AR: Coulomb's law and Newton's third law (1m)

**Assertion (A):** The Coulomb forces between two charges form an action-reaction pair.
**Reason (R):** The forces are equal in magnitude, opposite in direction, and act along the line joining the charges.

**Answer: (a)** — Both true; the stated properties are exactly the conditions for an action-reaction pair, so R explains A.
