# Chapter 9 — Ray Optics: Numericals (worked solutions)

> The repeated NCERT/board numericals. Every solution = formula → substitute (with signs) → answer with units. Mind the New Cartesian sign convention throughout.

### Q1. Concave mirror: candle image (3m)

**Type:** Numerical · **Section C**

A candle 2.5 cm tall is placed 27 cm in front of a concave mirror of radius of curvature 36 cm. Find the position, nature and size of the image.

**Solution:**
- f = R/2 = −36/2 = −18 cm; u = −27 cm. …(½m)
- Mirror formula: 1/v + 1/u = 1/f ⇒ 1/v = 1/f − 1/u = (−1/18) − (−1/27). …(1m)
- 1/v = −1/18 + 1/27 = (−3 + 2)/54 = −1/54 ⇒ **v = −54 cm**. …(½m)
- Magnification m = −v/u = −(−54)/(−27) = **−2**. Image height = m × 2.5 = **−5 cm**. …(½m)
- **Result:** image is 54 cm in front of the mirror, real, inverted, magnified, 5 cm tall. …(½m)
- (Move the candle nearer than 18 cm and the image becomes virtual, behind the mirror.)

### Q2. Convex mirror: needle (3m)

**Type:** Numerical · **Section C**

A 4.5 cm needle is placed 12 cm in front of a convex mirror of focal length 15 cm. Find the image distance, magnification and image size.

**Solution:**
- f = +15 cm (convex), u = −12 cm. …(½m)
- 1/v = 1/f − 1/u = 1/15 − (−1/12) = 1/15 + 1/12 = (4 + 5)/60 = 9/60. …(1m)
- **v = 60/9 = +6.7 cm** (behind the mirror → virtual, erect). …(½m)
- m = −v/u = −(6.7)/(−12) = +0.56 ⇒ image height = 0.56 × 4.5 = **2.5 cm**. …(1m)
- **Result:** virtual, erect, diminished image 6.7 cm behind the mirror, 2.5 cm tall.

### Q3. Apparent depth of a tank (2m)

**Type:** Numerical · **Section B**

A tank is filled with water (n = 1.33) to a depth of 12.5 cm. A microscope focused on a needle at the bottom must be raised by some amount to focus on its image. Find the apparent depth and the shift.

**Solution:**
- Apparent depth = real depth / n = 12.5 / 1.33 = **9.4 cm**. …(1m)
- Apparent shift = 12.5 − 9.4 = **3.1 cm** (microscope raised by 3.1 cm; independent of the actual depth only as a fraction (1 − 1/n)·depth). …(1m)

### Q4. Critical angle from speed of light (2m)

**Type:** Numerical · **Section B**

The refractive index of a medium is 1.5. Find (i) the speed of light in it and (ii) its critical angle with air.

**Solution:**
- (i) v = c/n = (3 × 10⁸)/1.5 = **2 × 10⁸ m/s**. …(1m)
- (ii) sin C = 1/n = 1/1.5 = 0.667 ⇒ **C = 41.8° ≈ 42°**. …(1m)

### Q5. Lens-maker's formula: find R (3m)

**Type:** Numerical · **Section C**

A double-convex lens is made of glass of refractive index 1.55, with both surfaces of equal radius. If its focal length is 20 cm, find the radius of curvature.

**Solution:**
- For equal radii: R₁ = +R, R₂ = −R, so (1/R₁ − 1/R₂) = 2/R. …(½m)
- Lens-maker: 1/f = (n − 1)(2/R) ⇒ R = 2(n − 1)f. …(1m)
- R = 2 × (1.55 − 1) × 20 = 2 × 0.55 × 20 = **22 cm**. …(1½m)

### Q6. Lenses in contact (2m)

**Type:** Numerical · **Section B**

A convex lens of focal length 30 cm is placed in contact with a concave lens of focal length 20 cm. Find the focal length and power of the combination, and state its nature.

**Solution:**
- 1/F = 1/f₁ + 1/f₂ = 1/30 + 1/(−20) = (2 − 3)/60 = −1/60. …(1m)
- **F = −60 cm = −0.6 m**, so P = 1/F = **−1.67 D**. The combination is **diverging**. …(1m)

---

### Q7. Power of lens combination (2m)

**Type:** Numerical · **Section B**

Two thin lenses of powers +4 D and −2 D are placed in contact. Find the focal length of the combination.

**Solution:**
- P = P₁ + P₂ = 4 + (−2) = +2 D. …(1m)
- f = 1/P = 1/2 = **0.5 m = 50 cm** (converging). …(1m)

### Q8. Prism: refractive index from minimum deviation (3m)

**Type:** Numerical · **Section C**

A ray passes through an equilateral glass prism (A = 60°) at minimum deviation Dm = 30°. Find the refractive index of the prism.

**Solution:**
- n = sin[(A + Dm)/2] / sin(A/2). …(1m)
- (A + Dm)/2 = (60 + 30)/2 = 45°; A/2 = 30°. …(½m)
- n = sin 45° / sin 30° = (0.707)/(0.5) = 1.414 = **√2 ≈ 1.41**. …(1½m)

---

### Q9. Prism: small-angle deviation (2m)

**Type:** Numerical · **Section B**

A thin prism of refracting angle 4° is made of glass of refractive index 1.5. Find the deviation produced.

**Solution:**
- For a thin prism, δ = (n − 1)A. …(1m)
- δ = (1.5 − 1) × 4° = 0.5 × 4° = **2°**. …(1m)

### Q10. Compound microscope magnifying power (3m)

**Type:** Numerical · **Section C**

A compound microscope has an objective of focal length 2.0 cm and an eyepiece of focal length 6.25 cm separated by 15 cm. If the final image is formed at the near point (D = 25 cm), find the magnifying power. (Standard NCERT data.)

**Solution:**
- Eyepiece, final image at D: image distance for eyepiece v_e = −25 cm; 1/v_e − 1/u_e = 1/f_e
  ⇒ 1/u_e = 1/(−25) − 1/6.25 = −0.04 − 0.16 = −0.20 ⇒ u_e = −5 cm. …(1m)
- Objective image distance v_o = tube separation + u_e = 15 − 5 = 10 cm (object real at u_o):
  1/v_o − 1/u_o = 1/f_o ⇒ 1/10 − 1/u_o = 1/2 ⇒ 1/u_o = 0.1 − 0.5 = −0.4 ⇒ u_o = −2.5 cm. …(½m)
- m_o = v_o/u_o = 10/(−2.5) = −4 (magnitude 4); m_e = 1 + D/f_e = 1 + 25/6.25 = 5. …(1m)
- **M = m_o × m_e = 4 × 5 = 20** (≈ 20× at near point). …(½m)

### Q11. Astronomical telescope: magnifying power and length (3m)

**Type:** Numerical · **Section C**

An astronomical telescope has an objective of focal length 144 cm and an eyepiece of focal length 6.0 cm. In normal adjustment find (i) the magnifying power and (ii) the separation between the lenses (tube length).

**Solution:**
- (i) M = f_o/f_e = 144/6 = **24**. …(1m)
- (ii) Length L = f_o + f_e = 144 + 6 = **150 cm**. …(1m)
- (Bonus: large M and large tube length are characteristic of refracting telescopes.) …(1m)

### Q12. Telescope: magnifying power 30 (2m)

**Type:** Numerical · **Section B**

A telescope objective has focal length 150 cm and the eyepiece 5 cm. Find its magnifying power in normal adjustment and the tube length.

**Solution:**
- M = f_o/f_e = 150/5 = **30**. …(1m)
- L = f_o + f_e = 150 + 5 = **155 cm**. …(1m)

### Q13. Convex lens: real magnified image (3m)

**Type:** Numerical · **Section C**

A convex lens forms a real image twice the size of the object on a screen. If the distance between object and screen is 90 cm, find the focal length of the lens.

**Solution:**
- Real inverted image, magnification m = −2 ⇒ v = −2u (with v positive, u negative): v = 2|u|. …(½m)
- Object-to-screen distance |u| + v = 90 ⇒ |u| + 2|u| = 90 ⇒ |u| = 30 cm, v = 60 cm. …(1m)
- So u = −30 cm, v = +60 cm; lens formula 1/v − 1/u = 1/f:
  1/f = 1/60 − 1/(−30) = 1/60 + 1/30 = (1 + 2)/60 = 3/60. …(1m)
- **f = 20 cm.** …(½m)

### Q14. Speed of light and wavelength in a medium (2m)

**Type:** Numerical · **Section B**

Light of wavelength 600 nm in vacuum enters glass of refractive index 1.5. Find its speed and wavelength in the glass.

**Solution:**
- v = c/n = (3 × 10⁸)/1.5 = **2 × 10⁸ m/s**. …(1m)
- λ_glass = λ_vacuum/n = 600/1.5 = **400 nm** (frequency unchanged). …(1m)

### Q15. Refraction at a spherical surface (3m)

**Type:** Numerical · **Section C**

A point object in air is placed 100 cm from the convex spherical surface (R = 20 cm) of a glass medium (n = 1.5). Find the position of the image. (Object in air, n₁ = 1, n₂ = 1.5.)

**Solution:**
- Formula: n₂/v − n₁/u = (n₂ − n₁)/R, with u = −100 cm, R = +20 cm. …(1m)
- 1.5/v − 1/(−100) = (1.5 − 1)/20 = 0.5/20 = 0.025. …(½m)
- 1.5/v = 0.025 − 0.01 = 0.015 ⇒ v = 1.5/0.015 = **100 cm** inside the glass (real image). …(1½m)

### Q16. Magnifying glass (simple microscope) (2m)

**Type:** Numerical · **Section B**

A converging lens of focal length 5 cm is used as a magnifying glass with the image at the near point (D = 25 cm). Find its magnifying power.

**Solution:**
- M = 1 + D/f = 1 + 25/5. …(1m)
- M = 1 + 5 = **6** (i.e. 6×). …(1m)
