### Q1. Sign convention for a concave mirror (1m)

**Type:** MCQ · **Section A**

For an object placed in front of a concave mirror (real object), which quantities are taken **negative** by the New Cartesian sign convention?

- (a) Only the object distance u
- (b) Only the focal length f
- (c) Object distance u, focal length f, and (for a real image) image distance v
- (d) None of these

**Answer:** (c).
Distances measured against the incident light (to the left of the pole) are negative. For a real object and real image of a concave mirror, u, v and f all lie to the left, so all three are negative.

### Q2. What stays constant during refraction (1m)

**Type:** MCQ · **Section A**

When light travels from air into glass, which property of the light does **not** change?

- (a) Speed
- (b) Wavelength
- (c) Frequency
- (d) Direction

**Answer:** (c) Frequency.
Frequency is fixed by the source. On entering a denser medium speed decreases and wavelength shortens (λ = v/f), and the ray bends toward the normal.

### Q3. Critical angle and refractive index (1m)

**Type:** MCQ · **Section A**

The critical angle for a medium-air interface is C. The refractive index of the medium is:

- (a) sin C
- (b) cos C
- (c) 1/sin C
- (d) tan C

**Answer:** (c) 1/sin C.
At the critical angle, sin C = 1/n (with the rarer medium being air), so n = 1/sin C.

### Q4. Power of a lens (1m)

**Type:** MCQ · **Section A**

The power of a lens of focal length −25 cm is:

- (a) +4 D
- (b) −4 D
- (c) +0.25 D
- (d) −0.25 D

**Answer:** (b) −4 D.
P = 1/f(in metres) = 1/(−0.25 m) = −4 D. Negative power → diverging (concave) lens.

---

### Q5. Lens combination power (1m)

**Type:** MCQ · **Section A**

Two thin lenses of powers +5 D and −2 D are placed in contact. The power of the combination is:

- (a) +7 D
- (b) +3 D
- (c) −3 D
- (d) +10 D

**Answer:** (b) +3 D.
Powers add for lenses in contact: P = P₁ + P₂ = 5 + (−2) = +3 D (combined f = 1/3 m ≈ 33.3 cm, converging).

### Q6. Angle of minimum deviation (1m)

**Type:** MCQ · **Section A**

At the angle of minimum deviation through a prism, the ray inside the prism:

- (a) is parallel to the incident ray
- (b) travels parallel to the base of the prism
- (c) emerges normal to the second face
- (d) suffers total internal reflection

**Answer:** (b) travels parallel to the base.
At minimum deviation i = e and r₁ = r₂; the refracted ray inside is symmetric and runs parallel to the base.

### Q7. Magnifying power of a telescope (1m)

**Type:** MCQ · **Section A**

For an astronomical telescope in normal adjustment, the magnifying power is:

- (a) f_e/f_o
- (b) f_o/f_e
- (c) f_o · f_e
- (d) (f_o + f_e)

**Answer:** (b) f_o/f_e.
A large objective focal length and a short eyepiece focal length give high magnification; M = f_o/f_e in normal adjustment.

---

### Q8. Image by a convex mirror (1m)

**Type:** MCQ · **Section A**

The image formed by a convex mirror of a real object is always:

- (a) real, inverted, magnified
- (b) virtual, erect, diminished
- (c) real, erect, diminished
- (d) virtual, inverted, magnified

**Answer:** (b) virtual, erect, diminished.
A convex mirror always forms a virtual, erect, diminished image between the pole and focus — hence its use as a rear-view mirror (wide field of view).

### Q9. Apparent depth (1m)

**Type:** MCQ · **Section A**

A coin lies at the bottom of a tank of water (n = 4/3) of real depth 8 cm. Its apparent depth, viewed normally, is:

- (a) 6 cm
- (b) 8 cm
- (c) 10.6 cm
- (d) 4 cm

**Answer:** (a) 6 cm.
Apparent depth = real depth / n = 8 / (4/3) = 6 cm.

### Q10. Refractive index and speed of light (1m)

**Type:** MCQ · **Section A**

The speed of light in a medium of refractive index 1.5 is:

- (a) 3 × 10⁸ m/s
- (b) 2 × 10⁸ m/s
- (c) 4.5 × 10⁸ m/s
- (d) 1.5 × 10⁸ m/s

**Answer:** (b) 2 × 10⁸ m/s.
v = c/n = (3 × 10⁸)/1.5 = 2 × 10⁸ m/s.

### Q11. Assertion-Reason: lens vanishing in a liquid (1m)

**Type:** Assertion-Reason · **Section A**

- **Assertion (A):** A glass lens immersed in a liquid of the same refractive index becomes invisible and stops converging or diverging light.
- **Reason (R):** When the surrounding medium has the same refractive index as the lens, the lens's focal length becomes infinite and its power becomes zero.

Options: (a) Both A and R true, R correct explanation of A. (b) Both true, R not the correct explanation. (c) A true, R false. (d) A false, R true.

**Answer:** (a).
By the lens-maker's formula 1/f = (n_lens/n_medium − 1)(1/R₁ − 1/R₂). When n_lens = n_medium the bracket (n_lens/n_medium − 1) = 0, so 1/f = 0, f → ∞ and P = 0. No refraction occurs at the surfaces (no change in n), so the lens is optically invisible. R correctly explains A.

### Q12. Assertion-Reason: total internal reflection (1m)

**Type:** Assertion-Reason · **Section A**

- **Assertion (A):** Total internal reflection can occur only when light travels from a denser medium to a rarer medium.
- **Reason (R):** For TIR the angle of incidence in the denser medium must exceed the critical angle.

Options as in Q11.

**Answer:** (a).
TIR needs the ray going denser → rarer (so the refracted ray bends away from the normal and a critical angle exists), AND the incidence angle > critical angle. Both true; R is the precise condition, so it correctly explains A.

### Q13. Assertion-Reason: focal length and colour (1m)

**Type:** Assertion-Reason · **Section A**

- **Assertion (A):** The focal length of a glass convex lens is shorter for violet light than for red light.
- **Reason (R):** The refractive index of glass is greater for violet light than for red light.

Options as in Q11.

**Answer:** (a).
n_violet > n_red (dispersion). By the lens-maker's formula, larger n gives smaller f, so f_violet < f_red. R correctly explains A. (This is the origin of chromatic aberration.)

---

### Q14. Assertion-Reason: convex mirror as driver's mirror (1m)

**Type:** Assertion-Reason · **Section A**

- **Assertion (A):** A convex mirror is preferred as a rear-view mirror in vehicles.
- **Reason (R):** A convex mirror always produces an erect, virtual, diminished image and gives a wider field of view than a plane mirror.

Options as in Q11.

**Answer:** (a).
Both statements are true and R is exactly why convex mirrors are chosen: the diminished image lets the driver see a wide field of traffic behind. Correct explanation.

---

### Q15. Assertion-Reason: magnifying power depends on f (1m)

**Type:** Assertion-Reason · **Section A**

- **Assertion (A):** To get high magnifying power in an astronomical telescope, the objective should have a large focal length and the eyepiece a small focal length.
- **Reason (R):** The magnifying power of a telescope in normal adjustment is M = f_e/f_o.

Options as in Q11.

**Answer:** (c) A true, R false.
A is correct (large f_o, small f_e → high M). But the formula is M = f_o/f_e, not f_e/f_o, so R is false.
