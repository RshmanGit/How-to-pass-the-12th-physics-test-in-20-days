## Chapter

- **Number / name:** Chapter 9 — Ray Optics and Optical Instruments

## Important / repeated questions (high board frequency)

- **Mirror formula derivation** 1/f = 1/v + 1/u (derivations Q1) — perennial 5m.
- **Refraction at a spherical surface** n₂/v − n₁/u = (n₂−n₁)/R (derivations Q2) — prerequisite for
  lens-maker; appears as a 5m derivation or a numerical (numericals Q15).
- **Lens-maker's formula** (derivations Q3) — highest-frequency 5m derivation in the chapter.
- **Prism: n = sin[(A+Dm)/2]/sin(A/2)** (derivations Q4) — recurring 5m; paired with the
  equilateral-prism numericals (numericals Q8, case Q3).
- **Compound microscope ray diagram + magnifying power** (derivations Q5) — alternates with telescope.
- **Astronomical telescope ray diagram + M = f_o/f_e + L = f_o+f_e** (derivations Q6) — recurring 5m.
- **Total internal reflection: conditions, critical angle, optical fibre** (short Q2/Q3, derivations
  Q7, case Q1) — very high frequency across MCQ, SA and case-study.
- **Lens in a liquid / lens vanishing when n_lens = n_medium** (MCQ Q11, short Q6) — classic 1-2m.
- **Lenses in contact / power addition** (short Q7, derivations Q8, numericals Q6, Q7) — almost every paper.

## Syllabus / scope notes (2025-26)

- All topics here are in the current rationalised CBSE Class 12 syllabus: reflection at spherical
  mirrors (mirror formula, magnification), refraction & Snell's law, TIR & optical fibre, refraction
  at spherical surfaces, lens-maker's & lens formula, power, lenses in contact, refraction through a
  prism (minimum deviation), compound microscope, astronomical telescope.
- **Deliberately excluded** (trimmed/rationalised or belong elsewhere): detailed treatment of
  scattering of light / "why the sky is blue" and rainbow formation (dropped in the rationalised
  text); reflecting (Cassegrain) telescope construction is kept light. Resolving power is mentioned
  conceptually only (short Q9/Q10) since the quantitative formula sits with wave optics.
- Some source/PYQ pages still list scattering and rainbow — those were left out to keep scope to the
  current syllabus.
