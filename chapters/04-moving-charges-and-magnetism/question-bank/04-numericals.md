# Chapter 4 — Moving Charges and Magnetism — Numericals (worked)

> Worked numericals in marking-scheme style: formula → substitution → answer with units. Constant used: `μ₀ = 4π × 10⁻⁷ T·m/A`, electron `e = 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ C`, `mₑ = 9.1 × 10⁻³¹ kg`, proton `mₚ = 1.67 × 10⁻²⁷ kg`.

---

### Q1. Field of a long straight wire (2m)

A long straight wire carries a current of 35 A. Find the magnetic field at a point 20 cm from it.

**Answer:**
- `B = μ₀ I/(2πr)` (…½m).
- `B = (4π × 10⁻⁷ × 35)/(2π × 0.20) = (2 × 10⁻⁷ × 35)/0.20` (…1m).
- **`B = 3.5 × 10⁻⁵ T`** (= 35 µT) (…½m).

---

### Q2. Field at the centre of a circular coil (2m)

A circular coil of 100 turns and radius 8.0 cm carries a current of 0.40 A. Find the field at its centre.

**Answer:**
- `B = μ₀ N I/(2R)` (…½m).
- `B = (4π × 10⁻⁷ × 100 × 0.40)/(2 × 0.08)` (…1m).
- `B = (5.03 × 10⁻⁵)/(0.16) = 3.14 × 10⁻⁴ T`.
- **`B ≈ 3.1 × 10⁻⁴ T`** along the axis (…½m).

---

### Q3. Field on the axis of a coil (3m)
<!-- id: ch04-num-q3 -->

A circular coil of radius 10 cm, 200 turns, carries a current of 5.0 A. Find the field (i) at the centre and (ii) at a point 12 cm from the centre on the axis. *(NCERT-style)*

**Answer:**
- (i) Centre: `B = μ₀NI/(2R) = (4π×10⁻⁷ × 200 × 5)/(2 × 0.10)` (…1m)
  `= (1.257×10⁻³)/(0.20) = 6.28 × 10⁻³ T ≈ 6.3 × 10⁻³ T` (…½m).
- (ii) Axis: `B = μ₀NIR²/[2(R²+x²)^{3/2}]`, `R = 0.10`, `x = 0.12`, `R²+x² = 0.0244`, `(R²+x²)^{3/2} = 0.00381` (…1m)
  `B = (4π×10⁻⁷ × 200 × 5 × 0.01)/(2 × 0.00381) = (1.257×10⁻⁵)/(0.00762)`
  **`B ≈ 1.65 × 10⁻³ T`** along the axis (…½m).

### Q4. Force on a moving charge (2m)

An electron moves at `4 × 10⁶ m/s` perpendicular to a field of 0.25 T. Find the force on it.

**Answer:**
- `F = qvB sinθ`, `θ = 90°` (…½m).
- `F = (1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹)(4 × 10⁶)(0.25)` (…1m).
- **`F = 1.6 × 10⁻¹³ N`** (…½m).

---

### Q5. Radius of circular path (3m)

A proton is accelerated through 1.0 kV and then enters a magnetic field of 0.20 T perpendicular to its velocity. Find the radius of its circular path. `(mₚ = 1.67 × 10⁻²⁷ kg)`

**Answer:**
- Speed: `qV = ½mₚv² ⇒ v = √(2qV/mₚ)` (…1m).
- `v = √[(2 × 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ × 1000)/(1.67 × 10⁻²⁷)] = √(1.916 × 10¹¹) = 4.38 × 10⁵ m/s` (…1m).
- `r = mₚv/(qB) = (1.67 × 10⁻²⁷ × 4.38 × 10⁵)/(1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ × 0.20)`
- **`r ≈ 2.3 × 10⁻² m`** (= 2.3 cm) (…1m).

---

### Q6. Pitch of a helical path (3m)

A proton enters a uniform field of 0.10 T with speed `3.0 × 10⁵ m/s`, making 30° with the field. Find the radius and pitch of its helical path.

**Answer:**
- `v⊥ = v sin30° = 1.5 × 10⁵ m/s`, `v∥ = v cos30° = 2.6 × 10⁵ m/s` (…1m).
- Radius `r = mₚv⊥/(qB) = (1.67 × 10⁻²⁷ × 1.5 × 10⁵)/(1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ × 0.10)`
  **`r ≈ 1.57 × 10⁻² m`** (≈ 1.6 cm) (…1m).
- Pitch `p = v∥ · T = v∥ · (2πmₚ/qB) = (2.6 × 10⁵)(2π × 1.67×10⁻²⁷/(1.6×10⁻¹⁹ × 0.10))`
  `T = 6.56 × 10⁻⁷ s ⇒` **`p ≈ 0.17 m`** (≈ 17 cm) (…1m).

### Q7. Force between two parallel wires (3m)

Two long parallel wires 4.0 cm apart carry currents of 10 A and 15 A in the same direction. Find the force per unit length and state its nature.

**Answer:**
- `F/L = μ₀ I₁ I₂/(2πd)` (…1m).
- `F/L = (4π × 10⁻⁷ × 10 × 15)/(2π × 0.04) = (2 × 10⁻⁷ × 150)/0.04` (…1m).
- **`F/L = 7.5 × 10⁻⁴ N/m`**, attractive (currents in the same direction) (…1m).

---

### Q8. Current-balanced suspended wire (3m)
<!-- id: ch04-num-q8 -->

A horizontal wire carrying current `I` is held in mid-air by the magnetic force from a parallel wire carrying 30 A located 2.0 mm below it. The upper wire has mass per unit length `5.0 × 10⁻⁴ kg/m`. Find `I`. *(repeated PYQ type)*

**Answer:**
- For equilibrium, magnetic force per length = weight per length: `μ₀ I₁ I₂/(2πd) = (m/L) g` (…1m).
- `(2 × 10⁻⁷ × 30 × I)/(2.0 × 10⁻³) = (5.0 × 10⁻⁴)(9.8)` (…1m).
- `(6 × 10⁻⁶ × I)/(2.0 × 10⁻³) = 4.9 × 10⁻³ ⇒ 3 × 10⁻³ I = 4.9 × 10⁻³`
- **`I ≈ 1.63 A`** (currents must be in the same direction so the force is upward/attractive) (…1m).

### Q9. Force on a current-carrying conductor (2m)

A straight wire of length 20 cm carrying 12 A lies at 30° to a uniform field of 0.30 T. Find the force on it.

**Answer:**
- `F = BIL sinθ` (…½m).
- `F = (0.30)(12)(0.20)(sin30°) = (0.30)(12)(0.20)(0.5)` (…1m).
- **`F = 0.36 N`** (…½m).

---

### Q10. Torque on a current loop (3m)

A rectangular coil of 50 turns and area `2.0 × 10⁻³ m²` carries a current of 4.0 A. It is placed in a uniform field of 0.10 T with its plane parallel to the field. Find the torque.

**Answer:**
- Plane parallel to `B` ⇒ angle between normal and `B` is 90° ⇒ `sinθ = 1` (…½m).
- `τ = NIAB sinθ = (50)(4.0)(2.0 × 10⁻³)(0.10)(1)` (…1½m).
- **`τ = 4.0 × 10⁻² N·m`** (= 0.04 N·m) (…1m).

---

### Q11. Magnetic moment and torque (3m)

A circular coil of 20 turns and radius 10 cm carries a current of 5.0 A. It is in a uniform field of 0.50 T such that its plane makes 30° with the field. Find (i) the magnetic moment and (ii) the torque. *(NCERT)*

**Answer:**
- Area `A = πr² = π(0.10)² = 3.14 × 10⁻² m²` (…½m).
- (i) `m = NIA = (20)(5.0)(3.14 × 10⁻²) =` **`3.14 A·m²`** (…1m).
- (ii) Plane makes 30° with `B` ⇒ normal makes 60° with `B`, so `θ = 60°`:
  `τ = mB sinθ = (3.14)(0.50)(sin60°) = (3.14)(0.50)(0.866)`
  **`τ ≈ 1.36 N·m`** (…1½m).

### Q12. Galvanometer → ammeter (shunt) (3m)
<!-- id: ch04-num-q12 -->

A galvanometer of resistance 15 Ω gives full-scale deflection for a current of 4.0 mA. How is it converted into an ammeter reading up to 6.0 A?

**Answer:**
- Shunt `S` in parallel; `S = I_g G/(I − I_g)` (…1m).
- `S = (4.0 × 10⁻³ × 15)/(6.0 − 4.0 × 10⁻³) = (0.06)/(5.996)` (…1m).
- **`S ≈ 1.0 × 10⁻² Ω`** (≈ 0.010 Ω), connected in parallel (…1m).

---

### Q13. Galvanometer → voltmeter (series R) (3m)

A galvanometer of resistance 12 Ω shows full-scale deflection for 3.0 mA. Convert it into a voltmeter reading up to 18 V.

**Answer:**
- Series resistance `R = V/I_g − G` (…1m).
- `R = 18/(3.0 × 10⁻³) − 12 = 6000 − 12` (…1m).
- **`R = 5988 Ω`** in series (≈ 5.99 kΩ) (…1m).

---

### Q14. Force on a rectangular loop near a long wire (3m)
<!-- id: ch04-num-q14 -->

A rectangular loop (sides 2.0 cm and 10 cm) carries 5.0 A. Its long side (10 cm) is parallel to and 1.0 cm from a long wire carrying 20 A in the same direction. Find the net force on the loop.

**Answer:**
- Forces act on the two 10 cm sides (parallel to the wire). Near side at `d₁ = 1.0 cm`, far side at `d₂ = 3.0 cm` (…½m).
- Near side (attraction): `F₁ = μ₀ I₁ I₂ L/(2πd₁) = (2×10⁻⁷ × 20 × 5 × 0.10)/0.010 = 2.0 × 10⁻⁴ N` (…1m).
- Far side (repulsion, currents anti-parallel relative to return path): `F₂ = (2×10⁻⁷ × 20 × 5 × 0.10)/0.030 = 0.67 × 10⁻⁴ N` (…1m).
- Net force (toward the wire) `= F₁ − F₂ =` **`1.33 × 10⁻⁴ N`** (attractive, toward the wire) (…½m).

### Q15. Electron orbiting in a hydrogen atom — magnetic field at the nucleus (3m)

In a hydrogen atom an electron moves in a circle of radius `0.53 × 10⁻¹⁰ m` with speed `2.2 × 10⁶ m/s`. Find the magnetic field produced at the nucleus. *(repeated)*

**Answer:**
- Orbital current `I = e/T = ev/(2πr)` (…1m).
- `I = (1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ × 2.2 × 10⁶)/(2π × 0.53 × 10⁻¹⁰) = (3.52 × 10⁻¹³)/(3.33 × 10⁻¹⁰) = 1.06 × 10⁻³ A` (…1m).
- `B = μ₀ I/(2r) = (4π × 10⁻⁷ × 1.06 × 10⁻³)/(2 × 0.53 × 10⁻¹⁰)`
  **`B ≈ 12.5 T`** (…1m).

---

### Q16. Speed in crossed E and B fields (2m)

A charged particle passes undeflected through perpendicular fields `E = 3.0 × 10⁴ V/m` and `B = 0.20 T`. Find its speed.

**Answer:**
- Undeflected ⇒ electric force balances magnetic force: `qE = qvB ⇒ v = E/B` (…1m).
- `v = (3.0 × 10⁴)/(0.20) =` **`1.5 × 10⁵ m/s`** (…1m).
