### Q1. Magnetic force on a stationary charge (1m)

A charge `q` is at rest in a uniform magnetic field `B`. The force on it is:
(a) `qvB`  (b) `qB`  (c) zero  (d) `qB/v`

**Answer:** (c) zero.
- Magnetic force `F = q(v × B)`. With `v = 0`, `F = 0`. A magnetic field acts only on a *moving* charge.

---

### Q2. Direction of magnetic force (1m)

A positive charge moves with velocity `v` along +x; field `B` is along +y. The magnetic force is along:
(a) +x  (b) +z  (c) −z  (d) +y

**Answer:** (b) +z.
- `F = q(v × B) = q(x̂ × ŷ) = q ẑ`. For a positive charge the force is along +z (right-hand rule).

---

### Q3. Work done by magnetic force (1m)

The work done by a magnetic force on a charged particle moving in a magnetic field is:
(a) always positive  (b) always negative  (c) zero  (d) depends on speed

**Answer:** (c) zero.
- `F` is always perpendicular to `v`, so `F·v = 0`. The magnetic force changes direction of motion, never the speed or kinetic energy.

---

### Q4. SI unit and ampere definition (1m)

One ampere is defined as the steady current which, when maintained in two infinitely long parallel wires 1 m apart in vacuum, produces a force per unit length of:
(a) `2 × 10⁻⁷ N/m`  (b) `10⁻⁷ N/m`  (c) `2 × 10⁷ N/m`  (d) `4π × 10⁻⁷ N/m`

**Answer:** (a) `2 × 10⁻⁷ N/m`.
- From `F/L = μ₀I₁I₂/(2πd)` with `I₁ = I₂ = 1 A`, `d = 1 m`: `F/L = (2 × 10⁻⁷)(1)(1)/1 = 2 × 10⁻⁷ N/m`.

### Q5. Field at the centre of a circular loop (1m)

The magnetic field at the centre of a circular loop of radius `R` carrying current `I` is:
(a) `μ₀I/(4πR)`  (b) `μ₀I/(2R)`  (c) `μ₀I/(2πR)`  (d) `μ₀NI/R`

**Answer:** (b) `μ₀I/(2R)`.
- On-axis formula `B = μ₀IR²/[2(R²+x²)^{3/2}]` at `x = 0` gives `B = μ₀I/(2R)`.

---

### Q6. Field of a long straight wire (1m)

The magnetic field at distance `r` from a long straight wire carrying current `I` varies as:
(a) `r`  (b) `r²`  (c) `1/r`  (d) `1/r²`

**Answer:** (c) `1/r`.
- By Ampere's law `B = μ₀I/(2πr)`, so `B ∝ 1/r`.

---

### Q7. Field inside a long solenoid (1m)

The magnetic field inside a long solenoid (`n` turns per unit length, current `I`) is:
(a) `μ₀nI`  (b) `μ₀nI/2`  (c) `μ₀I/(2πn)`  (d) `μ₀nI/L`

**Answer:** (a) `μ₀nI`.
- Ampere's law applied to a rectangular loop gives `B = μ₀nI`, independent of cross-section, uniform in the interior, and ≈ 0 just outside.

---

### Q8. Pitch of a helical path (1m)

A charge enters a uniform `B` at an angle `θ` (0 < θ < 90°) to the field. Its path is:
(a) a straight line  (b) a circle  (c) a helix  (d) a parabola

**Answer:** (c) a helix.
- `v∥ = v cosθ` is unaffected (gives uniform drift along `B`); `v⊥ = v sinθ` gives circular motion. The combination is a helix.

---

### Q9. Radius of circular motion (1m)

A charged particle of charge `q`, mass `m`, speed `v` moves perpendicular to `B`. Its radius is:
(a) `qB/(mv)`  (b) `mv/(qB)`  (c) `mvB/q`  (d) `qvB/m`

**Answer:** (b) `mv/(qB)`.
- Magnetic force provides the centripetal force: `qvB = mv²/r ⇒ r = mv/(qB)`.

---

### Q10. Cyclotron-independent: period of revolution (1m)

For a charge moving in a circle in a uniform `B`, the time period of revolution is:
(a) `2πm/(qB)`  (b) `2πqB/m`  (c) `2πmv/(qB)`  (d) `mv/(qB)`

**Answer:** (a) `2πm/(qB)`.
- `T = 2πr/v = 2π(mv/qB)/v = 2πm/(qB)`; it is independent of `v` and `r`.

---

### Q11. Torque on a current loop (1m)

A rectangular coil of `N` turns, area `A`, current `I`, in field `B` has its plane parallel to `B`. The torque is:
(a) zero  (b) `NIAB`  (c) `NIAB/2`  (d) `NIA/B`

**Answer:** (b) `NIAB` (maximum).
- `τ = NIAB sinθ`, where `θ` is the angle between `B` and the *normal* to the coil. Plane parallel to `B` ⇒ normal perpendicular to `B` ⇒ `θ = 90°` ⇒ `τ = NIAB`.

---

### Q12. Galvanometer to ammeter (1m)

A galvanometer is converted into an ammeter by connecting:
(a) a high resistance in series  (b) a low resistance (shunt) in parallel
(c) a high resistance in parallel  (d) a low resistance in series

**Answer:** (b) a low resistance (shunt) in parallel.
- The shunt bypasses most of the current and keeps the ammeter's net resistance low.

---

### Q13. Galvanometer to voltmeter (1m)

A galvanometer is converted into a voltmeter by connecting:
(a) a low resistance in parallel  (b) a high resistance in series
(c) a high resistance in parallel  (d) a low resistance in series

**Answer:** (b) a high resistance in series.
- The series resistance `R = V/I_g − G` limits current and makes the voltmeter's resistance high so it draws negligible current.

---

### Q14. Current sensitivity of a galvanometer (1m)

The current sensitivity of a moving-coil galvanometer is:
(a) `NAB/k`  (b) `k/(NAB)`  (c) `NAB`  (d) `NABI`

**Answer:** (a) `NAB/k`.
- Deflection per unit current `φ/I = NAB/k`. (Increasing turns, area, field or decreasing torsion constant raises it.)

---

### Q15. Why a radial field in a galvanometer (1m)

A radial magnetic field is used in a moving-coil galvanometer so that:
(a) `B` is large  (b) the scale is non-linear
(c) the deflection is proportional to current  (d) the coil does not rotate

**Answer:** (c) the deflection is proportional to current.
- With a radial field the plane of the coil is always parallel to `B`, so `sinθ = 1` always, giving `φ ∝ I` (a linear scale).

## Assertion–Reason

> Choose: (a) Both A and R true, R is the correct explanation of A. (b) Both true, R is NOT the correct explanation. (c) A true, R false. (d) A false, R true.

---

### Q16. A–R: magnetic force and kinetic energy (1m)

**Assertion (A):** A magnetic field cannot change the kinetic energy of a moving charged particle.
**Reason (R):** The magnetic force is always perpendicular to the velocity of the particle.

**Answer:** (a).
- A is true (no work done ⇒ KE constant). R is true and correctly explains A: `F ⊥ v ⇒ F·v = 0 ⇒` zero work ⇒ constant KE.

---

### Q17. A–R: field along the wire (1m)

**Assertion (A):** A charged particle moving parallel to a uniform magnetic field experiences no force.
**Reason (R):** The magnetic force is `F = qvB sinθ` and `θ = 0` for parallel motion.

**Answer:** (a).
- A true; R true with `sin0° = 0 ⇒ F = 0`, correctly explaining A.

---

### Q18. A–R: parallel currents attract (1m)

**Assertion (A):** Two parallel wires carrying currents in the same direction attract each other.
**Reason (R):** Each wire lies in the magnetic field of the other and experiences a force `F = BIL`.

**Answer:** (a).
- A true (like currents attract, unlike currents repel). R true and is the correct mechanism: the field of one wire exerts `BIL` on the other; the geometry makes it attractive for same-direction currents.

---

### Q19. A–R: solenoid field (1m)

**Assertion (A):** The magnetic field inside a long current-carrying solenoid is uniform.
**Reason (R):** The field inside depends on the radius of the solenoid.

**Answer:** (c).
- A true (field `μ₀nI` is uniform over the interior). R false — `B = μ₀nI` is independent of the radius/cross-section.

---

### Q20. A–R: galvanometer shunt (1m)

**Assertion (A):** An ideal ammeter should have zero resistance.
**Reason (R):** An ammeter is connected in series and must not alter the current it measures.

**Answer:** (a).
- A true (ideal ammeter resistance → 0). R true and correctly explains A: in series, any resistance would reduce the circuit current, so it must be as low as possible.
