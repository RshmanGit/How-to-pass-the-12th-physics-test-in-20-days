## 1. Acute ischaemic stroke

> **Q (15m).** Discuss the aetiopathogenesis, clinical features, investigations and management of acute
> ischaemic stroke (cerebrovascular accident).

**Answer to memorise** — write it under these headed blocks; each block scores on its own.

**Definition *(1m)*** — Rapidly developing **focal (or global) neurological deficit lasting >24 h** of
presumed **vascular** origin. Ischaemic (~80%) = infarction from arterial occlusion. Deficit fully resolving
<24 h with no infarct = **TIA**.

**Aetiopathogenesis *(3m)*** — **Large-artery atherosclerosis** (carotid/vertebral → thrombosis or
artery-to-artery embolism) · **cardioembolism** (AF commonest, recent MI, prosthetic valves, IE) ·
**small-vessel/lacunar** (lipohyalinosis from chronic HTN/diabetes) · less common: dissection, vasculitis,
prothrombotic states. **Risk factors:** HTN (most important modifiable), diabetes, smoking, dyslipidaemia,
AF, age. **Mechanism:** occlusion → irreversible **core** + salvageable **penumbra** → rationale for
time-critical reperfusion.

**Clinical features *(3m)*** — Sudden focal deficit by territory: **MCA** (contralateral hemiparesis face+arm
> leg, aphasia/neglect, gaze deviation toward lesion) · **ACA** (leg > arm) · **PCA** (homonymous
hemianopia) · **posterior/brainstem** (vertigo, diplopia, ataxia, crossed signs, dysarthria). Quantify with
**NIHSS**; note swallow, GCS, **time of onset (last-seen-well)**.

**Investigations *(3m)*** — **Non-contrast CT head FIRST** (exclude haemorrhage before thrombolysis) ·
**MRI-DWI** most sensitive early; CT/MR angiography for large-vessel occlusion; CT perfusion for extended
window · **cause work-up:** ECG ± Holter, echo, carotid Doppler, **blood glucose (mandatory — mimics
stroke)**, lipids, FBC, coagulation.

**Management *(5m)*** —
- **Reperfusion (the mark-magnet):** IV **alteplase 0.9 mg/kg (max 90 mg)** within **4.5 h** after CT
  excludes bleed (**tenecteplase 0.25 mg/kg** bolus now preferred in many guidelines — confirm yours) ·
  **mechanical thrombectomy** for anterior large-vessel occlusion ≤6 h (selected → 24 h, DAWN/DEFUSE-3) ·
  if not lysed, **aspirin 300 mg** within 24 h (delay to >24 h post-lysis if thrombolysed).
- **Stroke-unit/supportive:** airway, normoglycaemia, normothermia, **permissive hypertension**, swallow
  assessment, VTE prophylaxis, early rehab.
- **Secondary prevention:** antiplatelet (→ clopidogrel) **or anticoagulation if AF** · high-intensity
  statin · BP/diabetes control · smoking cessation · **carotid endarterectomy** for symptomatic 70–99% stenosis.

**✏️ What to draw / tabulate (independent marks)**
- [ ] Table — **arterial territory ⇒ deficit** (ACA / MCA / PCA / brainstem)
- [ ] Flowchart — **hyperacute pathway** (onset → CT → thrombolysis ±4.5 h / thrombectomy → stroke unit)

**📚 Learn it:** Harrison's — *Cerebrovascular Diseases* · Davidson's — *Stroke disease* · API — *Stroke*.

## 2. Multiple sclerosis

> **Q (5m).** Write a short note on multiple sclerosis (MS).

**Definition *(1m)*** — Chronic **immune-mediated demyelinating** CNS disease, lesions **disseminated in
space and time**; commonest in young adult women.
**Clinical features *(1½m)*** — Relapsing-remitting (commonest); optic neuritis, internuclear
ophthalmoplegia, sensory/motor + cerebellar signs, bladder dysfunction; **Uhthoff's** (heat) & **Lhermitte's**.
**Investigations *(1½m)*** — **MRI brain+spine** (periventricular/juxtacortical/infratentorial/cord plaques);
**CSF oligoclonal bands**; VEP. Diagnosed by **McDonald criteria** (below).
**Management *(1m)*** — Acute relapse: **IV methylprednisolone**; **DMTs** (interferon-β, glatiramer, dimethyl
fumarate, natalizumab, ocrelizumab); symptomatic care + rehab.

**✏️ Tabulate:** [ ] Course types (RRMS / SPMS / PPMS)
**📚 Learn it:** Harrison's — *Multiple Sclerosis* · Davidson's — *Demyelinating disease*.

## 3. McDonald criteria (MS) — a cheap-marks scoring system

> **Q (5m).** Outline the McDonald criteria for diagnosing multiple sclerosis.

**Principle *(2m)*** — Diagnose MS by showing **dissemination in space (DIS)** + **dissemination in time
(DIT)**, after excluding better explanations; combines clinical attacks with MRI (± CSF) so the diagnosis can
be made early.
**DIS *(1m)*** — ≥1 T2 lesion in ≥2 typical regions: **periventricular, (juxta)cortical, infratentorial,
spinal cord** (the 2017 four regions).
**DIT *(1m)*** — a new T2/enhancing lesion on follow-up, **or** simultaneous enhancing + non-enhancing
lesions, **or** a second clinical attack.
**CSF substitution *(1m)*** — in a typical CIS with DIS, **CSF-specific oligoclonal bands** substitute for DIT.

> ⚠ **Edition note:** this is the **2017** revision (widely examined in India). The **2024** revision adds the
> **optic nerve as a 5th region** — confirm which your examiners use.

**✏️ Tabulate:** [ ] The 4 DIS regions + the 3 ways to show DIT — *the table is the answer*.

## 4. Glasgow Coma Scale — pure recall, dense marks

> **Q (5m).** Describe the Glasgow Coma Scale (GCS) and its use.

**Purpose *(1m)*** — Reproducible bedside scale of impaired consciousness; **3 (worst) – 15 (best)**.

**Three components *(3m)* — reproduce this table:**

| Response | Range | Best → worst |
|---|---|---|
| **Eye (E)** | 1–4 | spontaneous (4) · to speech (3) · to pain (2) · none (1) |
| **Verbal (V)** | 1–5 | oriented (5) · confused (4) · inappropriate words (3) · sounds (2) · none (1) |
| **Motor (M)** | 1–6 | obeys (6) · localises (5) · withdraws (4) · abnormal flexion (3) · extension (2) · none (1) |

**Use *(1m)*** — Total = E + V + M; **≤8 = coma** (protect airway); document the breakdown (e.g. **E3V4M5 =
12**) and trend it.

**📚 Learn it:** Harrison's — *Coma* · Davidson's — *Coma & altered consciousness*.
