# CBSE Class 12 Physics (042) — Weightage Blueprint

> Where the 70 theory marks live, and which chapters give the most marks for the least effort.
> Unit totals are the **official** CBSE blueprint (stable for years). Per-chapter figures are
> **estimates** synthesised from NCERT-based sources (Physics Wallah, Vedantu, Oswaal) and 5-year
> trend analysis — treat them as approximate, not guaranteed.

## High-yield, low-effort "pass" set (generalised)

For a student who just needs to cross **23/70**, sources converge on the **modern-physics + semiconductors
+ current-electricity** cluster as the easiest scoring zone.

**Tier 1 — must-do (highest confidence marks):**
- Ch 14 Semiconductors (7) · Ch 3 Current Electricity (6) · Ch 9 Ray Optics (10) ·
  Ch 12 Atoms (4) · Ch 11 Dual Nature (4)
- These five alone expose **~31 marks** of the most accessible, predictable content.

**Tier 2 — cheap-mark harvest (memorise, don't master):**
- Gauss's law / dipole field (Ch 1) · capacitor energy ½CV² (Ch 2) · diamag/para/ferro materials
  table & Curie's law (Ch 5) · **drift velocity I = neAv_d** (Ch 3) · **YDSE fringe width β = λD/d**
  (Ch 10) · transformer & resonance formulas (Ch 7) · **binding-energy / energy-released numericals**
  (Ch 13).

> ⚠️ This is the *theory* path. Remember the **separate practical gate** (33% of 30) and that **50%
> of the paper is competency-based** — so also practise *applying* these ideas to unseen case-study
> situations, not only reproducing the standard questions.
