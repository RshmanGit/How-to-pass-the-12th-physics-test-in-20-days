# CBSE Class 12 Physics (Code 042) — Question Paper Template

> What the board paper actually looks like. Verified against the **official 2025-26 Sample Question
> Paper** and CBSE blueprint. The **compartment (re-attempt) paper follows the identical pattern**,
> so everything here applies to a re-sit too.

## At a glance

| | |
|---|---|
| Subject code | 042 |
| Theory | **70 marks** |
| Practical / internal | **30 marks** (assessed separately) |
| Duration | **3 hours** |
| Total questions | **33** (all compulsory; no overall choice, internal choice in some) |
| Negative marking | **None** |

## Section-by-section structure

| Section | Q. nos | Type | Qty | Marks each | Section total | Internal choice |
|---------|--------|------|-----|------------|---------------|-----------------|
| **A** | 1–16 | 12 MCQ + 4 Assertion–Reason | 16 | 1 | **16** | None |
| **B** | 17–21 | Very Short Answer (VSA) | 5 | 2 | **10** | in 2 questions |
| **C** | 22–28 | Short Answer (SA) | 7 | 3 | **21** | in 1 question |
| **D** | 29–30 | Case-/source-based | 2 | 4 | **8** | **None** |
| **E** | 31–33 | Long Answer (LA) | 3 | 5 | **15** | in all 3 questions |
| | | | **33** | | **70** | 6 questions carry choice |

**Check:** 16 + 10 + 21 + 8 + 15 = **70** ✓

### Section A — objective (16 marks)
- **12 MCQs + 4 Assertion–Reason** items, 1 mark each (confirmed, official 2025-26 SQP instructions).
- **No negative marking** — attempt *every* one. A blank scores 0; a guess never scores less than a blank.
- Assertion–Reason format: a statement **A** and a reason **R**; choose among options like
  "both true and R explains A / both true but R does not explain A / A true R false / A false".

### Section D — case-based (8 marks) ⚠️ not fully objective
Two case studies, 4 marks each. **Confirmed against the official 2025-26 SQP + marking scheme:**
- **Case Study 1 (Q29):** **4 MCQ sub-parts (I)–(IV), 1 mark each** → fully objective (4 × 1).
- **Case Study 2 (Q30):** **descriptive**, split **2 + 1 + 1** — written/short answers, not MCQ.
- **No internal choice in Section D.** (The official instructions grant internal choice only in
  Sections B, C and E — 6 questions in total.)

> Net objective marks on the paper = **16 (Section A) + 4 (Case Study 1) = exactly 20**, *not* 24.
> (A common pass-kit overstatement — now confirmed against the official marking scheme.)
> Note: the *which-chapter* of each case study varies by paper; in the 2025-26 SQP, CS1 was a
> semiconductor-diode case and CS2 a photoelectric-effect case. The 4-marks / objective-vs-descriptive
> split is the stable blueprint; the topic is not guaranteed.

## Competency split (2025-26)

| Category | Share | ≈ marks / 70 | Where it lives |
|----------|-------|--------------|----------------|
| **Competency-based** (application, case/source, data interpretation) | **50%** | ~35 | case studies, application MCQs, applied SA |
| **MCQ / objective** | **20%** | ~14 | Section A MCQs, case-study MCQs |
| **Short/constructed answer** | **30%** | ~21 | Sections B, C, E descriptive parts |

> **This changed for 2025-26:** competency-based rose from **40% → 50%**. Half the paper now rewards
> *applying* physics, not just reproducing memorized derivations. Memorized derivations still secure
> the 20% MCQ + 30% descriptive band — which is more than enough to pass — but they no longer cover
> the whole paper.

## How marks are awarded (step marking)

- CBSE awards **step marks**: writing the correct **formula** and **substituting known values** earns
  marks even if the final arithmetic slips. Always write the formula first.
- **Diagrams carry independent marks** — a labelled V–I curve, ray diagram, circuit, or waveform
  scores on its own. Draw it whenever asked.
- Derivations are marked on the **key intermediate steps**, not prose. State the law, set up the
  equation, show the critical steps, box the result.

## Passing criteria ⚠️ two separate gates

To pass Physics you must clear **both**, independently — you **cannot** average them:

1. **Theory:** ≥ **33% of 70 = 23 marks**.
2. **Practical / internal:** ≥ **33% of 30 ≈ 10 marks**.

Failing either component = fail the subject, even if the combined total exceeds 33%.

## Compartment / supplementary specifics

- **Same blueprint, same syllabus, same 70-mark structure.** Usually held in **July**.
- If the practical was already passed in the first attempt, typically only the **theory paper** is
  re-sat (practical may carry forward). Confirm against the individual admit card / CBSE circular.
