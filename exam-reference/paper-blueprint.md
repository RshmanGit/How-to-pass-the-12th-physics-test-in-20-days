# MD General Medicine (JNU) — Theory Paper Blueprint

> Where the 400 theory marks live, and how the study **systems** map onto the four exam **papers**.
> The 4-paper / 100-marks / 3-hour skeleton is the **NMC national pattern** (stable for years) and is what
> Jaipur National University, which examines its own MD, follows. The exact paper *titles* and the
> per-paper internal split (how many LAQs vs short notes) vary by university and exam — treat the
> per-system mark figures below as **planning estimates**, not an official weightage. There is **no official
> "important topics" list** in MD Medicine; this blueprint is a high-yield planning aid, not a syllabus.

## At a glance

| | |
|---|---|
| Degree | MD General Medicine |
| University | Jaipur National University (examines its own MD; NMC pattern) |

| Per paper | **100 marks · 3 hours** · essay format |

## The four papers (NMC template — titles vary by university)

| Paper | Title (template) | What it covers |
|-------|------------------|----------------|
| **Paper I** | Basic Sciences as related to Medicine | anatomy / physiology / biochemistry / pharmacology / pathology / genetics — applied to medicine |
| **Paper II** | Medicine & Allied Specialties (A) | Neurology · Infectious & Tropical Disease · Haematology / Oncology · clinical pharmacology · poisonings |
| **Paper III** | Medicine & Allied Specialties (B) | Cardiovascular · Respiratory · GI / Hepatology · Nephrology · Endocrinology · Rheumatology · Critical care |
| **Paper IV** | Recent Advances in Medicine | newer drugs, guidelines, evidence (time-sensitive — feeds from NEJM / Lancet / JAPI) |

> The Paper II / III division of systems follows the common NMC/AIIMS template; a given university may
> shuffle which allied system sits in which paper. The pass math below does **not** depend on the exact
> shuffle — it depends only on each paper summing to 100.

## System → paper map (the planning weightage)

This is how the kit's `systems/` content maps onto the four papers. Per-paper marks sum to **100**;
all four sum to **400**. Tier-1 = Mitsu's priority systems (Neurology first); Tier-2 = high-yield
systems she initially omitted, added as a secondary layer; "floor" = the minimum coverage that keeps
Papers I and IV from being zeroed.

| System | Paper | ≈ Marks | Tier |
|--------|-------|---------|------|
| Neurology | II | **45** | 1 (her #1) |
| Infectious & Tropical Disease | II | **35** | 1 |
| Haematology / Oncology | II | **20** | 2 |
| Cardiology | III | **20** | 1 |
| Respiratory | III | **16** | 1 |
| Gastroenterology / Hepatology | III | **16** | 1 |
| Endocrinology | III | **16** | 1 |
| Nephrology | III | **14** | 1 |
| Rheumatology | III | **9** | 2 |
| Critical Care / Sepsis | III | **9** | 2 |
| Basic Sciences | I | **100** | floor |
| Recent Advances | IV | **100** | floor |
| Diagnostic Criteria & Scores | — | cross-cutting | harvest |

**Checks:** Paper I = 100 · Paper II = 45 + 35 + 20 = **100** · Paper III = 20 + 16 + 16 + 16 + 14 + 9 + 9 =
**100** · Paper IV = 100 · **Total = 400** ✓

> *Diagnostic criteria & scores* (Duke, McDonald, Modified Jones, CURB-65, Child-Pugh, MELD, Glasgow,
> Wells, CHA₂DS₂-VASc, qSOFA…) are **cross-cutting** — each score is authored inside its owning clinical
> system's `03-criteria.md` (so it rolls into that system's marks); `08-criteria-scores` only collects the
> orphan scores that don't belong to one system. They carry **0** standalone weightage but are the
> cheapest, highest-density marks on the paper.

## High-yield "pass" framing (honest)

There is no short syllabus. The defensible signal is: **(a)** a stable reference canon (Harrison's,
Davidson's, API, Kumar & Clark) and **(b)** the question styles that recur across MD theory papers —
"Discuss aetiopathogenesis, clinical features, investigations & management of X", "Classify Y, discuss
management", "Enumerate…", "Write short note on…". Drilling structured, muggable mark-frames for the
high-yield topics raises the floor; it does not guarantee the paper. Keep preparation **general** — do
not bet on one university's past questions repeating verbatim.

## The pass gate ⚠️ two constraints, both binding (no grace marks)

Theory is cleared only if **both** hold (NMC PG regulations):

1. **≥ 40% in EACH of the four papers** (per-paper floor — a single paper below 40 fails the whole head).
2. **≥ 50% aggregate** across all four (≥ 200 / 400).

A separate **≥ 50% practical / clinical + viva** gate also exists, assessed independently — **out of
scope for this theory kit**, surfaced here only so it is never forgotten.

> **Why the per-paper floor drives the plan:** you can clear the 50% aggregate and still **fail** because
> one paper (often Paper I Basic Sciences or Paper IV Recent Advances) sat below 40%. So the kit covers all
> four papers and the dashboard projects **per paper**, not just an average — see the per-paper gauges.
